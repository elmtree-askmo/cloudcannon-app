First things first: understand what your professor is asking. Is it an analytical essay, a comparative analysis, or a research paper? Clarify the requirements, word count, and due date. If anything's unclear, don't hesitate to ask for clarification.

With your outline in hand, start writing. Don't aim for perfection on the first go; just get your ideas down. Start with the body paragraphs, then tackle the introduction and conclusion. Remember to use topic sentences to guide your reader through your argument.

Once you've got a draft, take a break before revising. When you return, read your paper aloud to catch awkward phrasing or errors. Check for clarity, coherence, and consistency. Ensure your citations are correct and that you've met all the assignment requirements.
