question: >-
    How does Yellow Brick House support children who are witnesses or victims of domestic violence?
answer: >-
    Yellow Brick House provides comprehensive support for children who are witnesses or victims of domestic violence through various specialized programs and services. Recognizing the unique needs of these children, Yellow Brick House offers evidence-based and trauma-informed support tailored to their experiences.\n\nOne of the key initiatives is the "Let's Talk Child Witness Program," which is designed for children aged 4 to 15. This program helps children understand the cycle of abuse and process their feelings related to witnessing violence. It creates a safe and supportive environment where children can express themselves and learn coping strategies. Concurrently, mothers participate in a group program that educates them on how to best support their children during this challenging time.\n\nAdditionally, Yellow Brick House conducts individual counseling sessions aimed at helping children deal with the negative impacts of abuse. These services are part of a broader effort to provide a holistic approach to healing, ensuring that both children and their mothers receive the necessary support to rebuild their lives after experiencing domestic violence.\n\nOverall, Yellow Brick House's commitment to supporting children affected by domestic violence is evident in its tailored programs, which focus on healing, education, and empowerment.
