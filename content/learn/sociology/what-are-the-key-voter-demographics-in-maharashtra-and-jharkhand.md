question: >-
    What are the key voter demographics in Maharashtra and Jharkhand?
answer: >-
    In the context of the recent elections in Maharashtra and Jharkhand, key voter demographics reveal significant insights into the electorate in both states.\n\n### Maharashtra\n1. **Total Electors**: Maharashtra has over 9.70 crore electors, with approximately 22.2 lakh first-time voters aged between 18 and 19 years.\n2. **Gender Distribution**: The voter demographic shows a predominance of male voters, with 66% male and 34% female participation.\n3. **Caste Composition**: The caste distribution among voters is as follows:\n   - General caste: 72%\n   - Scheduled Castes (SC): 11%\n   - Scheduled Tribes (ST): 9%\n   - Other Backward Classes (OBC): 8%\n4. **Urban vs. Rural**: About 58% of voters are from rural areas, while 42% are from urban areas.\n5. **Voter Turnout**: The voter turnout for the 2024 assembly elections was recorded at 65.05%, marking the highest turnout in 30 years.\n\n### Jharkhand\n1. **Total Voters**: Jharkhand has approximately 12.3 million eligible voters, including 6.08 million women.\n2. **Gender Participation**: In the recent elections, women outnumbered men in voter turnout across 37 out of 43 constituencies, indicating a significant increase in female participation.\n3. **Caste Dynamics**: The political landscape in Jharkhand is heavily influenced by the Scheduled Tribe-Muslim-Yadav (ST-MSY) vote bank, which constitutes nearly half of the state's population. The JMM-Congress-RJD alliance focused on this demographic, winning a majority of the reserved Scheduled Tribe seats.\n4. **Voter Turnout**: The first phase of assembly elections in Jharkhand witnessed a voter turnout of 66.65%, reflecting an increase from previous elections.\n\nThese demographics highlight the evolving political landscape in both states, with a notable emphasis on youth and female participation, as well as the influence of caste dynamics in electoral outcomes.
