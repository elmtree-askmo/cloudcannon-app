question: >-
    What social reforms were initiated by the Self-Respect Movement, and what impact did they have?
answer: >-
    The Self-Respect Movement, initiated by E. V. Ramasamy (commonly known as Periyar) in the mid-1920s, was a significant social reform movement in South India aimed at achieving social equality and empowering marginalized communities, particularly those oppressed by the caste system. The movement sought to dismantle the existing social order dominated by Brahminical values and practices, advocating for the rights and dignity of lower castes.\n\n### Key Social Reforms Initiated by the Self-Respect Movement:\n\n1. **Self-Respect Marriages**: One of the most radical reforms was the introduction of self-respect marriages, which eliminated the need for Brahmin priests and traditional religious rituals. This reform allowed individuals to marry without the constraints of caste and religious orthodoxy, thereby promoting inter-caste marriages and empowering women to choose their partners.\n\n2. **Women's Rights**: The movement emphasized women's rights, advocating for their autonomy over personal choices, including marriage and reproduction. It promoted the idea of women having the right to divorce and remarry, as well as the use of contraception and birth control measures.\n\n3. **Property Rights for Women**: The Self-Respect Movement also campaigned for property rights for women, challenging the patriarchal norms that restricted women's access to property and inheritance.\n\n4. **Anti-Caste Advocacy**: The movement was fundamentally anti-caste, questioning the authority of Brahmin priests and the legitimacy of caste-based discrimination. It encouraged lower castes to develop self-respect and reject the oppressive structures of the caste system.\n\n5. **Cultural Reformation**: The movement sought to redefine cultural practices by promoting rationalism and scientific thinking, distancing itself from superstitions and rituals that were prevalent in traditional Hindu society.\n\n### Impact of the Self-Respect Movement:\n\nThe Self-Respect Movement had a profound impact on Indian society, particularly in Tamil Nadu. It laid the groundwork for future social justice movements and contributed to the rise of Dravidian politics, which emphasized social equality and the rights of marginalized communities. The movement's principles influenced legislative changes, such as the legalization of self-respect marriages by the Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam government in 1967.\n\nMoreover, the movement's focus on gender equality and women's rights resonated with feminist ideologies, promoting a more egalitarian society. It also inspired subsequent movements aimed at social reform and the upliftment of oppressed communities, making it a cornerstone of modern social justice initiatives in India.\n\nIn summary, the Self-Respect Movement was not only a challenge to the caste system but also a transformative force that advocated for human rights, gender equality, and social justice, leaving a lasting legacy in Indian society.
