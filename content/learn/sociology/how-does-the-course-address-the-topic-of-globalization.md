question: >-
    How does the course address the topic of globalization?
answer: >-
    The course addresses the topic of globalization by exploring it as a multifaceted process that encompasses economic, political, and cultural dimensions. It aims to help students understand how globalization shapes major social problems in today's world through a variety of interdisciplinary approaches, drawing from economics, political science, and sociology.\n\nKey aspects of the course include:\n\n1. **Analytical Capabilities**: The curriculum emphasizes the need for international business students to develop analytical skills and knowledge in various fields, including politics and sociology, to effectively address the complex interests of local and global stakeholders.\n\n2. **Discussion and Essay Questions**: Students engage with critical questions regarding the impacts of globalization, such as its role in creating a "more secure and prosperous world" and the positive and negative effects it has on daily lives globally. This encourages critical thinking and a deeper understanding of globalization's implications.\n\n3. **Interdisciplinary Readings**: The course incorporates a range of readings, including journal articles and reports, that examine globalization's relationship with economic development, migration, and global inequalities. This helps students grasp the broader context of globalization and its effects on various sectors.\n\n4. **Global Trade and Economics**: Students analyze the impact of globalization on international commerce and finance, gaining insights into how global trade dynamics influence economic development.\n\n5. **Cultural and Social Dimensions**: The course also addresses how globalization affects society and culture, including the challenges of preserving local cultural values amidst the spread of global influences.\n\n6. **Educational Implications**: The course discusses the profound effects of globalization on education systems worldwide, particularly in developing countries, and how these changes can both enrich and challenge local educational practices.\n\nOverall, the course provides a comprehensive framework for understanding globalization, equipping students with the knowledge and skills necessary to navigate its complexities in various professional contexts.
