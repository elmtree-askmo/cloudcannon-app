    What has been the international reaction to Myanmar's current political situation?
answer: >-
    The international reaction to Myanmar's current political situation, particularly following the military coup in February 2021, has been marked by increasing concern and coordinated actions aimed at addressing the escalating human rights crisis and humanitarian needs. \n\nAs of late October 2023, there has been a notable trend of coordinated actions by various member states, including the imposition of sanctions targeting key financial institutions and jet fuel supplies to weaken the military junta. This approach is seen as a potential pathway to more effectively challenge the junta's authority, which has been driving Myanmar deeper into a humanitarian crisis. Tom Andrews, the UN Special Rapporteur on human rights in Myanmar, emphasized the need for these measures to be part of a broader strategy to hold the junta accountable for its actions.\n\nThe situation has been exacerbated by significant battlefield losses for the junta and a rise in civilian opposition, leading to an increase in attacks on civilians. UN experts have called for stronger, coordinated international action, urging an end to any form of engagement with the junta that lacks conditions for accountability.
