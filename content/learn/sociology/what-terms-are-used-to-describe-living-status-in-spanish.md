question: >-
    What terms are used to describe living status in Spanish?
answer: >-
    In Spanish, the term used to describe "living status" can be translated as "calidad de vida" or "condición de vida." These phrases refer to the quality or condition of life that an individual experiences, encompassing various factors such as economic stability, health, and overall well-being.\n\nAdditionally, when discussing specific living situations or environments, you might use phrases like:\n\n- **En el campo**: in the countryside\n- **En la costa**: on the coast\n- **En una ciudad**: in a city\n- **En Londres**: in London\n- **En Inglaterra**: in England\n\nThese expressions help to specify the context of where someone lives, which can also be an important aspect of their living status. \n\nIf you need more detailed vocabulary related to living conditions or specific contexts, feel free to ask!
