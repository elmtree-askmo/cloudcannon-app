question: >-
    What support does the Red Door Family Shelter offer to women fleeing violence?
answer: >-
    The Red Door Family Shelter offers a comprehensive range of support services specifically designed for women fleeing violence. Here are the key services provided:\n\n1. **Emergency Shelter**: The shelter provides a safe haven for women and their children escaping domestic abuse. This includes immediate access to secure housing, ensuring that they have a refuge from violence.\n\n2. **Counseling and Support**: The shelter offers trauma-informed counseling and support services to help women process their experiences and begin the healing journey. This includes crisis counseling and ongoing emotional support.\n\n3. **Child Care and Youth Programs**: Recognizing the needs of children, the shelter provides child care services and youth programs that cater to the developmental and emotional needs of children affected by domestic violence.\n\n4. **Housing Assistance and Transition Support**: The Red Door Family Shelter assists women in finding stable housing after their stay, helping them transition to independent living and ensuring they have the resources needed to maintain their safety and well-being.\n\n5. **Employment and Life Skills Training**: To empower women and help them regain independence, the shelter offers training programs that focus on employment skills and life skills, preparing them for future job opportunities.\n\n6. **Legal and Immigration Support**: The shelter provides access to legal resources and support for women navigating issues related to domestic violence, including immigration concerns.\n\n7. **Refugee Services and Settlement Assistance**: For women who are refugees, the shelter offers specialized services to help them settle and integrate into the community, ensuring they receive the support they need during a vulnerable time.\n\n8. **Eligibility Criteria for Shelter Access**: Women seeking shelter must meet specific eligibility criteria, which typically include being a victim of domestic violence and having children or being pregnant.\n\nThe Red Door Family Shelter has been operational since 1982 and has become one of the largest family shelters in Toronto, accommodating hundreds of families annually. Their mission is to provide not just shelter, but a pathway to healing and rebuilding lives for women and children affected by violence. \n\nFor more information or to access services, individuals can contact the Red Door Family Shelter at 416-915-5671 or visit their location at 21 Carlaw Avenue, Toronto, ON M4M 2R6.
