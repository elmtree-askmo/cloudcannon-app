question: >-
    Are there any specific roles for local farmers in the festival?
answer: >-
    Local farmers play a significant role in the Bambanti Festival, which is fundamentally a celebration of their contributions to Isabela's agricultural heritage. The festival serves as a tribute to the diligence, resilience, and hard work of these farmers, who are essential to the province's agricultural success. \n\nThe festival, first celebrated in 1997, was specifically initiated to honor the farmers and their vital role in the agricultural industry of Isabela. It showcases the province's main products, particularly corn and rice, which are central to the local economy and culture. During the festival, local farmers are often involved in various activities, including:\n\n1. **Exhibitions and Sales**: The Bambanti Village, an eco-agricultural exhibit and sale, features booths from different municipalities in Isabela, where farmers can showcase and sell their products. This not only promotes local agriculture but also allows farmers to gain recognition for their hard work.\n\n2. **Cultural Performances**: Farmers often participate in cultural performances, reflecting their traditions and the agricultural practices that define their way of life. These performances are integral to the festival's celebrations and help to highlight the connection between the community and its agricultural roots.\n\n3. **Competitions**: The festival includes competitions such as the "Search for the Best Giant Bambanti," where local government units create life-sized scarecrow figures. Farmers may contribute to these displays, emphasizing the importance of scarecrows in protecting crops and symbolizing agricultural vigilance.\n\n4. **Community Involvement**: The festival fosters a sense of community among farmers and residents, encouraging collaboration and participation in various events, including the grand street parade and street-dance competitions.\n\nOverall, the Bambanti Festival not only celebrates the agricultural bounty of Isabela but also serves as a platform for local farmers to showcase their contributions, fostering pride and community spirit within the province.
