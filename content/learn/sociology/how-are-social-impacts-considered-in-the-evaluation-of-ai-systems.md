question: >-
    How are social impacts considered in the evaluation of AI systems?
answer: >-
    The evaluation of AI systems increasingly incorporates considerations of social impact, recognizing that these technologies can have profound effects on society. Here are some key aspects of how social impacts are considered in the evaluation of AI systems:\n\n1. **Comprehensive Evaluation Frameworks**: Researchers are developing comprehensive guides for evaluating the social impacts of generative AI systems across various modalities, including text, image, audio, and video. These frameworks aim to unify metrics that can be used to compare social aspects across different AI systems, making complex evaluations more accessible.\n\n2. **Base System Evaluation**: This involves assessing the inherent properties of the AI model itself, independent of specific applications. It focuses on understanding how the design and functionality of the AI system can influence social outcomes.\n\n3. **Contextual Evaluation**: Beyond the base system evaluation, there is a need to consider the societal context in which the AI operates. This includes examining how the AI system interacts with different social groups and the potential for bias, discrimination, or other negative social consequences.\n\n4. **Lack of Standardization**: Currently, there is no official standard for evaluating the social impacts of AI systems. This absence of consensus complicates the ability to assess and compare the social implications of different AI technologies effectively.\n\n5. **Ethical Considerations**: Evaluations must also address ethical concerns, such as the potential for rights violations. While formal social impact evaluations are necessary, they cannot justify applications of AI that infringe on individual rights or promote harmful outcomes.\n\n6. **Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI)**: The evaluation process often incorporates DEI principles, recognizing the importance of addressing issues related to bias, culture, privilege, and equity. This approach encourages evaluators to be explicit about the values and perspectives they bring to the evaluation process.\n\n7. **Societal Benefits and Policy Goals**: Evaluations may also consider the societal benefits of AI systems, such as their alignment with policy goals like the Sustainable Development Goals. This includes assessing how AI can promote responsible innovation and increase public trust in technology.\n\nIn summary, the evaluation of AI systems is evolving to include a more nuanced understanding of their social impacts, emphasizing the need for comprehensive frameworks, ethical considerations, and attention to diversity and inclusion. However, the lack of standardized evaluation methods remains a significant challenge in this field.
