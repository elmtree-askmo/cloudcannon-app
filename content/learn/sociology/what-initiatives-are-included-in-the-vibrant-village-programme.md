question: >-
    What initiatives are included in the Vibrant Village Programme?
answer: >-
    The Vibrant Villages Programme (VVP) is a significant initiative launched by the Government of India in 2023, aimed at the comprehensive development of select villages located along the northern border of the country. Here are the key initiatives and components included in the programme:\n\n1. **Target Areas**: The VVP focuses on 663 villages across 46 blocks in 19 districts, specifically in the states of Arunachal Pradesh, Himachal Pradesh, Sikkim, Uttarakhand, and the Union Territory of Ladakh.\n\n2. **Infrastructure Development**: The programme includes the construction of essential village infrastructure such as housing, roads, and tourist centers. Notably, 136 border villages are set to receive connectivity through 113 all-weather road projects, with an investment of ₹2,420 crore.\n\n3. **Digital Connectivity**: A significant aspect of the VVP is the enhancement of digital infrastructure, with plans to provide 4G connectivity to all villages under the programme by December 2024.\n\n4. **Livelihood Opportunities**: The initiative aims to create sustainable livelihood opportunities for residents of these border villages. This includes the development of agricultural, dairy, and fishery cooperatives to support local economies.\n\n5. **Financial Allocation**: The Central Government has allocated ₹4,800 crores for the implementation of the VVP over the financial years 2022-23 to 2025-26.\n\n6. **Community Engagement**: The programme encourages local participation through the creation of Vibrant Village Action Plans, which will be developed by district administrations in collaboration with local communities.\n\n7. **Objective**: The overarching goal of the VVP is to improve the quality of life for people living in these border areas, thereby encouraging them to remain in their native locations and reversing the trend of out-migration.\n\nIn summary, the Vibrant Villages Programme represents a holistic approach to rural development in India's border regions, focusing on infrastructure, connectivity, and economic opportunities to enhance the living standards of the local population.
