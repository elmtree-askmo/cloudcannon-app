question: >-
    Who is Mililani Trask and what is her contribution to Hawaiian identity and sovereignty?
answer: >-
    Mililani Trask is a prominent leader in the Hawaiian sovereignty movement, recognized for her advocacy for Native Hawaiian rights and cultural revitalization. Born in 1951, she is an attorney and political speaker who has dedicated her career to addressing issues related to the sovereignty and self-determination of Native Hawaiians.\n\nOne of her significant contributions to the movement is the founding of Na Koa Ikaika o Ka Lāhui Hawaiʻi, a non-governmental organization focused on various aspects of cultural, social, and economic development for Native Hawaiians. This organization addresses critical issues such as education, health, housing, land entitlements, energy, and water rights, thereby empowering the community and fostering cultural revitalization.\n\nTrask is also known for her role as one of the primary drafters of the Declaration of the Rights of Indigenous Peoples, which was adopted by the United Nations General Assembly in 2007. Her work at the international level highlights her commitment to advocating for indigenous rights on a global scale.\n\nIn addition to her activism, Mililani Trask has served as a guest lecturer at various institutions, including the University of Hawaii and the International Training Center for Indigenous Peoples in Greenland. She is recognized not only for her legal expertise but also for her ability to articulate the struggles and aspirations of the Hawaiian people.\n\nTrask's contributions extend beyond legal advocacy; she is a vital figure in the cultural activism surrounding Hawaiian identity, intergenerational knowledge, and community empowerment. Her work continues to inspire many within the sovereignty movement and the broader context of indigenous rights.
