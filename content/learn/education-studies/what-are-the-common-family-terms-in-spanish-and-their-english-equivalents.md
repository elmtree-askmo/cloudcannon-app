question: >-
    What are the common family terms in Spanish and their English equivalents?
answer: >-
    Here are some common family terms in Spanish along with their English equivalents:\n\n1. **El padre** - Father\n2. **La madre** - Mother\n3. **El hermano** - Brother\n4. **La hermana** - Sister\n5. **El hijo** - Son\n6. **La hija** - Daughter\n7. **El abuelo** - Grandfather\n8. **La abuela** - Grandmother\n9. **El tío** - Uncle\n10. **La tía** - Aunt\n11. **El primo** - Cousin (male)\n12. **La prima** - Cousin (female)\n13. **El suegro** - Father-in-law\n14. **La suegra** - Mother-in-law\n15. **El cuñado** - Brother-in-law\n16. **La cuñada** - Sister-in-law\n17. **El yerno** - Son-in-law\n18. **La nuera** - Daughter-in-law\n19. **El bisabuelo** - Great-grandfather\n20. **La bisabuela** - Great-grandmother\n\nThese terms reflect both immediate and extended family relationships, which are significant in Spanish-speaking cultures. Understanding these vocabulary words can enhance communication and provide insights into familial structures and social norms within these communities.
