question: >-
    What types of questions are included in Section B?
answer: >-
    In Section B of the Fiji Year 8 Basic Science Examination, there are typically four questions, which are all compulsory and collectively worth 60 marks. The types of questions included in this section often cover various scientific topics. For example, one of the questions focuses on "Living Things and the Environment," which is subdivided into parts such as 'true' or 'false' statements. \n\nThe structure of Section B may include:\n\n1. **True or False Questions**: These assess students' understanding of specific facts related to living organisms and their environments.\n2. **Matching Questions**: Students may be required to match terms or concepts with their correct definitions or descriptions.\n3. **Short Answer Questions**: These questions often require students to explain scientific principles or processes in their own words.\n4. **Research-Based Questions**: Students might be asked to conduct brief research on specific topics, such as endangered species, and answer guided questions based on their findings.\n\nOverall, Section B is designed to evaluate students' comprehension of basic scientific principles and their ability to apply this knowledge in various contexts.
