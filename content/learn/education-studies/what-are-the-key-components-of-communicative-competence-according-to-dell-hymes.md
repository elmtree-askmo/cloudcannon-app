question: >-
    What are the key components of communicative competence according to Dell Hymes?
answer: >-
    According to Dell Hymes, communicative competence is a multifaceted concept that encompasses several key components essential for effective communication in a language. Hymes proposed that communicative competence includes at least three primary components:\n\n1. **Grammatical Competence**: This refers to the knowledge of the formal systems of a language, including lexis (vocabulary), morphology-syntax (the structure of words and sentences), and phonology (the sound system of the language). It is the foundational knowledge that allows speakers to construct grammatically correct sentences.\n\n2. **Discourse Competence**: This component involves understanding how expression units (such as sentences and phrases) combine into meaningful sequences. It encompasses the ability to produce coherent and cohesive texts, whether spoken or written, and to understand how context influences the interpretation of discourse.\n\n3. **Sociolinguistic Competence**: This aspect pertains to the knowledge of how utterances are produced and understood appropriately in various social contexts. It includes understanding the social norms, cultural references, and contextual factors that affect communication, such as politeness, register, and the relationship between speakers.\n\nAdditionally, some scholars have proposed a fourth component known as **Strategic Competence**. This involves the ability to manage communication effectively, including strategies for overcoming difficulties in communication, coping with breakdowns, and enhancing the overall effectiveness of communication through various rhetorical, stylistic, and affective devices.\n\nIn summary, Hymes' model of communicative competence emphasizes that effective communication is not solely about grammatical accuracy but also involves the ability to use language appropriately in context, understand discourse structures, and employ strategies to facilitate communication.
