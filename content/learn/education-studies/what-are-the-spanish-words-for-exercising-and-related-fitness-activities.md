question: >-
    What are the Spanish words for exercising and related fitness activities?
answer: >-
    When discussing fitness and exercise in Spanish, it's essential to know the relevant vocabulary. Here are some key terms and phrases related to exercising and fitness activities:\n\n1. **Ejercicio** - This translates to "exercise" and refers to any activity requiring physical effort, done to sustain or improve health and fitness. For example, "Hago ejercicio todos los días para mantenerme en forma" means "I exercise every day to stay fit."\n\n2. **Entrenar** - This means "to train" or "to work out." It is often used in the context of preparing for a specific sport or fitness goal.\n\n3. **Hacer ejercicio** - This phrase means "to do exercise." It is commonly used to refer to engaging in physical activities.\n\n4. **Ejercitarse** - This verb means "to exercise" and can be used interchangeably with "hacer ejercicio."\n\n5. **Gimnasio** - This translates to "gym," the place where people go to work out and engage in fitness activities.\n\n6. **Correr** - This means "to run," a common form of exercise.\n\n7. **Saltar** - This translates to "to jump," which can be part of various workout routines.\n\n8. **Levantar** - This means "to lift," often used in the context of lifting weights.\n\n9. **Pesas** - This translates to "weights," referring to the equipment used for strength training.\n\n10. **Mancuerna** - This means "dumbbell," a type of weight used in workouts.\n\n11. **Pesa rusa** - This translates to "kettlebell," another form of weight used in fitness routines.\n\n12. **Comba / cuerda** - These terms mean "jump rope," a common tool for cardiovascular workouts.\n\nUnderstanding these terms can significantly enhance your ability to communicate about fitness and exercise in Spanish-speaking environments, whether you're at the gym or discussing workout routines with others.
