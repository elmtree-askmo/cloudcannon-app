question: >-
    How is the AP Language and Composition exam structured?
answer: >-
    The AP English Language and Composition exam is structured to assess students' abilities in reading critically and writing effectively in an academic context. The exam lasts a total of 3 hours and 15 minutes and is divided into two main sections: \n\n1. **Multiple-Choice Section**:\n   - **Duration**: 1 hour\n   - **Number of Questions**: 45 questions\n   - **Weight**: This section accounts for 45% of the total exam score.\n   - **Content**: The multiple-choice questions include reading comprehension tasks based on excerpts from nonfiction texts, as well as questions that require students to analyze and revise written passages.\n\n2. **Free-Response Section**:\n   - **Duration**: 2 hours and 15 minutes (which includes a 15-minute reading period)\n   - **Number of Questions**: 3 essay prompts\n   - **Weight**: This section contributes 55% to the total exam score.\n   - **Content**: Students are required to write essays that demonstrate their ability to synthesize information from multiple sources, construct arguments, and analyze rhetorical strategies.\n\nOverall, the exam is designed to evaluate the skills outlined in the AP English Language and Composition course framework, which includes topics such as rhetorical situation, claims and evidence, reasoning and organization, and argumentation. The consistent format and structure of the exam help students prepare effectively for the test day.
