question: >-
    How should findings be connected to existing literature?
answer: >-
    To effectively connect your research findings to existing literature, you should follow a structured approach that highlights both the uniqueness of your results and their relationship to previous studies. Here are some key steps to consider:\n\n1. **Summarize Your Findings**: Begin by providing a clear and concise summary of your research findings. Highlight what is unique or different about your results compared to existing literature. This sets the stage for a deeper discussion.\n\n2. **Identify Similarities and Differences**: After summarizing your findings, present two to three examples from the literature that either support or contrast with your results. This comparative analysis helps to contextualize your findings within the broader field of study. For instance, if your study reveals a new trend or outcome, discuss how this aligns or diverges from previous research.\n\n3. **Articulate Contributions**: Clearly articulate how your research fills identified gaps in the literature or contributes new knowledge. This could involve discussing emerging trends or societal needs that your findings address, thereby demonstrating the relevance and importance of your work.\n\n4. **Synthesize Insights**: A well-crafted literature review does more than summarize existing research; it synthesizes insights by making thoughtful connections between sources. This synthesis can reveal patterns, trends, and gaps in the literature, which your research may help to address.\n\n5. **Critical Evaluation**: Critically evaluate the strengths and weaknesses of the studies you reference. This not only shows your understanding of the field but also positions your research as a valuable addition to the ongoing academic conversation.\n\n6. **Integration of Findings**: If applicable, integrate qualitative and quantitative findings to provide a comprehensive view of the topic. This can enhance the robustness of your conclusions and demonstrate the multifaceted nature of the research question.\n\nBy following these steps, you can effectively connect your findings to existing literature, thereby enhancing the impact and credibility of your research.
