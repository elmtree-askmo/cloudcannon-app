question: >-
    What is the NCPCR's stance on madrasa education?
answer: >-
    The National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR) has taken a strong stance against the madrasa education system in India. In its submissions to the Supreme Court, the NCPCR criticized the madrasa system for violating children's fundamental right to education. The commission has raised several concerns regarding the madrasa education framework, including issues related to curriculum quality, teacher eligibility, opaque funding mechanisms, violations of land laws, and the failure to provide a holistic educational environment for children.\n\nRecently, the NCPCR recommended that state governments cease funding to madrasa boards, dissolve these boards, and transfer students attending madrasas to mainstream educational institutions. This recommendation is part of a broader effort to ensure that all children, including those in madrasas, receive an education that complies with the Right to Education (RTE) Act.\n\nThe NCPCR's recommendations are currently subject to the judgment of the Supreme Court regarding the constitutionality of the Uttar Pradesh Board of Madrasa Education Act, 2004, which was previously struck down by the Allahabad High Court on grounds of violating secularism and fundamental rights under Article 14 of the Indian Constitution. The Supreme Court has put a hold on the NCPCR's recommendations until a decision is made.\n\nIn summary, the NCPCR's stance emphasizes the need for reform in madrasa education to align with national educational standards and to protect the rights of children.
