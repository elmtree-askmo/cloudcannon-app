question: >-
    What skilling programs are being prioritized in the latest budget?
answer: >-
    In the Union Budget 2024-25, significant emphasis has been placed on skilling programs as part of the government's broader strategy to enhance employment opportunities and foster economic growth. Here are the key highlights regarding skilling initiatives:\n\n1. **Budget Allocation**: The Finance Minister, Nirmala Sitharaman, announced a substantial allocation of Rs. 1.48 lakh crore specifically for education, employment, and skilling. This funding is aimed at bolstering the skilling ecosystem across the country.\n\n2. **Key Schemes and Initiatives**: The budget outlines five major schemes and initiatives focused on employment and skilling. These initiatives are part of the Prime Minister's package and are designed to create employment-linked incentives, thereby encouraging skill development among the youth.\n\n3. **Focus on Inclusivity**: The skilling programs are designed to be inclusive, targeting various demographics, including women and marginalized communities. The aim is to reduce divisions based on gender and urban-rural disparities in access to skill development.\n\n4. **Bihar Skill Development Mission**: In Bihar, the government has initiated the Bihar Skill Development Mission (BSDM), which aims to empower youth by providing them with essential skills. This mission includes establishing a network of training centers and offering employment opportunities to the youth.\n\n5. **Employment-Linked Incentives**: The budget emphasizes the introduction of new employment-linked incentive schemes, which are expected to stimulate job creation and enhance the employability of the workforce.\n\n6. **Support for MSMEs**: The budget also recognizes the role of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs) in job creation and economic development. Support for MSMEs is expected to include skilling initiatives that align with the needs of these businesses.\n\nOverall, the Union Budget 2024-25 reflects a comprehensive approach to skilling, with a focus on creating a robust ecosystem that not only enhances the skills of the workforce but also addresses the employment challenges faced by various segments of society.
