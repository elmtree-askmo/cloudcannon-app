question: >-
    What types of assignments are included in the course?
answer: >-
    The course includes a variety of assignments designed to engage students with the material and assess their understanding. Here are the types of assignments typically included:\n\n1. **Written Assignments**: These are often expository in nature, requiring students to engage with ideas through evidence-based arguments presented in formal prose. Common written assignments may include essays that analyze specific policies or legislation, where students provide their expert opinions and suggest areas for improvement.\n\n2. **Annotated Bibliography**: This assignment requires students to gather and summarize relevant findings related to their topic of investigation, arranged alphabetically. It emphasizes the use of objective language and critical analysis.\n\n3. **Read or Watch, Then Respond**: In this format, students are assigned video lectures, articles, or book chapters followed by a set of questions to respond to, mirroring the traditional face-to-face lecture experience.\n\n4. **Posters and Presentations**: Students may be tasked with creating visual presentations or posters to convey their understanding of specific topics, which can enhance their communication skills.\n\n5. **Online Discussions and Posts**: Participation in online discussion boards is often a component of the course, allowing students to engage with their peers and instructors in a collaborative learning environment.\n\n6. **Final Paper**: A comprehensive written assignment that typically synthesizes the knowledge gained throughout the course, requiring students to demonstrate their understanding of the course material.\n\n7. **Oral Presentations**: Students may also be required to present their findings or analyses orally, which helps develop their public speaking and presentation skills.\n\nThese assignments are designed to foster critical thinking, enhance research skills, and encourage active engagement with the course content.
