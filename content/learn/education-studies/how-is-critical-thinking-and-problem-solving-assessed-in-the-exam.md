question: >-
    How is critical thinking and problem solving assessed in the exam?
answer: >-
    The assessment of critical thinking and problem-solving skills in the Fiji Year 8 Basic Science Examination is integrated into various sections of the exam, particularly in Sections A and B. \n\n1. **Section A** typically includes multiple-choice questions and short answer questions that require students to apply their knowledge to solve problems. These questions often present scenarios or experiments where students must analyze data, draw conclusions, and make inferences based on scientific principles.\n\n2. **Section B** may involve more complex tasks, such as interpreting graphs, analyzing experimental results, or answering open-ended questions that require students to explain their reasoning. For example, students might be asked to discuss the impact of human activities on ecosystems, which necessitates critical thinking about environmental science and the interconnections within ecosystems.\n\n3. **Sample Questions**: The exam may include questions that ask students to evaluate the effectiveness of different solutions to a problem or to predict outcomes based on changes in variables. For instance, a question might present a situation involving endangered species and ask students to propose conservation strategies, requiring them to think critically about ecological balance and human impact.\n\nOverall, the examination structure is designed to assess not only the students' knowledge of basic scientific principles but also their ability to think critically and solve problems in real-world contexts. This approach encourages students to engage with the material actively and develop essential skills for scientific inquiry.
