question: >-
    What kind of guidance can students expect from reaching out to the Harvard Undergraduate Admissions Council?
answer: >-
    Students reaching out to the Harvard Undergraduate Admissions Council can expect a variety of guidance and support tailored to their college search and application process. The council is composed of current undergraduate students who are dedicated to connecting with prospective students. They share their personal experiences and insights about life at Harvard, which can help high school students better understand what to expect from their college journey.\n\nSpecifically, the council serves as mentors for admitted students, playing a significant role in welcoming and hosting them during their transition to college life. This outreach is designed to inform prospective students about the robust opportunities available at Harvard, including academic programs, extracurricular activities, and campus life.\n\nAdditionally, students can seek advice on various aspects of the application process, including essay writing. The council encourages prospective students to ask questions and provides feedback that can help them present their best selves in their applications. This support is particularly valuable as Harvard employs a holistic admissions process, considering a wide range of factors beyond just grades and test scores.\n\nOverall, students can expect a friendly and informative experience when they reach out to the Harvard Undergraduate Admissions Council, which aims to assist them in making informed decisions about their college education.
