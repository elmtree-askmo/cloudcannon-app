question: >-
    What are the key initiatives for language preservation in Hawaii?
answer: >-
    Key initiatives for language preservation in Hawaii focus on revitalizing the Hawaiian language, which has faced significant decline due to historical colonization and globalization. Here are some of the most notable efforts:\n\n1. **Hawaiian Language Immersion Program (HLIP)**: Established in 1987 by the Hawaiʻi State Department of Education, this program has expanded to include numerous kaiapuni (immersion) schools across the state. It serves as a globally recognized model for indigenous language and culture revitalization, providing education entirely in Hawaiian.\n\n2. **ʻAha Pūnana Leo**: This organization has been instrumental in the revival of the Hawaiian language. It operates multiple locations throughout the Hawaiian Islands and serves hundreds of families each year. The organization has also played a crucial role in lifting the ban on teaching Hawaiian in public and private schools, which had been in place for nearly 90 years.\n\n3. **Hawaiian Language Newspapers**: These publications serve as cultural and linguistic anchors, promoting the use of Hawaiian in written form and fostering community engagement with the language.\n\n4. **Community Programs and Cultural Events**: Various initiatives encourage the speaking, teaching, and learning of Hawaiian through community engagement, cultural events, and educational programs. These efforts aim to foster a renewed appreciation for the language's cultural significance.\n\n5. **Legislative Support**: In 1978, Hawaiian was designated as one of the two official languages of Hawaii. This legal recognition has led to increased efforts to promote Hawaiian history, culture, and language through various state mandates.\n\n6. **Intergenerational Knowledge Sharing**: Programs that focus on passing down language skills and cultural knowledge from elders to younger generations are vital for sustaining the language and its associated cultural practices.\n\n7. **Native Hawaiian Radio Stations**: The establishment of Hawaiian language radio stations has provided platforms for broadcasting in Hawaiian, thus promoting the language and culture, especially during times when it was illegal to teach Hawaiian in schools.\n\nThese initiatives collectively contribute to the revitalization and preservation of the Hawaiian language, ensuring its continued use and cultural significance in the community.
