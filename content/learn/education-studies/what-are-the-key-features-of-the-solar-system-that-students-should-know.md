question: >-
    What are the key features of the solar system that students should know?
answer: >-
    The key features of the solar system that students should know include the following:\n\n1. **Structure of the Solar System**: The solar system consists of the Sun and everything that orbits around it, including eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and artificial satellites. The Sun, a massive star, contains more than 99% of the solar system's mass and is the central gravitational force that keeps the system in order.\n\n2. **Planets**: There are eight planets in the solar system, which are divided into two categories:\n   - **Inner Planets**: Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. These planets are rocky and are located closer to the Sun.\n   - **Outer Planets**: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These are gas giants, with Jupiter being the largest and Saturn known for its prominent rings.\n\n3. **Unique Characteristics**:\n   - **Mercury**: The smallest planet and closest to the Sun.\n   - **Venus**: The hottest planet due to its thick, toxic atmosphere.\n   - **Earth**: The only planet known to support life.\n   - **Mars**: Known as the red planet, it has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system.\n   - **Jupiter**: The largest planet, with a Great Red Spot, a giant storm.\n   - **Saturn**: Famous for its beautiful rings made of ice and rock particles.\n   - **Uranus**: Unique for its sideways rotation.\n   - **Neptune**: The coldest planet, known for its strong winds.\n\n4. **Formation and Age**: The solar system formed approximately 4.5 billion years ago from a cloud of gas and dust. \n\n5. **Other Objects**: The solar system also contains millions of asteroids, meteoroids, and comets. Comets originate from the Kuiper Belt and the Oort Cloud, which are regions beyond the planets.\n\n6. **Exploration**: Over 300 unmanned spacecraft have been launched to explore the solar system, including missions to Mars such as NASA's Curiosity and Perseverance rovers.\n\n7. **Galactic Context**: The solar system is a small part of the Milky Way galaxy, which contains billions of stars and other celestial objects.\n\nUnderstanding these features provides a foundational knowledge of the solar system, its components, and its significance in the broader context of the universe.
