This includes providing resources and support systems for students facing discrimination or bullying.\n\n### Hindering Factors\n\n1. **Lack of Representation**: Despite some progress, many schools still lack comprehensive LGBTQ-inclusive education. Research shows that only a small percentage of students receive education that includes transgender and gender non-conforming topics. This absence can lead to feelings of isolation and invisibility among students who identify outside the traditional gender binary.\n\n2. **Bullying and Harassment**: Transgender and gender nonconforming students often face significant challenges, including bullying and harassment. Studies indicate that these students experience higher rates of victimization, which can adversely affect their mental health and academic performance. Even in schools that are generally considered safe, the presence of bias-based harassment remains a critical issue.\n\n3. **Rigid Gender Norms**: Schools often reinforce traditional gender norms through policies and practices that do not accommodate non-binary or gender-fluid identities. For instance, facilities and dress codes that are strictly divided by gender can alienate students who do not conform to these binaries.\n\n4. **Cultural and Societal Influences**: The broader socio-economic context and prevailing cultural norms can also impact how schools address gender and sexuality.
