question: >-
    What are the core units in the Life and Society curriculum?
answer: >-
    The core units in the Life and Society curriculum, as outlined in the gathered information, include the following key areas:\n\n1. **Personal and Social Development**: This unit focuses on the growth and development of individuals within a social context, emphasizing the importance of understanding oneself and one's role in society.\n\n2. **Resources and Economic Activities**: This unit covers the basics of economic principles, resource management, and the various economic activities that take place locally, nationally, and globally.\n\n3. **Social Systems and Civic Spirit**: This unit aims to educate students about the social and political systems in their community and country, fostering a sense of civic responsibility and awareness of civic rights and duties.\n\nThese core units are designed to provide students with foundational knowledge and skills that are essential for understanding their local, national, and global contexts. The curriculum encourages exploration of global issues and promotes awareness of social and political systems, thereby preparing students for active citizenship. \n\nAdditionally, the curriculum is structured to include both core units and enrichment units, with each core unit further divided into foundational and extended learning components. This approach aims to enhance students' understanding and engagement with the material. \n\nOverall, the Life and Society curriculum is integral to developing informed and responsible citizens who are equipped to navigate the complexities of modern society.
