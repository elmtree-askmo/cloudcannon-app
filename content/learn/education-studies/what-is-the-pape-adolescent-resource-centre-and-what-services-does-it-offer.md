question: >-
    What is the Pape Adolescent Resource Centre and what services does it offer?
answer: >-
    The Pape Adolescent Resource Centre (PARC) is a voluntary program operated by the Children's Aid Society of Toronto (CAST) in collaboration with the Catholic Children's Aid Society and Jewish Family and Child Service. It is designed to support youth aged 16-29 who are transitioning to independent living, particularly those who have been in the child welfare system.\n\n### Services Offered by PARC:\n1. **Case Management**: Individualized support to help youth navigate their transition.\n2. **Group Programming**: Opportunities for youth to engage with peers and participate in group activities.\n3. **Life Skills Workshops**: Both in-person and virtual workshops aimed at developing essential life skills necessary for independent living.\n4. **Specialized Supports**: Tailored assistance to meet the unique needs of former youth in care.\n5. **Employment and Education Assistance**: Support in finding employment and pursuing educational opportunities.\n6. **Housing Support**: Guidance and resources to help youth secure stable housing.\n7. **Special Events**: Organized activities that foster community and connection among participants.\n\nPARC serves as a crucial resource for youth transitioning out of care, providing them with the tools and support necessary to achieve independence and stability in their lives. The centre is located at 469 Pape Ave, Toronto, ON, and operates with funding from the Ministry of Children and Youth Social Services and the Children's Aid Foundation. \n\nFor more information, individuals can contact PARC directly during their operating hours, which are Monday to Tuesday from 9:00 am to 9:00 pm.
