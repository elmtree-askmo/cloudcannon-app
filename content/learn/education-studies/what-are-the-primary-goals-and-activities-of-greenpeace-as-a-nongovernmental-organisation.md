question: >-
    What are the primary goals and activities of Greenpeace as a non-governmental organisation?
answer: >-
    Greenpeace is a prominent non-governmental organization (NGO) focused on environmental activism and advocacy. Its primary goals and activities can be summarized as follows:\n\n### Primary Goals:\n1. **Environmental Protection**: Greenpeace aims to protect the environment and promote sustainable practices to combat issues such as climate change, deforestation, overfishing, and pollution.\n2. **Biodiversity Conservation**: The organization works to preserve biodiversity, recognizing its critical role in maintaining the health of ecosystems and the planet.\n3. **Promotion of Sustainable Practices**: Greenpeace advocates for sustainable fishing, energy, and agricultural practices to mitigate the negative impacts of human activities on the environment.\n4. **Raising Awareness**: The organization seeks to educate the public and policymakers about environmental issues and the importance of taking action to protect the planet.\n\n### Key Activities:\n1. **Campaigning**: Greenpeace conducts campaigns to address specific environmental issues, such as overfishing and the impacts of climate change on marine biodiversity. For instance, they inspect environmental damages caused by overfishing and advocate for sustainable fishing practices to support communities reliant on fishing.\n2. **Research and Reporting**: The organization engages in research to gather data on environmental issues, which is then used to inform their campaigns and raise awareness about the consequences of environmental degradation.\n3. **Advocacy and Lobbying**: Greenpeace works to influence policy decisions at local, national, and international levels, pushing for legislation that supports environmental protection and sustainability.\n4. **Public Engagement**: The organization encourages public involvement through petitions, volunteering, and donations, fostering a community of individuals committed to environmental activism.\n5. **Direct Action**: Greenpeace is known for its direct action tactics, which may include peaceful protests and interventions to highlight environmental issues and hold corporations and governments accountable.\n\nIn summary, Greenpeace's efforts are centered around promoting a green and peaceful world through activism, education, and advocacy, with a strong emphasis on sustainable practices and biodiversity conservation.
