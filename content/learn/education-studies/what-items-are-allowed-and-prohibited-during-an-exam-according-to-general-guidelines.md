question: >-
    What items are allowed and prohibited during an exam according to general guidelines?
answer: >-
    Based on the gathered information, general guidelines for items allowed and prohibited during an exam typically include the following:\n\n### Allowed Items:\n1. **Writing Instruments**: Students are often instructed to use specific colors of ink, such as blue or black, to ensure legibility.\n2. **Calculators**: In many cases, calculators are permitted, especially for math or science exams. However, students should verify if specific types of calculators are allowed.\n3. **Reference Sheets**: Some exams may allow students to bring reference sheets or formula sheets, but this should be confirmed in advance.\n4. **Personal Items**: Items like water bottles may be allowed, but students should check specific guidelines.\n\n### Prohibited Items:\n1. **Electronic Devices**: Most exams prohibit the use of electronic devices such as smartphones, tablets, or laptops unless explicitly allowed for the exam.\n2. **Notes and Textbooks**: Generally, bringing notes, textbooks, or any form of study material is not permitted during the exam.\n3. **Distractions**: Items that could cause distractions, such as music players or any non-exam-related materials, are typically prohibited.\n4. **Food**: While some exams may allow water, food is usually not permitted unless specified.\n\n### Additional Guidelines:\n- Students are often instructed to place their electronic devices away from their desks to minimize distractions and prevent cheating.\n- Clear communication about what materials can be used during the exam is essential to ensure that all students understand the rules before the exam begins.\n\nIt is crucial for students to review the specific exam instructions provided by their instructors, as these guidelines can vary by institution and exam type.
