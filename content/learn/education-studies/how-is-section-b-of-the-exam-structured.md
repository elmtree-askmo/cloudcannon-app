question: >-
    How is Section B of the exam structured?
answer: >-
    Section B of the Fiji Year 8 Basic Science Examination is structured to assess students' understanding of various scientific principles. It is worth a total of 60 marks and typically includes multiple questions that cover different topics within the science curriculum.\n\nFor example, in one of the recent examinations, Section B included:\n\n- **Question 1**: Focused on "Living Things and the Environment," which is worth 15 marks. This question may include a 'true' or 'false' section to evaluate students' knowledge about ecological concepts and the relationships between organisms and their environments.\n\nThe exam is designed to test students' comprehension and application of basic scientific principles, and it may include a variety of question types such as multiple-choice, short answer, and true/false questions. The overall aim is to ensure that students can demonstrate their understanding of the material covered throughout the year.\n\nStudents are instructed to write their answers in the provided answer booklet, using either blue or black ink, and are given a total of 1Â½ hours for the exam, with an additional 10 minutes allowed for reading the paper. \n\nThis structure helps to ensure a comprehensive assessment of students' knowledge and skills in basic science.
