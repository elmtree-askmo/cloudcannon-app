question: >-
    What topics are covered under the 'Ecosystem and Conservation' section in the exam?
answer: >-
    The 'Ecosystem and Conservation' section of the Fiji Year 8 Basic Science Examination typically covers a variety of topics related to the understanding of ecosystems and the importance of conservation efforts. Based on the gathered information, the following topics are likely included:\n\n1. **Ecosystems**: Understanding the components of ecosystems, including biotic (living organisms) and abiotic (non-living elements) factors.\n2. **Coral Reefs**: Discussion on the benefits of coral reef systems, their ecological significance, and the impact of human activities on their survival.\n3. **Endangered Species**: Research and identification of common endangered species in Fiji, including their habitats and conservation needs.\n4. **Agroforestry**: Definition and importance of agroforestry practices in maintaining environmental balance and supporting biodiversity.\n5. **Conservation Strategies**: Various methods and strategies for conserving ecosystems and protecting endangered species.\n\nStudents may be required to engage in paragraph writing, research, and discussions on these topics, emphasizing the interconnections within ecosystems and the role of conservation in sustaining biodiversity.
