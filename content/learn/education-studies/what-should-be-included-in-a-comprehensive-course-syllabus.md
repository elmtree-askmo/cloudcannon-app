question: >-
    What should be included in a comprehensive course syllabus?
answer: >-
    A comprehensive course syllabus should include several key components to ensure that students have a clear understanding of the course structure, expectations, and resources. Hereâ€™s a detailed outline of what should be included:\n\n1. **Basic Information**:\n   - Name of the university, semester, and year.\n   - Course title, number, and unit value.\n   - Course meeting times and location.\n   - Instructor and Graduate Student Instructor (GSI) names.\n   - Contact information for the instructor/GSIs, including in-person office hours and location.\n\n2. **Course Materials**:\n   - Primary or required books/readings, including author, title, edition, costs, and availability of electronic or alternative formats for students with disabilities.\n   - Supplemental or optional readings.\n   - Relevant websites and links.\n   - Other materials necessary for the course.\n\n3. **Course Description and Objectives**:\n   - A brief description of the course content.\n   - Learning outcomes that outline what students are expected to achieve by the end of the course.\n\n4. **Course Policies and Expectations**:\n   - Clear rules and expectations for student conduct and participation.\n   - Grading policies, including details on how grades will be calculated and what constitutes different grade levels.\n   - Attendance policies and any penalties for non-compliance.\n\n5. **Course Calendar and Schedule**:\n   - A detailed schedule that includes topics, chapter readings, assignments, exams, and other requirements with due dates.\n\n6. **Academic Resources**:\n   - Information about academic support services available to students, such as tutoring centers, writing labs, and the Disability Resource Center for students needing accommodations.\n\n7. **Engagement and Participation**:\n   - Strategies for student engagement, including how students can participate in discussions and activities.\n   - Encouragement for students to communicate any challenges they face during the course.\n\n8. **Accessibility Statement**:\n   - A statement regarding accessibility, emphasizing the importance of accommodating students with disabilities and encouraging them to reach out for support.\n\n9. **Teaching Philosophy Statement** (optional):\n   - A brief statement that reflects the instructor's teaching philosophy and commitment to student success, which can help foster a positive learning environment.\n\n10. **Additional Resources**:\n    - Links to academic integrity tutorials and other relevant resources that support student learning and ethical conduct.\n\nBy including these components, a syllabus can serve as an effective contract between the instructor and students, providing a roadmap for the course and helping to prevent misunderstandings throughout the semester.
