question: >-
    How is the expression 'tener que' used in Spanish sentences?
answer: >-
    The expression "tener que" is a fundamental phrase in Spanish used to express obligation or necessity. It translates to "to have to" or "must" in English and is typically followed by an infinitive verb. The structure of the expression is straightforward: you conjugate the verb "tener" according to the subject, add "que," and then include the infinitive verb that indicates the action that needs to be taken.\n\n### Structure\nThe general formula is:\n$$\n\text{Tener (conjugated)} + \text{que} + \text{(infinitive verb)}\n$$\n\n### Examples\nHere are some example sentences demonstrating the use of "tener que":\n- **Tengo que hacer la tarea.** (I have to do the homework.)\n- **Tienes que terminar de leer el libro.** (You have to finish reading the book.)\n- **Ellos tienen que llegar temprano.** (They have to arrive early.)\n- **Tengo que ir al dentista.** (I have to go to the dentist.)\n\n### Usage\n"Tener que" is commonly used in various contexts to indicate obligations, responsibilities, or necessary actions. It is a versatile phrase that can be applied in both formal and informal situations. For instance:\n- **Tengo que trabajar mañana.** (I have to work tomorrow.)\n- **Vas a tener que abrir tu corazón algún día.** (You are going to have to open your heart someday.)\n\n### Key Points\n1. **Conjugation**: The verb "tener" must be conjugated according to the subject of the sentence (e.g., tengo, tienes, tienen).\n2. **Infinitive Verb**: The action that needs to be performed is expressed in the infinitive form.\n3. **Common Usage**: "Tener que" is often one of the first phrases learned by Spanish students due to its frequent use in everyday conversation.\n\nUnderstanding how to use "tener que" effectively allows learners to express obligations and necessities clearly in Spanish.
