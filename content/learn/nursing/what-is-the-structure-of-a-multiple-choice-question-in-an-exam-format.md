question: >-
    What is the structure of a multiple choice question in an exam format?
answer: >-
    The structure of a multiple-choice question (MCQ) in an exam format typically includes the following components:\n\n1. **Stem**: This is the question or statement that presents the problem or scenario. It is designed to assess the student's knowledge or understanding of a specific topic.\n\n2. **Options**: Each MCQ provides four possible answers labeled A, B, C, and D. Among these options, one is the correct answer, while the others are distractors (incorrect answers).\n\n3. **Format**: The question is presented in a clear and concise manner, ensuring that students can easily understand what is being asked. The options should be distinct and plausible to challenge the student's knowledge effectively.\n\n4. **Closed-Book Conditions**: In the context of the exam format mentioned, students are not allowed to use any external materials, which emphasizes the need for thorough preparation and understanding of the subject matter.\n\n5. **Identification Requirements**: Students must bring identification to verify their identity, which helps prevent proxy test-taking.\n\n6. **Allowed Items**: Personal items such as pens and plain water bottles are permitted, but items that could conceal answers are not allowed.\n\nOverall, the MCQ format is designed to efficiently assess a wide range of knowledge and understanding in a structured manner.
