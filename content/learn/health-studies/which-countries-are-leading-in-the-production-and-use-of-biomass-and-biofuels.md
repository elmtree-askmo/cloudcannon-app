question: >-
    Which countries are leading in the production and use of biomass and biofuels?
answer: >-
    As of 2023, the leading countries in the production and use of biomass and biofuels are as follows:\n\n1. **United States**: The U.S. is the largest producer of biofuels globally, with a production amounting to approximately 1,795 petajoules. The country has a well-established biofuel industry, primarily focusing on ethanol and biodiesel. In 2023, the U.S. recorded a biofuel consumption of 72.12 million metric tons, with ethanol being the dominant biofuel.\n\n2. **Brazil**: Brazil ranks second in biofuel production, with an output of around 1,016 petajoules. The country has a robust bioethanol program, primarily derived from sugarcane, and is known for its extensive use of biofuels in transportation.\n\n3. **Indonesia**: Indonesia is the third-largest producer of biofuels, contributing significantly to the global market. The country has been increasing its biofuel production capacity, particularly in biodiesel.\n\n4. **China**: While primarily known for its biomass electricity production, China is also a significant player in the biofuels market. In 2023, China produced more than 25% of the world's electricity from biomass, indicating a strong utilization of biomass resources.\n\n5. **Europe**: Collectively, European countries account for a substantial share of global biofuel consumption, with policies promoting the use of biofuels in transport. The region is a leader in producing heat from biomass, contributing to the overall biomass energy landscape.\n\nThese countries are at the forefront of biomass and biofuel production, driven by policies promoting renewable energy, technological advancements, and a growing demand for sustainable energy sources. The U.S., Brazil, and Indonesia, in particular, are key players in the biofuels market, accounting for a significant portion of global production and consumption.
