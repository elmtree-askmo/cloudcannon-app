question: >-
    What awards and recognitions did Mother Teresa receive for her humanitarian work?
answer: >-
    Mother Teresa, renowned for her humanitarian work, received numerous awards and recognitions throughout her life, reflecting her profound impact on society. Some of the most significant honors include:\n\n1. **Nobel Peace Prize (1979)**: Awarded for her efforts in bringing help to suffering humanity, this prestigious accolade recognized her dedication to alleviating poverty and distress.\n\n2. **Bharat Ratna (1980)**: This is India's highest civilian award, conferred upon her by the Indian government in recognition of her exceptional service to the nation and its people.\n\n3. **Padma Shri (1962)**: One of India's highest civilian honors, this award was given to her for her distinguished service to the people of India.\n\n4. **Pope John XXIII Peace Prize (1971)**: This award acknowledged her commitment to peace and her humanitarian efforts.\n\n5. **Jawaharlal Nehru Award for International Understanding (1972)**: This award recognized her contributions to international understanding and goodwill.\n\n6. **Presidential Medal of Freedom (1985)**: This is one of the highest civilian awards in the United States, awarded to her for her humanitarian work.\n\n7. **Balzan Prize (1978)**: This award was given for her contributions to humanity and peace among peoples.\n\nThroughout her lifetime, Mother Teresa received over 700 awards for her charitable work, making her one of the most awarded individuals of the 20th century. Her legacy continues to inspire humanitarian efforts worldwide, and her work has been honored through various awards established in her name, such as the Mother Teresa Memorial International Award for Social Justice.
