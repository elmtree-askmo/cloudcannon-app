question: >-
    What are the main objectives and goals of Mission Mausam?
answer: >-
    Mission Mausam is an ambitious initiative launched by the Government of India, primarily aimed at enhancing the country's capacity to predict and respond to extreme weather events and climate-related challenges. The mission is set to be implemented from 2024 to 2026, with a financial outlay of â‚¹2,000 crore approved by the Union Cabinet.\n\n### Main Objectives and Goals of Mission Mausam:\n\n1. **Transforming India into a "Weather-ready and Climate-smart" Nation**: The overarching goal of Mission Mausam is to mitigate the impacts of climate change and extreme weather events, thereby strengthening the resilience of communities across the country.\n\n2. **Advanced Weather and Climate Forecasting**: The mission aims to improve weather and climate forecasting capabilities through the integration of advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML). This includes the development of next-generation radars and satellites equipped with advanced instrument payloads.\n\n3. **Research and Development in Atmospheric Sciences**: Mission Mausam is designed to significantly boost research and development in atmospheric sciences, focusing on weather surveillance, modeling, forecasting, and management. This will involve establishing a wide network of radars, wind profilers, radiometers, and high-performance computing systems.\n\n4. **Capacity Building**: The initiative seeks to enhance the capacity of stakeholders, including citizens and last-mile users, to effectively tackle extreme weather events. This includes providing better weather data that can assist in agriculture, disaster management, and public health.\n\n5. **Cloud Simulation and Lightning Control**: Among its ambitious goals, Mission Mausam includes initiatives for cloud simulation and lightning control, which could lead to groundbreaking advancements in weather management.\n\nOverall, Mission Mausam represents a multi-faceted and transformative approach to improving India's weather and climate-related science, research, and services, ultimately aiming to create a more resilient society in the face of climate challenges.
