question: >-
    When and where did the initial cases of the Nipah Virus outbreak occur in Kerala?
answer: >-
    The initial cases of the Nipah Virus outbreak in Kerala were reported on May 19, 2018, in the Kozhikode district. This outbreak marked the first occurrence of the Nipah virus disease (NiV) in South India. By June 1, 2018, there were 18 confirmed cases and 17 deaths associated with this outbreak. The affected areas were primarily confined to the Kozhikode and Malappuram districts. The outbreak was traced back to fruit bats in the region and was officially declared over on June 10, 2018. \n\nSince then, Kerala has experienced additional outbreaks, including one in 2021 and the most recent one in late August 2023.
