question: >-
    What are the potential complications of untreated GLUT1 deficiency syndrome?
answer: >-
    Untreated GLUT1 deficiency syndrome (GLUT1-DS) can lead to a range of serious complications due to impaired glucose transport across the blood-brain barrier, which is essential for brain function. The potential complications of untreated GLUT1 deficiency syndrome include:\n\n1. **Seizures**: Many patients experience anticonvulsant-refractory epilepsy, which can manifest as infantile seizures and other seizure types. These seizures can be difficult to control without appropriate treatment.\n\n2. **Developmental Delays**: Affected individuals often face significant developmental delays, which can impact cognitive and motor skills. This can lead to challenges in learning and daily functioning.\n\n3. **Acquired Microcephaly**: The condition may result in a reduction in head size over time, known as acquired microcephaly, which is associated with abnormal brain development.\n\n4. **Movement Disorders**: Patients may develop complex movement disorders, including spasticity and ataxia, which can affect coordination and balance.\n\n5. **Cognitive Impairments**: Long-term cognitive deficits are common, affecting the ability to process information and perform tasks.\n\n6. **Impaired Brain Microvasculature Development**: There may be complications related to the development of the brain's microvasculature, which can further exacerbate neurological issues.\n\n7. **Hypoglycorrhachia**: This condition, characterized by low glucose levels in the cerebrospinal fluid, can lead to additional neurological complications.\n\n8. **Long-term Health Risks**: Patients may also face long-term health risks associated with dietary interventions, such as kidney stones, growth retardation, and cardiovascular issues.\n\nOverall, the untreated condition can lead to significant morbidity, emphasizing the importance of early diagnosis and intervention, typically through dietary management such as a ketogenic diet, which can help mitigate some of these complications.
