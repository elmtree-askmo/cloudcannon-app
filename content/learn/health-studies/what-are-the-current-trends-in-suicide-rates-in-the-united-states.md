question: >-
    What are the current trends in suicide rates in the United States?
answer: >-
    Current trends in suicide rates in the United States indicate a concerning increase, with 2022 recording the highest number of suicide deaths on record, surpassing the previous peak in 2018 by over 1,000 deaths. According to provisional data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), from 2011 to 2022, more than half a million lives (539,810) were lost to suicide.\n\nKey statistics from recent analyses include:\n\n1. **Overall Increase**: Between 2011 and 2021, suicide death rates increased by 25% or more in 12 states, with the most significant increases observed in Alaska (54%), South Dakota (48%), Nebraska (43%), and Montana (42%).\n\n2. **Demographic Disparities**: The suicide rate among males in 2022 was approximately four times higher than that of females, with males accounting for nearly 80% of all suicides despite making up 50% of the population. The highest rates of suicide were observed among individuals aged 85 and older.\n\n3. **Age-Specific Trends**: While suicide rates increased for individuals aged 35 and older, there was a decrease in rates for those aged 34 and below. Specifically, the suicide rate for adolescents and young adults aged 15 to 24 declined from 15.15 per 100,000 in 2021 to 13.62 per 100,000 in 2022.\n\n4. **Racial and Ethnic Disparities**: From 2011 to 2021, suicide rates among people of color rose significantly, with the highest increase among American Indian/Alaskan Native (AIAN) individuals (70% increase), followed by Black (58%) and Hispanic (39%) populations.\n\n5. **Leading Causes of Death**: Suicide remains one of the leading causes of death in the United States, particularly among younger adults. It was the second leading cause of death for individuals aged 10-34 and the fifth leading cause for those aged 35-54 in 2021.\n\nThese trends highlight the urgent need for public health interventions and continued research to address the factors contributing to rising suicide rates and to provide support for at-risk populations.
