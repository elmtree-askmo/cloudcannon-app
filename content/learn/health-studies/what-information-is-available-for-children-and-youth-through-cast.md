question: >-
    What information is available for children and youth through CAST?
answer: >-
    The Children's Aid Society of Toronto (CAST) provides a variety of resources and services aimed at supporting children and youth, particularly those who are at risk of abuse or neglect. Here are the key offerings available through CAST:\n\n1. **Protection Services**: CAST is mandated to protect children and youth from abuse and neglect. This includes intervention in cases of physical, sexual, and emotional abuse, ensuring that children are safe and supported.\n\n2. **Foster Care and Kinship Care**: For children and youth who cannot safely remain at home, CAST facilitates foster care placements. Foster parents provide a temporary, supportive, and stable environment. Kinship caregivers may also be utilized when appropriate.\n\n3. **Voluntary Youth Services Agreement (VYSA)**: This program is designed for youth aged 16 and 17, helping them to support themselves as they transition to independent living. It includes access to counseling and other resources.\n\n4. **Community and Family Support**: CAST works collaboratively with parents, caregivers, and the community to create safe environments for children and youth. This includes culturally competent services that aim to strengthen families during times of crisis.\n\n5. **Youth Mentorship and Employment Services**: CAST offers mentorship programs and employment services to help youth develop skills and gain work experience, which are crucial for their personal and professional growth.\n\n6. **Mental Health Resources**: Access to mental health support is provided to help children and youth cope with various challenges they may face.\n\n7. **Food Supports and Housing Services**: CAST also offers food assistance and housing services to ensure that basic needs are met for children and youth in their care.\n\n8. **Rights and Resources**: CAST emphasizes the rights of children and youth, providing them with information and resources to understand and advocate for their rights.\n\nOverall, CAST is dedicated to ensuring the safety and well-being of children and youth in Toronto, providing a comprehensive range of services to support their development and protect them from harm. For more information or to access services, individuals can contact CAST directly at 416-924-4646 or 1-866-527-0833, available 24/7.
