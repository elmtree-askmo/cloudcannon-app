question: >-
    What are inhalants and what risks do they pose to users?
answer: >-
    Inhalants are substances that produce chemical vapors which can be inhaled to induce a psychoactive effect, often described as a "high." They are commonly found in various household and workplace products, and can be categorized into four main types: volatile solvents (like paint thinners and glue), aerosols (such as spray paints and deodorants), gases (including nitrous oxide), and nitrites (often used in some sexual enhancement products).\n\n### Risks Associated with Inhalant Use\n\n1. **Short-term Effects**: Inhalants can cause immediate effects such as euphoria, dizziness, and hallucinations. However, they can also lead to severe health risks, including loss of consciousness, asphyxiation, and even sudden death from a single use.\n\n2. **Long-term Effects**: Chronic inhalant use can result in irreversible damage to the brain and other organs. Long-term health consequences may include:\n   - Neurological damage\n   - Liver and kidney damage\n   - Hearing loss\n   - Bone marrow disease\n   - Cognitive impairments\n   - Psychological issues such as anxiety and irritability upon withdrawal\n\n3. **Addiction and Dependence**: Contrary to the misconception that inhalants are less addictive than other drugs, they can lead to rapid physical and psychological dependence. Users may experience withdrawal symptoms when they stop using inhalants, which can complicate recovery efforts.\n\n4. **Fatal Risks**: The toxicity of inhalants means that even first-time use can be fatal. Regular use can lead to life-threatening conditions, including overdose and severe health complications.\n\nInhalant use is particularly concerning among young people, as it is often associated with accessibility and experimentation. Treatment for inhalant use disorder can occur in various settings, including outpatient and inpatient care, emphasizing the need for professional help for those struggling with addiction.\n\nOverall, inhalants pose significant health risks that can have devastating short- and long-term effects on users.
