question: >-
    What type of trial design was used in the community-based diabetes prevention study?
answer: >-
    The community-based diabetes prevention studies mentioned utilize various trial designs. Specifically, the "Reaching Out and Preventing Increases in Diabetes" (RAPID) study is a community-based randomized trial that evaluates the comparative costs and effectiveness of a group-based adaptation of the Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) lifestyle intervention in partnership with the YMCA. \n\nAdditionally, the "Pre-Diabetes Interventions and Continued Tracking to Ease-out Diabetes" (Pre-DICTED) trial employs a pragmatic trial design, comparing the effectiveness of a community-based stepwise diabetes prevention program with added financial incentives against the standard of care.\n\nThese designs highlight the focus on community engagement and practical application in real-world settings, which are essential for effective diabetes prevention strategies.
