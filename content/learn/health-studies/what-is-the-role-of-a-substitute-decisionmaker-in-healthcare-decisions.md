question: >-
    What is the role of a substitute decision-maker in healthcare decisions?
answer: >-
    A substitute decision-maker (SDM) plays a crucial role in healthcare decisions for individuals who are unable to make those decisions for themselves due to incapacity. The primary responsibility of an SDM is to act in the best interest of the patient, guided by the principle of what the patient would want if they were capable of making their own decisions.\n\nHere are the key roles and responsibilities of a substitute decision-maker:\n\n1. **Understanding the Patient's Wishes**: The SDM must engage with the patient, if possible, to understand their values, beliefs, and preferences regarding healthcare. This involves discussions about what is important to the patient, which can help inform decisions about treatment and care.\n\n2. **Making Healthcare Decisions**: The SDM is authorized to make decisions on behalf of the patient regarding various aspects of their care, including medical treatment, nutrition, living arrangements, hygiene, and safety. This encompasses both immediate healthcare needs and long-term care considerations.\n\n3. **Legal Authority**: In Ontario, the role of the SDM is governed by the Health Care Consent Act, 1996. This legislation outlines the process for determining who can act as an SDM and the legal framework within which they operate. If a designated SDM is unable or unwilling to fulfill their role, healthcare providers will refer to the next eligible individual according to the hierarchy established by law.\n\n4. **Informed Consent**: Healthcare providers must obtain informed consent from either the patient or their SDM before proceeding with treatment. This ensures that the patient's rights and preferences are respected, even when they cannot express them directly.\n\n5. **Navigating Legal and Ethical Considerations**: The SDM must be aware of the legal implications of their decisions, including the potential for statutory limitations on their authority. They may need to navigate complex situations where the patient's wishes are not clear or where there are disagreements among family members.\n\nIn summary, the substitute decision-maker serves as a vital advocate for patients who cannot advocate for themselves, ensuring that their healthcare decisions align with their values and preferences while adhering to legal requirements.
