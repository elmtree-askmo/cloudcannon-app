question: >-
    What initiatives does the United Nations Fisheries and Aquaculture Department undertake to promote sustainable fishing?
answer: >-
    The United Nations Fisheries and Aquaculture Department, primarily through the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), undertakes several initiatives to promote sustainable fishing practices globally. Here are some key initiatives:\n\n1. **Sustainable Aquaculture Guidelines**: The FAO is developing Sustainable Aquaculture Guidelines (SAG) to provide practical guidance to government authorities and policymakers. These guidelines aim to promote the implementation of the Code of Conduct for Responsible Fisheries, ensuring that aquaculture practices meet the growing demand for aquatic food sustainably.\n\n2. **Blue Transformation**: The FAO's "Blue Transformation" initiative focuses on integrating aquatic foods into global food security and sustainability. This initiative includes enhancing policy advocacy, scientific research, and capacity building, as well as disseminating sustainable practices and technological innovations.\n\n3. **International Years and Decades**: The FAO supports various international observances, such as the United Nations Decade of Ocean Science for Sustainable Development (2021-2030) and the International Year of Artisanal Fisheries and Aquaculture (2022). These initiatives aim to raise awareness and promote sustainable practices in fisheries and aquaculture.\n\n4. **Capacity Building and Community Involvement**: The FAO collaborates with member countries, partners, and stakeholders to build capacity and involve communities in sustainable fishing practices. This includes providing evidence-based information and insights to policymakers, managers, and fishers.\n\n5. **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**: The fishing and seafood sector plays a crucial role in achieving the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals, particularly SDG 14, which focuses on life below water. The FAO's initiatives are aligned with these goals, promoting sustainable management of marine resources.\n\n6. **Addressing Emerging Issues**: The FAO is actively involved in addressing emerging issues such as climate change and the impacts of COVID-19 on fisheries and aquaculture. This includes adapting practices to ensure resilience and sustainability in the face of these challenges.\n\nThrough these initiatives, the United Nations Fisheries and Aquaculture Department aims to foster sustainable fishing practices that contribute to food security, environmental health, and economic development.
