question: >-
    What does the global hunger assessment reveal about food insecurity and malnutrition?
answer: >-
    The global hunger assessment, particularly highlighted in the 2023 State of Food Security and Nutrition in the World (SOFI) report, reveals significant challenges regarding food insecurity and malnutrition. As of 2023, approximately 733 million people worldwide faced hunger, which translates to one in eleven individuals globally and one in five in Africa. This marks a concerning plateau in global hunger levels, with the number of undernourished individuals remaining between 713 and 757 million for three consecutive years, indicating an increase of about 152 million since 2019.\n\nThe report emphasizes that despite some progress in areas such as reducing stunting and promoting exclusive breastfeeding, the overall situation remains dire. The Global Hunger Index (GHI) score reflects a dangerous array of overlapping crises, including the impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic, the Russia-Ukraine war, and various conflicts and climate disasters. These factors have exacerbated food insecurity and malnutrition, with the prevalence of moderate or severe food insecurity remaining alarmingly high.\n\nIn India, the situation is particularly severe, with an estimated 200 million people suffering from malnutrition, highlighting widespread food insecurity. The report suggests that variations in methodologies for assessing food insecurity contribute to uncertainty in data and the severity of the issue across the country. It calls for increased public investment in food security and nutrition programs, emphasizing the need for effective resource allocation to address the root causes of food insecurity and malnutrition.\n\nOverall, the global hunger assessment underscores the urgent need for comprehensive strategies to combat food insecurity and malnutrition, particularly in vulnerable populations, to meet the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by 2030.
