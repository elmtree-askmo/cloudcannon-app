question: >-
    What are the primary components of the musculoskeletal system?
answer: >-
    The primary components of the musculoskeletal system include:\n\n1. **Bones**: The skeletal system is primarily composed of bones, which provide structure, support, and shape to the body. In adults, there are typically 206 bones that make up the skeleton.\n\n2. **Muscles**: The muscular system consists of over 600 skeletal muscles that facilitate movement by contracting and relaxing. These muscles are attached to bones via tendons.\n\n3. **Cartilage**: This flexible connective tissue acts as padding between bones at joints, reducing friction and absorbing shock.\n\n4. **Tendons**: Tendons are strong connective tissues that connect muscles to bones, enabling the transfer of force from muscle contractions to bone movement.\n\n5. **Ligaments**: These are fibrous connective tissues that connect bones to other bones at joints, providing stability and support.\n\n6. **Joints**: Joints are the points where two or more bones meet, allowing for movement and flexibility. They can be classified into various types based on their structure and function.\n\n7. **Other Connective Tissues**: This includes bursae (small fluid-filled sacs that reduce friction), fascia (a connective tissue that surrounds muscles), and other connective tissues that support and bind various structures within the body.\n\nTogether, these components work in harmony to enable movement, provide stability, and protect vital organs, making the musculoskeletal system essential for overall bodily function.
