question: >-
    What are the common symptoms associated with squamous cell lung cancer?
answer: >-
    Squamous cell lung cancer (SCC) is a subtype of non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) that originates from the squamous cells lining the airways. It is particularly associated with smoking and is one of the more common types of lung cancer. The symptoms of squamous cell lung cancer can vary, but some of the most common symptoms include:\n\n1. **Persistent Cough**: A new, persistent cough that does not go away or worsens over time is often one of the earliest signs of lung cancer.\n\n2. **Wheezing**: This is a high-pitched whistling sound made while breathing, which can indicate airway obstruction.\n\n3. **Difficulty Swallowing**: Patients may experience trouble swallowing, which can be a result of the tumor pressing on the esophagus.\n\n4. **Swelling in the Face or Neck**: This can occur due to the obstruction of blood flow in the veins, often referred to as superior vena cava syndrome.\n\n5. **Fatigue**: Extreme tiredness that does not improve with rest is a common symptom associated with many cancers, including SCC.\n\n6. **Weight Loss and Loss of Appetite**: Unexplained weight loss and a decrease in appetite can be indicative of cancer.\n\n7. **Pain**: Patients may experience pain throughout the body or localized pain in the chest area.\n\n8. **Headaches**: These can occur if the cancer has metastasized to the brain or due to increased intracranial pressure.\n\n9. **Broken or Fractured Bones**: This can happen if the cancer has spread to the bones, leading to weakened bone structure.\n\nIt is important to note that having one or more of these symptoms does not necessarily mean a person has squamous cell lung cancer, as these symptoms can be associated with various other conditions. Early detection and diagnosis are crucial for improving prognosis and treatment outcomes.
