    What types of questions are included in the Structured Question Sections?
answer: >-
    The Structured Question Sections in the Fiji Year 8 Healthy Living Examination typically include a variety of question types that assess students' understanding of health-related topics. While the gathered information does not provide specific details about the exact types of questions included in these sections, it is common for such assessments to feature:\n\n1. **Short Answer Questions**: These require students to provide concise responses based on their knowledge of health topics, such as nutrition, personal safety, and emotional well-being.\n\n2. **Scenario-Based Questions**: Students may be presented with hypothetical situations related to health and asked to analyze or respond to them, demonstrating their critical thinking and application of health education principles.\n\n3. **True or False Questions**: These questions assess students' ability to discern factual information about health topics.\n\n4. **Matching Questions**: Students might be asked to match terms with their definitions or related concepts, reinforcing their understanding of key health vocabulary.\n\n5. **Descriptive Questions**: These require students to elaborate on specific health concepts, such as the importance of physical activity or the impact of non-communicable diseases.\n\nOverall, the Structured Question Sections aim to evaluate students' comprehension and critical thinking skills in relation to health education, covering various aspects of healthy living, personal safety, and emotional changes.
