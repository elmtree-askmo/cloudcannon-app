question: >-
    How does livestock production affect land and water resources?
answer: >-
    Livestock production significantly affects land and water resources in various ways, contributing to environmental challenges and resource depletion. Here are some key points regarding its impact:\n\n1. **Resource Use**: Livestock production is responsible for a substantial portion of agricultural land and water use. It occupies approximately 70% of all agricultural land and 30% of the Earth's land surface is directly or indirectly involved in livestock production (FAO, 2006). This high demand for land and water resources is particularly concerning given the increasing global population and the corresponding rise in food demand.\n\n2. **Water Consumption**: The livestock sector is a major consumer of freshwater, accounting for over 90% of global consumptive freshwater use (Hoekstra and Mekonnen, 2012). Livestock farming utilizes about 30% of the Earth's fresh water, primarily for animal hydration and feed production. The type of water resources involved includes both "green" precipitation water and "blue" water from surface and groundwater sources, which can lead to water scarcity in regions where livestock farming is prevalent.\n\n3. **Land Degradation**: The expansion of livestock farming contributes to land degradation through overgrazing, which causes soil compaction and erosion. This degradation affects the quality of 13% of global soil, including 34% of agricultural land. The excessive use of fertilizers and the conversion of natural habitats into grazing lands further exacerbate this issue, leading to biodiversity loss and reduced land productivity (FAO, 2006).\n\n4. **Environmental Pollution**: Livestock production is a leading driver of air, water, and land pollution. It contributes to greenhouse gas emissions, accounting for approximately 14.5% of all anthropogenic emissions, which includes 7.1 gigatonnes of CO2 equivalent per year. Additionally, the runoff from livestock operations can contaminate water bodies, affecting water quality and aquatic ecosystems.\n\n5. **Biodiversity Impact**: The demand for animal products has led to habitat conversion and resource demand that negatively impacts biodiversity. Livestock farming can lead to the spread of invasive species and overexploitation of natural resources, further threatening wildlife and ecosystem health (Gerber et al., 2013).\n\nIn summary, livestock production exerts considerable pressure on land and water resources, leading to significant environmental impacts such as land degradation, water scarcity, and pollution. Addressing these challenges requires improving resource use efficiency and adopting sustainable practices in livestock management.
