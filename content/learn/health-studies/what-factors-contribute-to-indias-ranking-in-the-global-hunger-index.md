question: >-
    What factors contribute to India's ranking in the Global Hunger Index?
answer: >-
    India's ranking in the Global Hunger Index (GHI) 2024 is influenced by several interrelated factors that highlight the ongoing challenges of food security and nutrition in the country. Here are the key contributors to India's position, which stands at 105 out of 127 countries with a score of 27.3, categorizing it in the 'serious' range:\n\n1. **Undernourishment**: A significant portion of the Indian population, estimated at 16.3%, is undernourished. This high prevalence of undernourishment is a critical factor affecting the GHI score.\n\n2. **Child Malnutrition**: India faces alarming rates of child malnutrition, including the highest global rate of child wasting at 18.7%. Child stunting and mortality rates also contribute to the overall GHI score, reflecting the severe impact of hunger on the most vulnerable populations.\n\n3. **Economic Disparities**: Despite India's economic growth, the benefits have not translated into improved social indicators related to hunger and nutrition. This disconnect raises concerns about the effectiveness of economic policies in addressing food security.\n\n4. **Government Initiatives**: While initiatives like the National Food Security Act and the PM Garib Kalyan Yojana aim to provide food assistance, their effectiveness is often questioned. The ongoing challenges in implementation and reach of these programs can hinder their impact on reducing hunger.\n\n5. **Socio-Economic Factors**: Broader socio-economic issues, including poverty, gender inequality, and climate change, exacerbate food insecurity. The interlinking of these factors complicates the situation and requires comprehensive policy responses.\n\n6. **Comparative Performance**: India's GHI ranking is particularly concerning when compared to neighboring countries like Bangladesh and Nepal, which have achieved better rankings despite having fewer economic resources. This comparison underscores the need for targeted interventions to improve food security in India.\n\nIn summary, India's GHI ranking reflects a complex interplay of undernourishment, child malnutrition, economic disparities, and the effectiveness of government initiatives, all of which contribute to the ongoing hunger crisis in the country. Addressing these issues will require a multifaceted approach that goes beyond economic growth to ensure food security and improved nutrition for all citizens.
