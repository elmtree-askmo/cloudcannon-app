question: >-
    What resources are available to students to support their learning in this course?
answer: >-
    Students enrolled in courses related to Field Epidemiology have access to a variety of resources designed to support their learning and enhance their skills. Here are some key resources available:\n\n1. **eLearning Courses**: The Field Epidemiology in Action initiative offers several eLearning courses that are freely accessible. These include:\n   - Excel Fundamentals for Field Epidemiology\n   - Rapid Response Team Training\n   - Basics of Public Health Surveillance (currently a three-module course, with the third module under development)\n   - Outbreak Investigation course (newly launched)\n\n2. **Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP) Resources**: Students can access a library of resources that includes case studies and other educational materials. Some materials may require instructor access, but the library serves as a valuable repository for building knowledge and skills in epidemiology and public health.\n\n3. **Online Certificate in Field Epidemiology**: Offered by the UNC Gillings School of Global Public Health, this program is tailored for working practitioners and emphasizes practical skills. Courses such as "Fundamentals of Public Health Surveillance" and "Methods in Field Epidemiology" provide foundational knowledge and practical applications for public health challenges.\n\n4. **Campus and Online Support Resources**: Public health students have access to various support services, including:\n   - Academic writing assistance\n   - Research and library resources\n   - Computer support\n   - Internship and fellowship opportunities\n   - Outpatient health care services\n\n5. **Public Health Resource Centre (PHRC)**: This center offers support for anyone involved in public health, including students. Membership is free and provides access to additional resources and networking opportunities.\n\n6. **Financial Aid Resources**: Information on scholarships, grants, loans, and other financial aid opportunities is available to help students finance their education.\n\nThese resources collectively aim to enhance the educational experience of students in the field of epidemiology, providing them with the tools and support necessary to succeed in their studies and future careers.
