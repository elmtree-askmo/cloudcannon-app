question: >-
    What is the public health impact of road traffic collisions related to drug driving?
answer: >-
    The public health impact of road traffic collisions related to drug driving is significant and multifaceted. Drug driving is a major public health concern, contributing to a substantial number of road traffic accidents, injuries, and fatalities. In the United States, for instance, motor vehicle crashes are the second leading cause of unintentional injury deaths, with drugged driving increasingly recognized as a critical factor. In 2020, 25.3% of the 38,824 fatalities in the U.S. were related to drug-involved driving, highlighting the severity of this issue.\n\nResearch indicates that the presence of drugs, including marijuana, opioids, and other illicit substances, can significantly impair driving abilities. For example, drivers with THC (the active component in cannabis) in their blood are approximately twice as likely to be involved in fatal crashes compared to sober drivers. Additionally, the combination of drugs, such as alcohol and marijuana, can exacerbate impairment, leading to even higher crash risks.\n\nIn the UK, drug driving has also seen a troubling increase, with a reported 60% rise in the number of deceased drivers testing positive for drugs from 2014 to 2019. This trend reflects not only the growing prevalence of drug use among drivers but also improvements in testing and record-keeping practices. The impact of drug driving extends beyond immediate road traffic injuries; it contributes to broader public health issues, including mental health problems, domestic violence, and other accidental injuries.\n\nYoung people, particularly those aged 16 to 19, are especially vulnerable, as car crashes are the leading cause of death in this age group. A study found that 1 in 6 college students had driven under the influence of drugs other than alcohol at least once in the past year, indicating a concerning trend among younger populations.\n\nOverall, the public health impact of drug driving is profound, necessitating comprehensive prevention strategies and public health initiatives to address this growing problem effectively.
