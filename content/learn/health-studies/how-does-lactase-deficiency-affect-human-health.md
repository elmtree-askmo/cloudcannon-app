question: >-
    How does lactase deficiency affect human health?
answer: >-
    Lactase deficiency, commonly known as lactose intolerance, significantly affects human health, particularly in individuals who are unable to properly digest lactose, the sugar found in milk and dairy products. This condition arises from a deficiency in the enzyme lactase, which is responsible for breaking down lactose into glucose and galactose for absorption in the small intestine.\n\n### Prevalence and Types\nLactase deficiency is most prevalent among certain ethnic groups, including African Americans, Hispanics/Latinos, and Asians, with lower rates observed in individuals of European descent. The primary form of lactase deficiency, known as lactase non-persistence, typically develops after weaning and can lead to symptoms in late childhood, adolescence, or adulthood. In contrast, congenital lactase deficiency is a rare genetic condition where infants are born with little to no lactase activity, leading to immediate symptoms upon milk ingestion.\n\n### Symptoms\nIndividuals with lactase deficiency may experience a range of gastrointestinal symptoms after consuming lactose-containing foods. Common symptoms include:\n- Abdominal bloating and pain\n- Loose stools (diarrhea)\n- Nausea\n- Flatulence (gas)\n- Borborygmi (stomach rumbling)\n\nLess commonly, symptoms may extend to headaches, muscle and joint pain, mouth ulcers, urinary symptoms, and cognitive issues such as loss of concentration.\n\n### Nutritional Impact\nOne of the significant health implications of lactase deficiency is the potential for nutritional deficiencies. Many dairy products are rich sources of essential nutrients, particularly calcium and vitamin D. Individuals who avoid dairy due to lactose intolerance may struggle to meet their nutritional needs, which can lead to conditions such as osteoporosis or other health issues related to inadequate nutrient intake.\n\n### Management\nManagement of lactase deficiency typically involves dietary modifications, such as reducing or eliminating lactose-containing foods. Lactose-free alternatives and enzyme supplements that provide lactase can help individuals manage their symptoms while still obtaining necessary nutrients. It is also important for individuals with lactose intolerance to read food labels carefully to avoid hidden sources of lactose.\n\n### Conclusion\nIn summary, lactase deficiency can have a profound impact on an individual's health, affecting not only digestive comfort but also nutritional status. Awareness of the condition and appropriate management strategies are essential for maintaining overall health and well-being in affected individuals.
