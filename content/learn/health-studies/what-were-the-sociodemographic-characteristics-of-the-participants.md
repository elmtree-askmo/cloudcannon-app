question: >-
    What were the socio-demographic characteristics of the participants?
answer: >-
    The socio-demographic characteristics of participants in community-based diabetes prevention programs can vary significantly depending on the specific study and its target population. Based on the gathered information, here are some key characteristics observed in various studies:\n\n1. **Gender**: A significant majority of participants in some studies were female, with one study reporting that 65.6% of participants were women.\n\n2. **Age**: Participants typically included adults aged 18 and older. For instance, one study focused on community-dwelling African Americans aged 18 and above who had a physician-diagnosed type 2 diabetes.\n\n3. **Ethnicity/Race**: Some studies aimed to include a diverse racial and ethnic representation, although limitations were noted in achieving a broad spectrum of diversity. For example, one study specifically targeted African American participants.\n\n4. **Education Level**: Educational attainment varied, with some studies indicating that a significant portion of participants had tertiary education. \n\n5. **Geographic Location**: Participants were often drawn from specific regions, such as southern European countries or urban areas like Durham County, North Carolina, indicating a focus on local community engagement.\n\n6. **Socioeconomic Status**: Efforts were made to include participants across the socioeconomic spectrum, although the extent of this inclusion varied by study.\n\n7. **Health Status**: Many participants were at risk for type 2 diabetes or had a diagnosis of prediabetes or type 2 diabetes, which was a criterion for inclusion in these programs.\n\nThese characteristics are crucial for understanding the demographic context of the studies and the applicability of the findings to broader populations. Future research may benefit from further exploration of how these socio-demographic factors influence the effectiveness of diabetes prevention interventions.
