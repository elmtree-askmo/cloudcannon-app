question: >-
    What are the common symptoms and signs of hyperthyroidism?
answer: >-
    Hyperthyroidism, characterized by the overproduction of thyroid hormones, presents a variety of symptoms that can significantly impact an individual's quality of life. Common symptoms and signs of hyperthyroidism include:\n\n1. **Weight Loss**: Despite an increased appetite, individuals often experience unplanned weight loss.\n2. **Increased Appetite**: Many patients report feeling hungrier than usual.\n3. **Nervousness and Anxiety**: A heightened state of anxiety and nervousness is frequently observed.\n4. **Irritability**: Mood swings and irritability can be common.\n5. **Fatigue**: Severe fatigue is a prevalent symptom, even with adequate rest.\n6. **Tremors**: Fine tremors, particularly in the hands, may occur.\n7. **Rapid or Irregular Heartbeat**: Palpitations or a racing heart are typical signs.\n8. **Increased Sweating**: Patients may experience excessive sweating and heat intolerance.\n9. **Muscle Weakness**: Weakness, particularly in the upper arms and thighs, can be noted.\n10. **Frequent Bowel Movements**: Some individuals may experience increased frequency of bowel movements or diarrhea.\n11. **Goiter**: An enlarged thyroid gland (goiter) may be visibly noticeable.\n12. **Difficulty Sleeping**: Insomnia or trouble sleeping is often reported.\n\nIn severe cases, hyperthyroidism can lead to a thyroid crisis (thyroid storm), which is a sudden worsening of symptoms that may include fever, decreased alertness, and abdominal pain. \n\nIt is important to note that these symptoms can overlap with other health conditions, making diagnosis challenging. Therefore, a comprehensive evaluation by a healthcare provider, including blood tests and imaging, is essential for accurate diagnosis and management.
