question: >-
    What are the conditions under which a data subject can object to data processing?
answer: >-
    Under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) in the Philippines, a data subject has the right to object to the processing of their personal data under specific conditions. These conditions include:\n\n1. **General Right to Object**: A data subject can object to the processing of their personal data, including processing for direct marketing, automated processing, or profiling. This right allows individuals to refuse consent for their data to be processed in these contexts.\n\n2. **Notification and Opportunity to Object**: The data subject must be notified and given an opportunity to withhold consent to the processing in case of any changes or amendments to the information supplied regarding the processing of their personal data.\n\n3. **Exceptions to the Right to Object**: The personal information controller (PIC) must cease processing the personal data if the data subject objects, unless the processing falls under other allowable instances as specified in Sections 12 or 13 of the Act, which include situations where processing is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation or for the performance of a contract.\n\n4. **Informed Consent**: Before the entry of personal data into the processing system, the data subject should be informed about the purposes for which their data will be processed, the scope and method of processing, and the identity of the personal information controller. This transparency is crucial for the data subject to make an informed decision regarding their consent.\n\nIn summary, a data subject can object to data processing if they are informed of their rights and the purposes of processing, and if the processing does not fall under specific exceptions outlined in the law. This framework aims to protect the privacy rights of individuals while allowing for necessary data processing under regulated conditions.
