question: >-
    How can individuals exercise their right to be informed about data processing activities?
answer: >-
    Individuals can exercise their right to be informed about data processing activities under the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) in the Philippines by following these guidelines:\n\n1. **Notification Prior to Data Processing**: Before any personal data is collected, individuals must be informed whether their personal data will be processed. This includes being notified at the next practical opportunity if the data has already been collected.\n\n2. **Information to be Provided**: The personal information controller (PIC) is required to furnish the following information:\n   - A description of the personal data to be entered into the processing system.\n   - The purposes for which the data will be processed, including any processing for direct marketing, profiling, or statistical purposes.\n   - The legal basis for processing, especially if it is not based on the consent of the data subject.\n   - The scope and method of the personal data processing.\n   - The recipients or classes of recipients to whom the personal data may be disclosed.\n   - Information about any automated processing, including the logic involved and the potential consequences for the data subject.\n   - The identity and contact details of the personal information controller or its representative.\n   - The period for which the information will be stored.\n   - The existence of the rights of data subjects, including the right to access, correct, and object to the processing of their data, as well as the right to lodge a complaint with the National Privacy Commission.\n\n3. **Format of Information**: The information provided must be in a simple and concise manner, presented in an easy-to-understand format to ensure that individuals can comprehend their rights and the implications of data processing.\n\n4. **Right to Access**: Individuals also have the right to request access to their personal data, which includes information about how their data has been processed, the sources of the data, and the recipients of the data.\n\n5. **Right to Object**: If individuals have concerns about how their data is being processed, they can exercise their right to object, particularly if the processing is based on consent or legitimate interest.\n\nBy being proactive and requesting this information, individuals can better understand how their personal data is being handled and assert their rights under the Data Privacy Act.
