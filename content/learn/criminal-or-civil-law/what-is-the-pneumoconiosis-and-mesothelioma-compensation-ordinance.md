question: >-
    What is the Pneumoconiosis and Mesothelioma (Compensation) Ordinance?
answer: >-
    The Pneumoconiosis and Mesothelioma (Compensation) Ordinance, officially designated as Cap. 360, is a legislative framework in Hong Kong aimed at providing compensation to individuals suffering from pneumoconiosis or mesothelioma, or to their family members in cases of incapacity or death resulting from these diseases. \n\n### Key Features of the Ordinance:\n\n1. **Purpose**: The ordinance establishes a compensation scheme specifically for those affected by pneumoconiosis and mesothelioma, which are occupational diseases primarily linked to exposure to harmful dust, such as silica and asbestos.\n\n2. **Eligibility**: To qualify for compensation under this ordinance, individuals must be residents of Hong Kong. There are specific provisions for those who have lived in Hong Kong for five years or more, as well as for those who have resided there for less than five years but contracted the diseases within the territory.\n\n3. **Compensation Items**: The ordinance outlines a total of nine compensation items, which detail the types of benefits available to claimants. These may include lump-sum payments for medical expenses, loss of earnings, and other related costs.\n\n4. **Application Process**: Claimants must follow a prescribed process to apply for compensation, which includes submitting necessary documentation to prove their condition and its connection to occupational exposure.\n\n5. **Legislative Context**: The ordinance is part of a broader legislative structure in Hong Kong that addresses occupational health and safety, ensuring that workers who suffer from specific diseases due to their employment are provided with adequate support and compensation.\n\n6. **Amendments and Updates**: The ordinance has undergone various amendments to improve its provisions and adapt to changing circumstances regarding occupational health.\n\nIn summary, the Pneumoconiosis and Mesothelioma (Compensation) Ordinance serves as a critical legal instrument in Hong Kong, ensuring that individuals affected by these serious occupational diseases receive the necessary compensation and support.
