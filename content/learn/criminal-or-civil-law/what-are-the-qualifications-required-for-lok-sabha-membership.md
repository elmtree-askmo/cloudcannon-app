question: >-
    What are the qualifications required for Lok Sabha membership?
answer: >-
    To become a member of the Lok Sabha, which is the lower house of India's Parliament, an individual must meet specific qualifications as outlined in Article 84 of the Indian Constitution and further detailed in the Representation of the People Act of 1951. The qualifications required for Lok Sabha membership are as follows:\n\n1. **Citizenship**: The candidate must be a citizen of India.\n2. **Age**: The candidate must be at least 25 years of age.\n3. **Voter Registration**: The candidate must be a registered voter in India.\n4. **Legal Standing**: The candidate should not have been convicted of any offense and sentenced to imprisonment for two years or more. Additionally, they should not be declared insolvent by any court or bank.\n\nThese qualifications ensure that the members of the Lok Sabha are representative of the Indian populace and possess the necessary legal and ethical standing to serve in the legislative assembly. The Lok Sabha members are elected directly by the people through general elections held every five years.
