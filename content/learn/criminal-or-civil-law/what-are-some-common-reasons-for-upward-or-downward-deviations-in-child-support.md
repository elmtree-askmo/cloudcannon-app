question: >-
    What are some common reasons for upward or downward deviations in child support?
answer: >-
    Common reasons for upward or downward deviations in child support calculations include:\n\n1. **Extraordinary Medical Expenses**: If a child has significant medical needs that require additional financial support, such as ongoing treatments or therapies, the court may increase the child support obligation to cover these costs.\n\n2. **Educational Costs**: Expenses related to private schooling, tutoring, or special educational programs can lead to upward deviations. If a child requires specialized education that incurs higher costs, the court may adjust the support amount accordingly.\n\n3. **Special Needs of the Child**: Children with disabilities or special needs may require additional resources for care, therapy, or support services, prompting the court to deviate from the standard child support calculation.\n\n4. **Changes in Income**: If a parent experiences a significant increase or decrease in income, this can lead to adjustments in child support. For example, if a parent receives a promotion or a new job with a higher salary, the court may increase the support amount.\n\n5. **Shared Parenting Arrangements**: In cases where parents share custody and responsibilities, the time spent with each parent can affect the child support calculation. If one parent has the child for a greater portion of the time, this may justify a deviation.\n\n6. **Statutory Add-Ons**: Additional expenses such as health insurance premiums, childcare costs, and other necessary expenditures can be included in the child support calculation, leading to upward deviations.\n\n7. **Good Fortune Child Support**: If a parent experiences a windfall or significant financial gain, the court may consider this when determining child support obligations, potentially resulting in an upward adjustment.\n\n8. **Other Unique Circumstances**: Courts may consider other factors specific to the family situation, such as the lifestyle of the child prior to the separation or divorce, which may warrant a deviation from the standard calculation.\n\nThese deviations are assessed on a case-by-case basis, and the court will consider the best interests of the child when making adjustments to child support obligations.
