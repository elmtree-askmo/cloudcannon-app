question: >-
    What are the recent controversies between the judiciary and executive in India?
answer: >-
    Recent controversies between the judiciary and executive in India have been marked by significant tensions regarding the appointment of judges, the interpretation of laws, and the balance of power between these two branches of government.\n\n1. **Judicial Appointments**: A major point of contention has been the National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC). The government has reiterated the need for the NJAC, which would change the current Collegium system of appointing judges. The Collegium system, which has been in place since the early 1990s, allows a group of senior judges to make appointments. The Supreme Court has defended this system, arguing that it ensures judicial independence. The debate intensified when Union Minister Kiren Rijiju stated that the government cannot blindly accept the recommendations made by the Supreme Court collegium for judicial appointments, highlighting the executive's desire for more control over the process.\n\n2. **Executive Overreach**: There have been instances where the Supreme Court has criticized the executive for overstepping its bounds. For example, the Supreme Court emphasized that the executive cannot assume the role of adjudication, particularly in cases involving the arbitrary demolition of homes based on mere accusations. This reflects a broader concern about the separation of powers and the need for the judiciary to act as a check on executive actions.\n\n3. **Legal Interpretations and Rights**: The judiciary has also been involved in significant rulings that challenge executive decisions. For instance, the Supreme Court upheld the application of the Right to Information (RTI) Act to the Directorate of Enforcement (ED) in cases involving human rights and corruption, which the executive has contested. Additionally, the Supreme Court's decisions on matters such as the abrogation of Article 370 and the interpretation of laws related to personal rights have sparked debates about the limits of executive authority.\n\n4. **Historical Context**: The conflict between the judiciary and executive is not new; it has roots dating back to 1973 when the appointment and transfer of judges became contentious. The Kesavananda Bharati case further exemplified the struggle for supremacy over judicial appointments, with the Supreme Court asserting its authority against legislative changes.\n\n5. **Judicial Independence**: Chief Justice of India (CJI) Sanjiv Khanna has stressed the importance of maintaining a degree of separation and autonomy between the judiciary and the executive while also recognizing their interdependence. This reflects ongoing efforts to ensure that the judiciary remains an independent arbiter of justice, free from executive influence.\n\nOverall, the recent controversies highlight a complex and evolving relationship between the judiciary and executive in India, characterized by ongoing debates over judicial appointments, executive overreach, and the fundamental principles of democracy and governance.
