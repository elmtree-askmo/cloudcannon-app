question: >-
    What are primary concerted activities and how do they differ from secondary activities?
answer: >-
    Primary concerted activities refer to actions taken by employees that are directly aimed at improving their working conditions, wages, or other employment-related issues. These activities are protected under the National Labor Relations Act (NLRA) and can include actions such as discussing pay with coworkers, forming a union, or participating in strikes. The key aspect of primary concerted activities is that they involve direct engagement among employees regarding their own workplace conditions and concerns.\n\nIn contrast, secondary activities involve actions that are not directly related to the employees' own workplace but rather target another employer or entity to influence that entity's relationship with the primary employer. For example, secondary activities might include picketing a secondary employer that does business with the primary employer, with the aim of pressuring the primary employer to change its practices. While primary concerted activities are protected under the NLRA, secondary activities can be more complex in terms of legal protections and may not always be covered under the same protections.\n\nIn summary, the primary difference lies in the focus of the activities: primary concerted activities are directly related to the employees' own workplace issues, while secondary activities involve actions that target other employers or entities to influence the primary employer's practices.
