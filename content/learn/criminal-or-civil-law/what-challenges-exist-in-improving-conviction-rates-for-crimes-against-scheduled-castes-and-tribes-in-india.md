question: >-
    What challenges exist in improving conviction rates for crimes against Scheduled Castes and Tribes in India?
answer: >-
    Improving conviction rates for crimes against Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs) in India faces several significant challenges:\n\n1. **Low Conviction Rates**: The national conviction rate for crimes against SCs and STs was reported at 28.5% in 2018, with a decline to 32.4% in 2022 from 39.2% in 2020. This indicates a troubling trend in judicial outcomes, suggesting systemic issues within the legal framework.\n\n2. **Pending Cases**: A staggering 88.5% of cases under the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act remain pending trial from 2009 to 2018. This backlog contributes to delays in justice and can discourage victims from pursuing their cases.\n\n3. **Infrastructure Deficiencies**: There are significant gaps in the judicial infrastructure, with only 194 out of 498 districts in 14 states having established special courts to expedite trials for atrocities against SCs and STs. This lack of dedicated judicial resources hampers the timely processing of cases.\n\n4. **Rising Crime Rates**: The crime rate against SCs and STs has been increasing, with a nearly 50% rise in crimes against Scheduled Tribes from 2012 to 2021. This escalation in violence complicates the legal response and increases the burden on the judicial system.\n\n5. **Geographical Concentration of Cases**: A large proportion of atrocities against SCs and STs are concentrated in just 13 states, which reported 98.91% of all cases in 2022. This concentration can lead to regional disparities in the enforcement of laws and the availability of legal support.\n\n6. **Social Stigma and Fear**: Victims often face social stigma and fear of retaliation, which can deter them from reporting crimes or pursuing legal action. This societal pressure can lead to underreporting of incidents and a lack of cooperation with law enforcement.\n\n7. **Lack of Awareness and Support**: There is often a lack of awareness among victims regarding their rights and the legal provisions available to them. Additionally, inadequate support systems for victims can hinder their ability to navigate the legal process effectively.\n\nThese challenges highlight the need for comprehensive reforms in the legal system, increased resources for special courts, and enhanced support for victims to improve conviction rates for crimes against SCs and STs in India.
