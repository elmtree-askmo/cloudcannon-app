    How does 'D*** in a Box' exemplify absurdity in comedy?
answer: >-
    """D*** in a Box"" exemplifies absurdity in comedy through its outrageous premise and exaggerated presentation. The digital short, featuring Andy Samberg and Justin Timberlake, presents a ludicrous scenario where the duo gifts their romantic partners an inappropriate item in a manner reminiscent of an R&B music video. This juxtaposition of a serious musical style with a ridiculous concept creates a comedic tension that is both surprising and humorous.\nThe use of absurdity is a hallmark of SNL's comedic style, allowing for the exploration of everyday situations taken to ludicrous extremes. In ""D*** in a Box,"" the absurdity is heightened by the over-the-top performances and the catchy, yet outrageous, lyrics. The song's catchy chorus and the elaborate way the characters present their gift serve to amplify the humor, transforming a simple idea into a memorable comedic moment.\nMoreover, the sketch employs comedic techniques such as exaggeration and surprise, which are essential for eliciting laughter. The unexpected nature of the gift, combined with the serious tone of the musical delivery, catches viewers off guard, leading to a strong comedic effect. This blend of absurdity and musicality not only showcases SNL's innovative approach to comedy but also reflects broader trends in contemporary humor, where music and absurdity often intersect to create engaging and memorable content.\nIn summary, ""D*** in a Box"" exemplifies absurdity in comedy by presenting an outrageous premise in a serious format, utilizing exaggeration and surprise to elicit laughter, and showcasing SNL's ability to adapt and innovate within the comedic landscape."
