question: >-
    How did Stuart Smalley become a notable guest host character on SNL?
answer: >-
    "Stuart Smalley, portrayed by Al Franken, became a notable guest host character on ""Saturday Night Live"" (SNL) due to his unique blend of humor and earnestness, which resonated with audiences. The character is a self-help guru who delivers affirmations and motivational advice, famously stating, ""I'm good enough, I'm smart enough, and doggone it, people like me."" This mantra encapsulates the character's essence, combining a comedic take on self-help culture with a sincere approach to personal validation.\nSmalley's segments often featured him addressing various societal issues and personal dilemmas, using humor to provide commentary on the self-help movement and the absurdities of modern life. The character's relatability and the earnest delivery of his affirmations made him a memorable figure in SNL's history. Al Franken's portrayal allowed for a satirical yet affectionate critique of the self-help industry, contributing to the character's popularity and making him a recurring presence on the show.\nOverall, Stuart Smalley's impact on SNL can be attributed to the character's humorous yet heartfelt approach, which effectively captured the zeitgeist of the 1990s self-help culture while providing audiences with both laughter and reflection."
