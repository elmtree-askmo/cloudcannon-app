question: >-
    What are the proposed areas for review in future audit planning?
answer: >-
    In future audit planning, particularly for the year 2025, several proposed areas for review can be identified based on the gathered information. These areas are essential for aligning the internal audit function with the organization's risk profile and control environment. Here are some key thematic areas to consider:\n\n1. **Cybersecurity and Data Security**: Given that cybersecurity remains a top-ranked risk, internal audits should focus on assessing the effectiveness of existing cybersecurity measures and data protection strategies. This includes evaluating the organization's response to emerging threats and ensuring compliance with relevant regulations.\n\n2. **Regulatory and Legislative Changes**: As regulations evolve, internal audits should review compliance with new laws and standards. This area is critical to mitigate risks associated with non-compliance and to ensure that the organization adapts to changing legal landscapes.\n\n3. **Technology and Digital Transformation**: The integration of advanced technologies, such as generative artificial intelligence (AI) and automation, should be a focal point. Auditors need to assess how these technologies are being implemented, their associated risks, and the effectiveness of controls in place to manage these risks.\n\n4. **Strategic Planning and Risk Management**: Internal audits should evaluate the alignment of the audit plan with the organization's strategic goals. This includes assessing how well the organization identifies and manages emerging risks that could impact its objectives.\n\n5. **Stakeholder Engagement and Trust**: Building relationships with key stakeholders is vital. Auditors should review how stakeholder needs are being met and how trust can be fostered through transparent communication and effective audit practices.\n\n6. **Data Analytics and Insights**: The use of data analytics in audits is becoming increasingly important. Future audits should leverage data visualization tools to identify trends and anomalies, enhancing the overall effectiveness of the audit process.\n\n7. **Agility in the Operating Model**: The audit function should be responsive to disruptions and flexible in its approach. This includes reassessing the competencies required for auditors and ensuring that the audit process can adapt to the organization's changing needs.\n\nBy focusing on these areas, internal audit functions can enhance their effectiveness and provide valuable insights that support organizational growth and compliance in a rapidly evolving environment.
