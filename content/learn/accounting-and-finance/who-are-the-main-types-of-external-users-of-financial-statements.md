question: >-
    Who are the main types of external users of financial statements?
answer: >-
    The main types of external users of financial statements include:\n\n1. **Investors**: Individuals or entities that invest or are considering investing in a company. They analyze financial statements to assess the company's profitability, financial health, and potential for future growth.\n\n2. **Creditors**: Banks and other financial institutions that lend money to businesses. They review financial statements to evaluate the creditworthiness of a business and the risk associated with lending.\n\n3. **Regulatory Agencies**: Government bodies that oversee and regulate financial reporting and compliance. They use financial statements to ensure that companies adhere to legal and regulatory requirements.\n\n4. **Analysts**: Financial analysts and market researchers who assess a company's performance and market position. They utilize financial statements to provide insights and recommendations to investors and other stakeholders.\n\n5. **Suppliers**: Companies that provide goods or services to a business may review financial statements to determine the financial stability of their customers before extending credit or entering into contracts.\n\n6. **Customers**: Large customers may analyze financial statements to assess the viability of a supplier, especially if they are entering into long-term contracts.\n\nThese external users rely on financial statements to make informed decisions regarding investments, lending, regulatory compliance, and business relationships.
