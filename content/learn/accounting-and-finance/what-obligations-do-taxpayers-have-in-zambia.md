question: >-
    What obligations do taxpayers have in Zambia?
answer: >-
    In Zambia, taxpayers have several key obligations that they must fulfill to ensure compliance with the tax laws as administered by the Zambia Revenue Authority (ZRA). These obligations include:\n\n1. **Registration**: Taxpayers are required to register with the ZRA in a timely manner. This is essential for obtaining a Tax Identification Number (TIN) and for the proper administration of tax obligations.\n\n2. **Filing Returns**: Taxpayers must file all tax returns within the prescribed time periods. This includes income tax returns, value-added tax (VAT) returns, and any other relevant tax documentation.\n\n3. **Timely Payment of Taxes**: It is crucial for taxpayers to make timely payments of all taxes due. This includes ensuring that any taxes withheld on behalf of others, such as Pay As You Earn (PAYE) for employees, are remitted by the due dates.\n\n4. **Accuracy in Reporting**: Taxpayers have an obligation to declare correct taxes at all times. This means providing complete and accurate information in tax returns and other documents submitted to the ZRA.\n\n5. **Cooperation with ZRA**: Taxpayers are expected to cooperate with ZRA officers whenever required. This may involve providing additional information or documentation during audits or investigations.\n\n6. **Record Keeping**: Maintaining sufficient records for auditing purposes is essential. Taxpayers must keep these records for a minimum period of six years, which allows for proper verification of income and tax liabilities.\n\n7. **Seeking Guidance**: If taxpayers have uncertainties regarding their tax obligations, it is advisable to seek guidance from tax professionals or the ZRA to avoid potential penalties and legal consequences.\n\nFailure to comply with these obligations can result in severe penalties and interest charges, emphasizing the importance of understanding and fulfilling tax responsibilities in Zambia.
