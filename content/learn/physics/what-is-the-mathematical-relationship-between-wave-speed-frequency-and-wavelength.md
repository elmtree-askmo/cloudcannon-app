question: >-
    What is the mathematical relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength?
answer: >-
    The mathematical relationship between wave speed, frequency, and wavelength is encapsulated in the wave equation, which can be expressed as:\n\n$$\nv = f \cdot \lambda\n$$\n\nWhere:\n- \( v \) is the wave speed,\n- \( f \) is the frequency of the wave, and\n- \( \lambda \) is the wavelength.\n\nThis equation indicates that the speed of a wave is equal to the product of its frequency and wavelength. \n\nFor electromagnetic waves, such as light in a vacuum, the wave speed is the speed of light, denoted as \( c \). Therefore, for electromagnetic radiation, the equation can be specifically written as:\n\n$$\nc = f \cdot \lambda\n$$\n\nIn this context, the frequency \( f \) is measured in hertz (Hz), and the wavelength \( \lambda \) is typically measured in meters (m). \n\nIt's important to note that wavelength and frequency are inversely proportional; as the wavelength increases, the frequency decreases, and vice versa. This relationship is crucial in understanding various phenomena in wave physics, including the behavior of electromagnetic waves across the electromagnetic spectrum. \n\nIn summary, the wave equation \( v = f \cdot \lambda \) serves as a fundamental principle in wave mechanics, linking the three key properties of waves: speed, frequency, and wavelength.
