question: >-
    How is kinetic energy calculated, and what factors affect it?
answer: >-
    Kinetic energy (KE) is the energy that an object possesses due to its motion. It is a fundamental concept in physics and is calculated using the formula:\n\n$$\nKE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2\n$$\n\nwhere:\n- $ KE $ is the kinetic energy (measured in joules),\n- $ m $ is the mass of the object (measured in kilograms),\n- $ v $ is the velocity of the object (measured in meters per second).\n\n### Factors Affecting Kinetic Energy\n\nThe kinetic energy of an object is influenced by two primary factors:\n\n1. **Mass (m)**: The greater the mass of the object, the more kinetic energy it will have at a given velocity. This relationship is linear; if the mass doubles, the kinetic energy also doubles, assuming the velocity remains constant.\n\n2. **Velocity (v)**: The velocity of the object has a more significant impact on kinetic energy because it is squared in the formula. This means that if the velocity of an object doubles, its kinetic energy increases by a factor of four (since \( (2v)^2 = 4v^2 \)). Thus, changes in velocity have a quadratic effect on kinetic energy.\n\n### Summary\n\nIn summary, kinetic energy is calculated using the formula $ KE = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 $, and it is directly proportional to the mass of the object and the square of its velocity. Understanding these relationships is crucial for solving problems related to motion and energy in physics.
