question: >-
    What is the equation for pressure and what are its SI units?
answer: >-
    The equation for pressure is defined as the total force applied divided by the area over which the force is applied. Mathematically, it is expressed as:\n\n$$\nP = \frac{F}{A}\n$$\n\nwhere:\n- $ P $ is the pressure,\n- $ F $ is the force applied, and\n- $ A $ is the area over which the force is applied.\n\nThe SI unit of pressure is the Pascal (Pa), which is equivalent to one Newton per square meter (N/m²). For example, if a force of 100 N is applied over a surface area of 0.5 m², the pressure exerted on that surface would be calculated as follows:\n\n$$\nP = \frac{100 \text{ N}}{0.5 \text{ m}^2} = 200 \, \text{Pa}\n$$\n\nThis relationship is fundamental in various fields, including biomechanics, where understanding pressure is crucial for analyzing forces acting on the body during movement and balance.
