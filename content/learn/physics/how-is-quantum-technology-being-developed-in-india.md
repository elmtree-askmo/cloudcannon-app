question: >-
    How is quantum technology being developed in India?
answer: >-
    India is making significant strides in the development of quantum technology through a coordinated effort involving government initiatives, academic research, and private sector collaboration. The cornerstone of this effort is the National Quantum Mission (NQM), which was approved by the Union Cabinet, chaired by Prime Minister Narendra Modi. This mission aims to seed, nurture, and scale up scientific and industrial research and development in quantum technologies, thereby creating a vibrant and innovative ecosystem.\n\nKey aspects of India's quantum technology development include:\n\n1. **National Quantum Mission (NQM)**: The NQM is a major initiative by the Department of Science and Technology (DST) with an estimated budget of â‚¹6,003 crore (approximately $720 million) over eight years. The mission focuses on developing intermediate-scale quantum computers with 50-1000 physical qubits using various platforms, including superconducting and photonic technologies. It also aims to establish satellite-based secure quantum communications over distances of up to 2000 kilometers within India.\n\n2. **Research and Development**: The NQM is complemented by the QuEST (Quantum Enabled Science and Technology) research program, which has allocated a budget of $11.2 million (800 million Indian rupees) over three years to drive quantum technology research. This funding is intended to support significant advancements in quantum science and technology.\n\n3. **Ecosystem Development**: India is actively building a quantum ecosystem that includes over 100 research universities, corporate initiatives, and specialized T-Hubs that foster innovation in quantum technology. This ecosystem is designed to create a quantum-ready workforce and secure India's leadership position in the global quantum technology landscape.\n\n4. **Collaboration and Innovation**: The mission encourages collaboration between various stakeholders, including academic institutions like the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), IIT Madras, and the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR), as well as private sector companies such as Infosys and Tata Consultancy Services. This collaborative approach is expected to enhance research initiatives and drive comprehensive growth in quantum technology development.\n\n5. **Future Prospects**: The NQM is set to ensure sustained growth and development in quantum technologies, positioning India alongside other leading nations in this field. The government is also planning to support startups in quantum technologies, further showcasing India's commitment to advancing this critical area of research.\n\nIn summary, India's quantum technology development is characterized by a robust national strategy, significant government investment, and a collaborative ecosystem that aims to propel the country to the forefront of quantum research and applications.
