question: >-
    Can you provide a brief biography of Ryan Cayabyab?
answer: >-
    Ryan Cayabyab, born Raymundo Cipriano Pujante Cayabyab on May 4, 1954, is a prominent Filipino musician, composer, and conductor, widely regarded as one of the pillars of contemporary music in the Philippines. He graduated from the University of the Philippines College of Music with a bachelor's degree in music composition and later taught music theory and composition at the same institution for nearly two decades.\n\nCayabyab's career began in the 1970s, and he quickly made a name for himself in the Philippine music scene. His composition "Kay Ganda ng Ating Musika," created in 1978, became an anthem for the Original Pilipino Music (OPM) movement, significantly shaping the landscape of Filipino music. Over the years, he has composed a diverse array of works, including full-length ballets, theater musicals, choral pieces, orchestral works, and popular songs, showcasing his versatility across various musical genres.\n\nIn addition to his compositions, Cayabyab served as the Executive and Artistic Director of the San Miguel Foundation for the Performing Arts and was a resident judge on the first season of Philippine Idol in 2006. His contributions to music have earned him numerous accolades, including the prestigious title of National Artist for Music in 2018, recognizing his significant impact on Philippine musical arts.\n\nCayabyab's influence extends beyond the Philippines, as he has performed internationally at renowned venues such as Carnegie Hall and the Kennedy Center. His works have been instrumental in promoting Filipino music on the global stage, and he continues to inspire new generations of musicians and composers.\n\nIn 2019, he was honored with the Ramon Magsaysay Award, acknowledging his ability to unify people through music and instill pride in Filipino culture. With a legacy that spans close to 50 years, Ryan Cayabyab remains a vital figure in the evolution of contemporary music in the Philippines.
