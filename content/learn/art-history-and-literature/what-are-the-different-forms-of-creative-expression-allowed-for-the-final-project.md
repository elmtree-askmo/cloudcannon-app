question: >-
    What are the different forms of creative expression allowed for the final project?
answer: >-
    For the final project, various forms of creative expression are allowed, enabling students to showcase their understanding and analysis of human creativity. Some of the accepted forms include:\n\n1. **Visual Arts**: This can encompass painting, drawing, sculpture, and other craft projects.\n2. **Performing Arts**: Students may choose to express themselves through acting, dancing, or musical performances, such as a musical arrangement or composition.\n3. **Literary Works**: Writing poetry, essays, or other literary compositions is encouraged.\n4. **Multimedia Projects**: This includes creating videos, digital storytelling, or other audiovisual works that demonstrate the student's learning and creative expression.\n5. **Instructional Units**: Projects that involve creating educational materials or units in subjects like science or social science are also permissible.\n\nThe final project requires students to analyze a chosen work of creative expression, describe its purpose, and assess its impact on and by surrounding situations. This analysis should also explore the relationship between culture and expression, as well as how values shape and are shaped by creative works. \n\nOverall, the project aims to encourage students to engage deeply with their chosen form of expression while reflecting on its broader implications.
