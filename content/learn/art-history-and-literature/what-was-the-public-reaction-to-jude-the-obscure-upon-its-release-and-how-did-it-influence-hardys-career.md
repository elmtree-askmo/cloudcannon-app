question: >-
    What was the public reaction to 'Jude the Obscure' upon its release, and how did it influence Hardy's career?
answer: >-
    Upon its release in 1895, Thomas Hardy's "Jude the Obscure" elicited a strong public reaction characterized by shock and outrage. The novel's candid portrayal of sexuality and its critical examination of marriage and societal norms were particularly controversial. Critics were taken aback by the themes presented in the narrative, which challenged the rigid social structures of Victorian society. This backlash was so intense that it led Hardy to withdraw from writing novels altogether, marking "Jude the Obscure" as his last work in that genre.\n\nThe initial outrage surrounding the novel has since diminished, and "Jude the Obscure" is now recognized as a significant contribution to English literature. Its themes of social mobility, individual aspiration, and critique of institutional power resonate with contemporary audiences, influencing modern writers and thinkers. Hardy's exploration of these themes has solidified his legacy, as his work continues to be studied for its profound insights into human nature and societal constraints.\n\nIn summary, the public reaction to "Jude the Obscure" was one of significant backlash, which ultimately influenced Hardy's decision to cease writing novels, while the novel itself has since gained recognition as a critical work in the literary canon.
