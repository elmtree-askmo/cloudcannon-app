question: >-
    What are some notable works of Francisco Santiago?
answer: >-
    Francisco Santiago, often referred to as the 'Father of Kundiman,' made significant contributions to Filipino music, particularly in the genre of kundiman, which are traditional Tagalog-language art songs. Some of his most notable works include:\n\n1. **"Nasaan Ka Irog"** - This piece is one of Santiago's most famous kundiman songs and has become a classic in the Filipino music repertoire.\n2. **"Mutya ng Pasig"** - Another well-known kundiman, this song is celebrated for its lyrical beauty and emotional depth.\n3. **"Kundiman (Anak-Dalita)"** - This piece gained international recognition when it was performed at the Royal Court of Spain in 1917 at the request of King Alfonso XIII.\n4. **Piano Concerto** - Generally recognized as his best work, this composition showcases his skills as a composer in the classical genre.\n5. **"Philippine Overture"** - A significant orchestral work that reflects his ability to blend traditional Filipino themes with classical music forms.\n6. **"Sonata Filipina in D flat Major"** - Composed in 1922, this piece is another example of his contributions to classical music.\n7. **"Ave Maria"** - Santiago composed a version of this well-known piece for high voice with violin obligato, demonstrating his versatility as a composer.\n\nIn addition to these works, Santiago's catalog includes many other compositions, although a significant number of them were lost or destroyed during World War II. His legacy continues to influence contemporary Filipino music and composers.
