question: >-
    Who are the major Olympian deities in Greek mythology, and what are their primary domains?
answer: >-
    In Greek mythology, the major Olympian deities are a group of twelve principal gods who reside on Mount Olympus. Each of these gods has specific domains, attributes, and myths associated with them. Here is a list of the major Olympian gods along with their primary domains:\n\n1. **Zeus**: The king of the gods and ruler of Mount Olympus. He is the god of the sky, lightning, and thunder. Zeus is often associated with law, order, and justice.\n\n2. **Hera**: The queen of the gods and the goddess of marriage and family. She is known for her jealous and vengeful nature, particularly towards Zeus's lovers and offspring.\n\n3. **Poseidon**: The god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses. Poseidon is often depicted with a trident and is known for his temperamental nature.\n\n4. **Demeter**: The goddess of agriculture, fertility, and the harvest. She is closely associated with the growth of crops and the changing of the seasons.\n\n5. **Athena**: The goddess of wisdom, warfare, and crafts. Athena is known for her strategic skill in battle and is often depicted with an owl and a shield.\n\n6. **Apollo**: The god of the sun, music, poetry, prophecy, and healing. Apollo is associated with the arts and is often depicted with a lyre.\n\n7. **Artemis**: The goddess of the hunt, wilderness, and childbirth. She is also associated with the moon and is often depicted with a bow and arrows.\n\n8. **Ares**: The god of war and violence. Ares represents the chaotic and brutal aspects of warfare and is often depicted in armor.\n\n9. **Aphrodite**: The goddess of love, beauty, and desire. She is often associated with romantic attraction and is depicted as a beautiful woman.\n\n10. **Hephaestus**: The god of fire, metalworking, and craftsmanship. He is known for his skill in creating weapons and armor for the gods and heroes.\n\n11. **Hermes**: The messenger of the gods and the god of commerce, thieves, and travelers. Hermes is known for his speed and cunning, often depicted with winged sandals.\n\n12. **Hestia**: The goddess of the hearth, home, and domesticity. Hestia represents the warmth and stability of the home and is often associated with family and community.\n\nThese Olympian gods play a central role in many Greek myths and are often depicted in various forms of art and literature, symbolizing different aspects of life and nature. Their stories and relationships are integral to the understanding of ancient Greek culture and religion.
