question: >-
    Identify one key characteristic of William Wordsworth's writing style.
answer: >-
    One key characteristic of William Wordsworth's writing style is his emphasis on the beauty of nature and the importance of personal experience. Wordsworth often explores the transformative power of nature, as seen in his works like "Lines Composed a Few Miles Above Tintern Abbey." In this poem, he reflects on how nature influences human emotions and memories, illustrating his belief that personal experiences in the natural world can lead to profound insights and emotional growth. This focus on the connection between nature and individual experience is a hallmark of his Romantic style, highlighting the significance of the imagination and the subjective experience of the individual.
