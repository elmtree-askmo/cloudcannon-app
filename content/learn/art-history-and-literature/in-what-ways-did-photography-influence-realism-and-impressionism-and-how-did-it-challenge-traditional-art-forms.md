The advent of photography provided a new means of capturing reality, which Realist artists sought to emulate. They aimed to depict the world as they experienced it, often highlighting the struggles of the working class. This alignment with photography's objective nature allowed Realist artists to present their subjects with a sense of authenticity that was previously unattainable in painting.\n\n2. **Naturalism vs. Realism**: The relationship between photography and naturalism is notable. Naturalist painters aimed for lifelike representations, paralleling the capabilities of photography. However, Realism diverged by emphasizing the artist's subjective experience of reality, rather than merely replicating it.
