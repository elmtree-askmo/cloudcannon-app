question: >-
    What is the definition of a cumulative distribution function?
answer: >-
    The Cumulative Distribution Function (CDF) of a random variable is a fundamental concept in probability theory and statistics. It is defined as a function that provides the probability that the random variable takes a value less than or equal to a specific value $ x $. Mathematically, it is expressed as:\n\n$$\nF(x) = P(X \leq x)\n$$\n\nwhere \( F(x) \) is the CDF evaluated at $ x $, and \( P(X \leq x) \) represents the probability that the random variable $ X $ is less than or equal to $ x $.\n\nThe CDF is a cumulative function, meaning it sums the probabilities of all outcomes up to the point $ x $. Its output ranges from 0 to 1, where:\n\n- \( F(x) = 0 \) indicates that the probability of the random variable being less than or equal to $ x $ is zero.\n- \( F(x) = 1 \) indicates that the probability of the random variable being less than or equal to $ x $ is certain.\n\nThe CDF can be defined for any type of random variable, including discrete, continuous, and mixed types. For continuous random variables, the CDF is obtained by integrating the probability density function (PDF) \( f(x) \):\n\n$$\nF(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{x} f(t) \, dt\n$$\n\nIn summary, the CDF is a crucial tool for understanding the distribution of random variables and is widely used in statistical analysis and data interpretation.
