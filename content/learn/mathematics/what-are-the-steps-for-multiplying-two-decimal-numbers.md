question: >-
    What are the steps for multiplying two decimal numbers?
answer: >-
    To multiply two decimal numbers, you can follow these steps:\n\n1. **Ignore the Decimals**: Temporarily ignore the decimal points in both numbers and treat them as whole numbers. For example, if you are multiplying $0.8$ and $0.4$, you would consider them as $8$ and $4$.\n\n2. **Multiply the Numbers**: Perform the multiplication as you would with whole numbers. Continuing the example, you would calculate $8 \times 4 = 32$.\n\n3. **Count the Decimal Places**: Count the total number of decimal places in both of the original decimal numbers. In our example, $0.8$ has 1 decimal place and $0.4$ has 1 decimal place, giving a total of $1 + 1 = 2$ decimal places.\n\n4. **Place the Decimal Point**: In the product obtained from the multiplication, place the decimal point from the right end according to the total number of decimal places counted in the previous step. For our example, you would place the decimal point two places from the right in $32$, resulting in $0.32$.\n\nThus, the product of $0.8$ and $0.4$ is $0.32$.\n\nThis method can be applied to any multiplication involving decimal numbers, ensuring that you account for the decimal places correctly to arrive at the accurate result.
