question: >-
    Describe the cyclic pattern observed in the powers of i.
answer: >-
    The powers of the imaginary unit $ i $ exhibit a distinct cyclic pattern that is fundamental in complex number operations. The sequence of powers of $ i $ is as follows:\n\n- $ i^1 = i $\n- $ i^2 = -1 $\n- $ i^3 = -i $\n- $ i^4 = 1 $\n\nThis cycle repeats every four powers. Therefore, for any integer $ n $, the value of $ i^n $ can be determined by finding the remainder of $ n $ when divided by 4. The results based on the remainder are:\n\n- If $ n \equiv 0 \mod 4 $, then $ i^n = 1 $\n- If $ n \equiv 1 \mod 4 $, then $ i^n = i $\n- If $ n \equiv 2 \mod 4 $, then $ i^n = -1 $\n- If $ n \equiv 3 \mod 4 $, then $ i^n = -i $\n\nFor example:\n- $ i^0 = 1 $\n- $ i^4 = 1 $\n- $ i^8 = 1 $\n\nThis cyclic nature is crucial for simplifying expressions involving higher powers of $ i $ and is a key concept in complex number arithmetic. Understanding this pattern allows for efficient calculations and simplifications in various mathematical contexts, particularly in polynomial operations and when dealing with imaginary numbers.
