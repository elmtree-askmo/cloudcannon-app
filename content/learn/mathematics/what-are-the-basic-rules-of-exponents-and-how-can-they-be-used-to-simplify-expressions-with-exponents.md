    What are the basic rules of exponents, and how can they be used to simplify expressions with exponents?

Hereâ€™s a detailed overview of these rules and how they can be applied:\n\n### Basic Rules of Exponents\n\n1. **Product Rule**: When multiplying expressions with the same base, you add the exponents.\n   - Formula: $ B^m \cdot B^n = B^{m+n} $\n   - Example: $ x^2 \cdot x^5 = x^{2+5} = x^7 $\n\n2. **Quotient Rule**: When dividing expressions with the same base, you subtract the exponents.\n   - Formula: $ \frac{B^m}{B^n} = B^{m-n} $\n   - Example: $ \frac{x^5}{x^2} = x^{5-2} = x^3 $\n\n3. **Power Rule**: When raising an expression to another power, you multiply the exponents.\n   - Formula: \( (B^m)^n = B^{m \cdot n} \)\n   - Example: \( (x^2)^3 = x^{2 \cdot 3} = x^6 \)\n\n4. **Zero Exponent Rule**: Any non-zero base raised to the power of zero equals one.\n   - Formula: $ B^0 = 1 $ (for $ B \neq 0 $)\n   - Example: $ 5^0 = 1 $\n\n5. **Negative Exponents**: A negative exponent indicates the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive exponent.\n   - Formula: $ B^{-n} = \frac{1}{B^n} $\n   - Example: $ x^{-3} = \frac{1}{x^3} $\n\n6. **Distributing Exponents**: When raising a product to a power, you can distribute the exponent to each factor.\n   - Formula: \( (AB)^n = A^n \cdot B^n \)\n   - Example: \( (xy)^2 = x^2 \cdot y^2 \)\n\n### Simplifying Expressions with Exponents\n\nTo simplify expressions using these rules, follow these steps:\n\n1. **Identify the Base**: Ensure that the bases are the same when applying the product or quotient rules.\n2. **Apply the Appropriate Rule**: Use the product, quotient, or power rule as needed.\n3. **Combine Like Terms**: If applicable, combine terms to reduce the expression further.\n4. **Convert Negative Exponents**: If negative exponents are present, convert them to positive by taking the reciprocal.\n5. **Use the Zero Exponent Rule**: Replace any base raised to zero with one.\n\n### Example of Simplification\n\nConsider the expression $ \frac{x^4 \cdot x^{-2}}{x^3} $:\n\n1. Apply the product rule in the numerator: \( x^4 \cdot x^{-2} = x^{4 + (-2)} = x^2 \).\n2. Now, apply the quotient rule: $ \frac{x^2}{x^3} = x^{2 - 3} = x^{-1} $.\n3. Convert the negative exponent: $ x^{-1} = \frac{1}{x} $.\n\nBy following these rules and steps, you can effectively simplify expressions involving exponents.
