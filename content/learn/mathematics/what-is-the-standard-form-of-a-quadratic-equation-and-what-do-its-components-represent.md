question: >-
    What is the standard form of a quadratic equation and what do its components represent?
answer: >-
    The standard form of a quadratic equation is given by:\n\n$$\ny = ax^2 + bx + c\n$$\n\nwhere:\n- $ a $, $ b $, and $ c $ are constants,\n- $ a \neq 0 $ (if $ a = 0 $, the equation is linear, not quadratic).\n\n### Components of the Standard Form:\n\n1. **Coefficient $ a $**:\n   - Determines the direction of the parabola:\n     - If $ a > 0 $, the parabola opens upwards.\n     - If $ a < 0 $, the parabola opens downwards.\n   - Affects the width of the parabola:\n     - Larger absolute values of $ a $ (e.g., $ |a| > 1 $) make the parabola narrower.\n     - Smaller absolute values of $ a $ (e.g., $ |a| < 1 $) make it wider.\n\n2. **Coefficient $ b $**:\n   - Influences the position of the vertex along the x-axis.\n   - It is part of the formula used to find the x-coordinate of the vertex, which is given by $ x = -\frac{b}{2a} $.\n\n3. **Constant $ c $**:\n   - Represents the y-intercept of the parabola, which is the point where the graph intersects the y-axis (when $ x = 0 $).\n   - It provides a vertical shift of the parabola.\n\n### Example:\nFor the quadratic equation $ y = -x^2 + 4x - 3 $:\n- $ a = -1 $ (the parabola opens downwards),\n- $ b = 4 $ (affects the vertex's x-coordinate),\n- $ c = -3 $ (the y-intercept is at \( (0, -3) \)).\n\nUnderstanding these components is crucial for graphing quadratic functions, finding the vertex, and determining the axis of symmetry, which is given by the line $ x = -\frac{b}{2a} $.
