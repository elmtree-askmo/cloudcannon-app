question: >-
    How does a vertical shift affect the graph of a function?
answer: >-
    A vertical shift affects the graph of a function by moving it up or down along the vertical (y) axis without altering its shape. This transformation is achieved by adding or subtracting a constant value from the function. Hereâ€™s a detailed explanation of how vertical shifts work and their effects:\n\n### Definition of Vertical Shifts\n- **Upward Shift**: When a positive constant $ c $ is added to the function, represented as \( f(x) + c \). This shifts the entire graph upwards by $ c $ units. For example, if we take the quadratic function $ y = x^2 $ and transform it to $ y = x^2 + 4 $, the graph shifts upward by 4 units, moving the vertex from (0,0) to (0,4).\n  \n- **Downward Shift**: When a positive constant $ c $ is subtracted from the function, represented as \( f(x) - c \). This shifts the graph downwards by $ c $ units. For instance, transforming $ y = x^2 $ to $ y = x^2 - 3 $ shifts the graph down by 3 units, relocating the vertex to (0,-3).\n\n### Effects on the Graph\n1. **Preservation of Shape**: Vertical shifts do not change the shape of the graph; they only change its vertical position. This means that the characteristics of the function, such as its curvature and intercepts (other than the y-intercept), remain the same.\n\n2. **Impact on Domain and Range**: The domain of the function remains unchanged, as vertical shifts do not affect the input values. However, the range of the function is altered. For example, if the original function has a range of \( [0, \infty) \) and is shifted upward by 2 units, the new range becomes \( [2, \infty) \).\n\n3. **Graphing Techniques**: When graphing functions, understanding vertical shifts allows for easier manipulation and visualization of the function's behavior. For example, if you know the graph of a parent function, you can quickly determine the new position of the graph after applying a vertical shift.\n\n### Combined Transformations\nVertical shifts can be combined with other transformations, such as horizontal shifts, reflections, and stretches. For instance, the function $ y = \sqrt{x - 3} + 2 $ represents a graph that is shifted 3 units to the right and 2 units up from the parent function $ y = \sqrt{x} $.\n\nIn summary, vertical shifts are a fundamental transformation that allows for the adjustment of a function's graph along the vertical axis while maintaining its overall shape and characteristics. Understanding these shifts is crucial for effectively analyzing and graphing functions in various mathematical contexts.
