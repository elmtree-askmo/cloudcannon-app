This means that the image cannot be projected onto a screen because it appears to be located behind the mirror. The virtual image is formed by the divergence of light rays that reflect off the mirror.\n\n3. **Properties of the Mirror Image**:\n   - **Size and Shape**: The image created by a plane mirror is always the same size and shape as the object being reflected. For example, if a 2-foot tall object is placed 5 meters in front of the mirror, the image will also appear to be 2 feet tall and located 5 meters behind the mirror.\n   - **Upright Orientation**: The image appears upright, meaning it maintains the same orientation as the object. However, it undergoes a phenomenon known as lateral inversion.\n   - **Lateral Inversion**: This is a key characteristic of mirror images. When an object is reflected in a mirror, the left and right sides are reversed. For instance, if a person raises their right hand in front of a mirror, the reflection will show the left hand raised. This effect occurs because the light rays reflecting off the mirror cross over, leading to the apparent reversal of sides.\n\n4. **Law of Reflection**: The behavior of light during reflection follows the law of reflection, which states that the angle of incidence (the angle at which incoming light strikes the mirror) is equal to the angle of reflection (the angle at which light reflects off the mirror). This principle is crucial in understanding how images are formed.\n\n5. **Formation of the Image**: The image is formed by light rays that originate from various points on the object. When these rays hit the mirror, they reflect according to the law of reflection, creating an image that appears to be behind the mirror. The distance of the image from the mirror is equal to the distance of the object from the mirror.\n\n6. **Applications and Examples**: The concept of mirror images is not only important in geometry but also has practical applications in everyday life, such as in the design of optical devices, safety mirrors, and even in art and photography.\n\nIn summary, the mirror image concept in reflections involves the creation of a virtual image that is the same size and shape as the object, appears upright, and exhibits lateral inversion.
