    What were the geographic locations of major Celtic tribes and their natural resources?

The island was also known for its abundant natural resources, including bog iron and limestone.\n\n4. **Celtiberians**:\n   - **Location**: Resided in the central region of the Iberian Peninsula (modern Spain).\n   - **Natural Resources**: The Celtiberians had access to fertile plains for agriculture, as well as mineral resources, including iron and silver, which were important for their economy and trade.\n\n5. **Boii**:\n   - **Location**: Originally located in what is now the Czech Republic and parts of Austria and Hungary.\n   - **Natural Resources**: The Boii had access to fertile agricultural land and were known for their metalworking skills, particularly in iron and bronze.\n\n6. **Galatians**:\n   - **Location**: Settled in central Anatolia (modern Turkey) after migrating from Europe.\n   - **Natural Resources**: The Galatians had access to rich agricultural land and were involved in trade with neighboring cultures, benefiting from the region's diverse resources.\n\n7. **Belgae**:\n   - **Location**: Inhabited northern Gaul (modern Belgium and parts of France).\n   - **Natural Resources**: The Belgae had access to fertile agricultural land and were known for their warrior culture, utilizing the region's resources for weaponry and trade.\n\nThe Celts established extensive trade networks that connected them with Mediterranean cultures, facilitating the exchange of goods, ideas, and technologies. The desire for trade and resources often brought the Celts into conflict with neighboring cultures, including the Romans.\n\nIn summary, the geographic distribution of Celtic tribes was closely tied to the natural resources available in their respective regions, which played a significant role in their economic activities, trade, and interactions with other cultures.
