question: >-
    What services are included in the Trauma Counseling and Food Security Program?
answer: >-
    The Trauma Counseling and Food Security Program, part of the services offered by the North York Women's Shelter (NYWS) and the Anne Marie D'Amico Community Collective (AMDCC), includes several key components aimed at supporting women and children who have experienced violence. \n\n1. **Trauma Counseling Services**: This aspect of the program provides psychological support to help survivors process their experiences and heal from trauma. The counseling is delivered in a trauma-informed manner, ensuring that the needs and sensitivities of the individuals are prioritized.\n\n2. **Food Security Initiatives**: The program addresses food justice and security, recognizing the importance of access to nutritious food for overall well-being. This includes providing meals and resources to ensure that women and children have adequate nutrition, which is crucial for their physical and mental health.\n\n3. **Expressive Arts Therapy**: This therapeutic approach allows participants to express their feelings and experiences through creative outlets, which can be particularly beneficial for those who may find it difficult to articulate their trauma verbally.\n\n4. **Holistic Support**: The program adopts a holistic approach to address the interconnected issues of trauma and food insecurity, acknowledging how these factors impact the health and well-being of individuals.\n\nOverall, the Trauma Counseling and Food Security Program is designed to empower women and children, helping them rebuild their lives free from violence while ensuring they have access to essential resources and support.
