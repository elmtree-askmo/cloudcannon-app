question: >-
    What are the key differences between Internet Gaming Disorder (IGD) and Gaming Disorder (GD)?
answer: >-
    The key differences between Internet Gaming Disorder (IGD) and Gaming Disorder (GD) primarily stem from their definitions, diagnostic criteria, and the frameworks in which they are recognized.\n\n1. **Definition and Framework**:\n   - **Internet Gaming Disorder (IGD)**: This term is used in the fifth edition of the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM-5) and is considered a condition requiring further research. It is characterized by a persistent and compulsive pattern of gaming behavior that leads to significant impairment or distress. The DSM-5 outlines specific criteria for diagnosis, which include symptoms such as preoccupation with games, withdrawal symptoms, and loss of interest in other activities.\n   - **Gaming Disorder (GD)**: This term is defined in the 11th Revision of the International Classification of Diseases (ICD-11) by the World Health Organization (WHO). GD is characterized by a pattern of gaming behavior (digital or video gaming) that includes impaired control over gaming, prioritization of gaming over other activities, and continuation of gaming despite negative consequences. The ICD-11 allows for a diagnosis to be made sooner than 12 months if symptoms are severe.\n\n2. **Diagnostic Criteria**:\n   - The DSM-5 proposes nine diagnostic criteria for IGD, which must be met for a diagnosis. These criteria focus on the behavioral aspects of gaming and the resulting negative impacts on daily life.\n   - The ICD-11's criteria for GD emphasize the behavioral pattern and its impact on daily functioning, with a more straightforward approach to diagnosis that can be made based on the severity of symptoms.\n\n3. **Recognition and Research Status**:\n   - IGD is still considered a tentative diagnosis in the DSM-5, indicating that more research is needed to fully understand its implications and treatment. It is included in a section recommending conditions for further study.\n   - GD, on the other hand, is officially recognized in the ICD-11 as a mental health disorder, reflecting a more established understanding of the condition and its implications for treatment and public health.\n\n4. **Treatment Approaches**:\n   - Treatment strategies for IGD often involve cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and other psychotherapeutic approaches aimed at helping individuals manage their gaming habits and address any underlying mental health issues.\n   - GD treatment may also include similar therapeutic approaches, but given its recognition in the ICD-11, there may be more structured treatment protocols and resources available for individuals diagnosed with GD.\n\nIn summary, while both IGD and GD share similarities in their focus on problematic gaming behaviors, they differ in their definitions, diagnostic criteria, and the frameworks in which they are recognized. IGD is still under research in the DSM-5, whereas GD is formally recognized in the ICD-11, which may influence the approach to diagnosis and treatment.
