question: >-
    What types of child care and youth programs are available at the Red Door Family Shelter?
answer: >-
    The Red Door Family Shelter offers a variety of child care and youth programs designed to support the emotional and psychological well-being of children and youth. These programs focus on helping children cope with trauma and build resilience, ensuring they can lead fulfilling lives despite the challenges they may face.\n\nKey aspects of the child care and youth programs at the Red Door Family Shelter include:\n\n1. **Emotional and Psychological Support**: The programs prioritize helping children heal from trauma, providing a safe environment where they can express themselves and develop coping strategies.\n\n2. **Academic Support**: The shelter offers educational support to help children and youth flourish academically, ensuring they have the resources they need to succeed in school.\n\n3. **Social Skills Development**: Programs are designed to help children make friends and build social connections, which are crucial for their overall development and resilience.\n\n4. **Specialized Youth Programs**: There are specific initiatives aimed at supporting children and teens dealing with a parent's cancer diagnosis, treatment, or loss. This program addresses the unique emotional needs of these young individuals.\n\n5. **Fun and Safe Environment**: The shelter provides a fun and safe space for children during what can be an unsettled time in their lives, allowing them to engage in activities that promote joy and stability.\n\nOverall, the Red Door Family Shelter's child care and youth programs are comprehensive, focusing on the holistic development of children and youth in vulnerable situations. For more information, individuals can contact the shelter at 21 Carlaw Avenue, Toronto, ON M4M 2R6, or by phone at 416-915-5671.
