question: >-
    What are the signs of the exhaustion phase in GAS?
answer: >-
    The exhaustion phase of Hans Selye's General Adaptation Syndrome (GAS) is characterized by a range of signs and symptoms that indicate the body's resources have been depleted due to prolonged exposure to stress. Key signs of the exhaustion phase include:\n\n1. **Extreme Fatigue**: A profound sense of tiredness that does not improve with rest.\n2. **Pain**: Increased physical discomfort, which may manifest as headaches, muscle pain, or other bodily aches.\n3. **Depression**: Feelings of sadness or hopelessness that can significantly impact daily functioning.\n4. **Residual Anxiety**: Ongoing feelings of anxiety that persist even in the absence of immediate stressors.\n5. **Cognitive Dysfunction**: This includes symptoms such as "brain fog," short-term memory loss, and difficulty concentrating.\n6. **Weight Gain and Blood Sugar Imbalance**: Changes in metabolism that can lead to weight gain and fluctuations in blood sugar levels.\n7. **Exercise Intolerance**: A decreased ability to engage in physical activity due to fatigue and lack of energy.\n\nIn this phase, the body's ability to cope with stress is significantly diminished, and symptoms from earlier stages may reappear. Recognizing these signs early is crucial for preventing long-term physical and psychological damage. Implementing effective stress management strategies can help mitigate the effects of this exhaustion phase.
