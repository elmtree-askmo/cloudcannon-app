question: >-
    What are the key developmental stages in the first and second week of development?
answer: >-
    The key developmental stages in the first and second week of human development are primarily characterized by the germinal stage, which encompasses the period from fertilization to implantation.\n\n1. **Fertilization (Day 0)**: The process begins when a sperm penetrates an egg, resulting in the formation of a zygote. This typically occurs within 12 to 24 hours after ovulation.\n\n2. **Cell Division (Days 1-3)**: The zygote undergoes a series of mitotic divisions as it travels down the fallopian tube towards the uterus. This process is known as cleavage, resulting in a multicellular structure called a morula by around day 3.\n\n3. **Blastocyst Formation (Days 4-5)**: By the fourth day, the morula develops into a blastocyst, which consists of an inner cell mass (that will eventually form the embryo) and an outer layer of cells called the trophoblast (which will contribute to the placenta).\n\n4. **Implantation (Days 6-10)**: The blastocyst reaches the uterus and begins the process of implantation into the uterine lining. This typically occurs between days 6 and 10 after fertilization. The trophoblast cells proliferate and invade the uterine lining, establishing a connection that will support the developing embryo.\n\n5. **Formation of the Yolk Sac (End of Week 2)**: By the end of the second week, the yolk sac begins to form, which plays a role in early nutrient transfer and the development of the embryonic membranes.\n\nThese stages are crucial as they lay the foundation for subsequent embryonic development, which begins in earnest in the third week. The first two weeks are critical for establishing the environment necessary for the embryo's growth and development.
