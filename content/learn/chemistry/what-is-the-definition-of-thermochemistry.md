question: >-
    What is the definition of thermochemistry?
answer: >-
    Thermochemistry is defined as the branch of chemistry that studies the heat energy associated with chemical reactions and phase changes, such as melting and boiling. It focuses on the energy exchange between a system and its surroundings in the form of heat. This field of study is crucial for understanding how energy is transformed during chemical reactions, which can either absorb energy (endothermic reactions) or release energy (exothermic reactions).\n\nIn thermochemistry, chemical reactions are not limited to those that result in a change in chemical composition; it also includes reactions that involve physical transformations. The study of thermochemistry encompasses various concepts, including calorimetry, enthalpy, and the laws of thermodynamics. It is utilized in various scientific fields, such as biochemistry and chemical engineering, to predict reactant and product quantities and to understand energy transformations in chemical systems.\n\nOverall, thermochemistry provides insights into the energy dynamics of chemical processes, making it an essential area of study in both theoretical and applied chemistry.
