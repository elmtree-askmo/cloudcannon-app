question: >-
    What methodology was used in the Cold-Eeze trials?
answer: >-
    The methodology used in the Cold-Eeze trials primarily involved a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled design. This approach is considered the gold standard in clinical research as it minimizes bias and allows for a more accurate assessment of treatment efficacy.\n\n1. **Study Design**: The trials were designed as prospective, randomized, double-masked, placebo-controlled investigations. This means that participants were randomly assigned to receive either the zinc gluconate glycine lozenges (Cold-Eeze) or a placebo, and neither the participants nor the researchers knew which treatment was being administered (double-masking).\n\n2. **Setting and Participants**: The trials were conducted in outpatient settings, such as the Cleveland Clinic Foundation and the Detroit Medical Center. Participants included individuals who developed symptoms of the common cold within 24 hours prior to enrollment. For instance, one study involved 100 employees of the Cleveland Clinic.\n\n3. **Treatment Administration**: For therapeutic purposes, participants were instructed to take the lozenges four times a day, while for prophylactic purposes, they were administered once daily during the cold season.\n\n4. **Outcome Measurement**: The primary objective was to determine the therapeutic and prophylactic effectiveness of the lozenges in reducing the duration of cold symptoms. The studies aimed to assess whether the zinc lozenges would significantly shorten the time to resolution of cold symptoms compared to the placebo.\n\n5. **Statistical Analysis**: The trials employed statistical methods to analyze the data, including combining P-values from different trials and pooling results to evaluate the overall efficacy of the treatment.\n\nOverall, the methodology of the Cold-Eeze trials was robust, utilizing established principles of randomized controlled trials to evaluate the effectiveness of zinc lozenges in treating the common cold.
