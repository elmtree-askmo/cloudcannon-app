question: >-
    What are the causes and symptoms of hypophosphatemia?
answer: >-
    Hypophosphatemia is a condition characterized by low levels of phosphate in the blood, typically defined as a serum phosphorus concentration below 2.5 mg/dL (0.81 mmol/L) in adults. The causes of hypophosphatemia can vary and may include:\n\n1. **Inadequate Dietary Intake**: Insufficient consumption of phosphorus-rich foods can lead to low phosphate levels.\n2. **Malabsorption**: Conditions that affect nutrient absorption in the gastrointestinal tract, such as celiac disease or Crohn's disease, can result in hypophosphatemia.\n3. **Increased Renal Excretion**: Certain conditions, such as hyperparathyroidism or the use of diuretics, can cause the kidneys to excrete more phosphate than normal.\n4. **Refeeding Syndrome**: In patients who are malnourished and then receive nutritional support, phosphate can shift into cells, leading to low serum levels.\n5. **Chronic Alcoholism**: Alcohol can interfere with phosphate metabolism and absorption, contributing to hypophosphatemia.\n6. **Diabetic Ketoacidosis**: This condition can also lead to phosphate depletion.\n\nThe symptoms of hypophosphatemia can be nonspecific but may include:\n\n- Muscle weakness\n- Bone pain or softening\n- Seizures\n- Stupor or altered mental status\n- In severe cases, it can lead to coma or death\n\nDue to the potential for serious complications, it is important for healthcare providers to monitor phosphate levels and address any underlying causes of hypophosphatemia.
