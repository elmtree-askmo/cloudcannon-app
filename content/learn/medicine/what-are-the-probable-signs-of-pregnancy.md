question: >-
    What are the probable signs of pregnancy?
answer: >-
    The probable signs of pregnancy are indicators that suggest a woman may be pregnant, but they are not definitive. These signs can be observed by a healthcare provider during an examination. The most common probable signs of pregnancy include:\n\n1. **Cervical Changes**: Changes in the cervix can indicate pregnancy, such as softening (Goodell's sign) and changes in color (Chadwick's sign).\n2. **Mild Uterine Cramping**: Some women may experience cramping as the uterus begins to expand.\n3. **Quickening**: This refers to the first noticeable movements of the fetus, typically felt around 16 to 25 weeks of pregnancy.\n4. **Braxton Hicks Contractions**: These are irregular, often painless contractions that can occur throughout pregnancy, usually starting in the second trimester.\n5. **Positive Pregnancy Test**: A home pregnancy test or a blood test that detects the hormone human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG) is a strong indicator of pregnancy.\n\nIt is important to note that while these signs are likely to indicate pregnancy, they are not conclusive. A definitive confirmation of pregnancy is achieved through positive signs, which are observable and confirm the presence of a fetus.
