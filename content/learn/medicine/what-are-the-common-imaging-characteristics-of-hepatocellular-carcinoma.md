question: >-
    What are the common imaging characteristics of hepatocellular carcinoma?
answer: >-
    Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is characterized by specific imaging features that are crucial for its diagnosis and management. The common imaging characteristics of HCC, particularly when assessed through modalities such as computed tomography (CT) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), include:\n\n1. **Arterial Phase Hyperenhancement**: HCC typically demonstrates hyperenhancement during the arterial phase of imaging. This means that the tumor appears brighter compared to the surrounding liver tissue due to its vascularity.\n\n2. **Washout in Portal Venous Phase**: Following the initial hyperenhancement, HCC lesions often exhibit washout in the portal venous or delayed venous phases. This indicates that the tumor loses contrast enhancement relative to the surrounding liver tissue as the contrast agent is cleared.\n\n3. **Capsule Appearance**: In some cases, HCC may present with a capsule or a rim of enhancement surrounding the tumor, which can be a significant feature in differentiating it from other liver lesions.\n\n4. **Size and Shape**: HCC lesions can vary in size and may appear as irregularly shaped masses. The diagnostic algorithm suggests that lesions larger than 1 cm with the typical vascular enhancement pattern can be diagnosed as HCC without the need for biopsy.\n\n5. **Use of Advanced Imaging Techniques**: Techniques such as diffusion-weighted imaging and contrast-enhanced ultrasound (CEUS) can provide additional information about the tumor's biological behavior and enhance diagnostic accuracy.\n\n6. **Liver Imaging Reporting and Data System (LI-RADS)**: This system categorizes liver lesions based on imaging features, helping to standardize the diagnosis of HCC and improve the accuracy of imaging assessments.\n\nOverall, the imaging diagnosis of HCC relies heavily on its vascular characteristics, with CT and MRI being the primary modalities used for evaluation, especially in patients with chronic liver disease who are at risk for developing HCC.
