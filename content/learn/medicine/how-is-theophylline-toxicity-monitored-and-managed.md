question: >-
    How is theophylline toxicity monitored and managed?
answer: >-
    Theophylline toxicity is a significant concern due to the narrow therapeutic window of the drug, which is primarily used as a bronchodilator for conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). Monitoring and managing theophylline toxicity involves several key steps:\n\n### Monitoring\n1. **Routine Theophylline Level Monitoring**: \n   - For well-managed adults, theophylline levels should be monitored annually.\n   - For children, monitoring should occur every six months when the drug is administered orally.\n   - In cases of suspected toxicity or overdose, serum theophylline levels should be checked immediately, with repeat measurements every 2 hours until the peak level is reached.\n\n2. **Electrolyte and Vital Sign Monitoring**: \n   - Regular monitoring of serum electrolytes (including potassium and magnesium), calcium, and glucose is essential, as imbalances can occur with toxicity.\n   - Continuous monitoring of vital signs, including heart rate and rhythm, is crucial due to the risk of arrhythmias.\n\n### Management\n1. **Supportive Care**: \n   - Most patients with theophylline toxicity can be managed with supportive care, which includes ensuring airway, breathing, and circulation are stable.\n   - In severe cases, intubation and ventilator support may be necessary for airway protection.\n\n2. **Symptomatic Treatment**: \n   - For symptoms such as vomiting, intravenous ondansetron can be administered (0.15 mg/kg, maximum dose 16 mg), with an electrocardiogram (EKG) performed prior to administration to screen for prolonged QTc.\n\n3. **Severe Toxicity Management**: \n   - In cases of severe toxicity, particularly with life-threatening symptoms such as arrhythmias or seizures, hemodialysis may be indicated. This is especially true for theophylline levels exceeding 60 mcg/mL in patients aged 6 months to 60 years, or levels greater than 50 mcg/mL in patients younger than 6 months or older than 60 years.\n\n4. **Admission and Observation**: \n   - Patients exhibiting signs of toxicity or with theophylline levels higher than 30 mcg/mL should be admitted for further observation and management until their symptoms resolve and theophylline levels decrease.\n\n### Education\nPatients should be educated about the potential side effects and signs of theophylline toxicity, as early recognition and treatment are critical to prevent life-threatening complications.\n\nIn summary, effective monitoring and management of theophylline toxicity involve routine level checks, supportive care, symptomatic treatment, and, in severe cases, advanced interventions like hemodialysis.
