question: >-
    What are the primary causes of Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)?
answer: >-
    Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is the most common type of primary liver cancer and is primarily associated with chronic liver diseases. The primary causes of HCC include:\n\n1. **Chronic Viral Hepatitis**: The most significant risk factors for HCC are chronic infections with hepatitis B virus (HBV) and hepatitis C virus (HCV). These infections can lead to liver inflammation and cirrhosis, which significantly increase the risk of developing HCC. More than 80% of HCC cases related to HBV occur in patients with underlying cirrhosis.\n\n2. **Cirrhosis**: Cirrhosis, which can result from various causes including chronic viral hepatitis, alcoholic liver disease, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), is a major risk factor for HCC. Approximately 85% of patients diagnosed with HCC have cirrhosis.\n\n3. **Alcoholic Liver Disease**: Chronic alcohol consumption can lead to alcoholic liver disease, which is another significant risk factor for the development of HCC.\n\n4. **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)**: This condition, which is often associated with obesity and metabolic syndrome, can progress to non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) and subsequently lead to HCC.\n\n5. **Other Factors**: Additional risk factors include exposure to certain carcinogens, hereditary diseases, and metabolic disorders. For instance, conditions that cause liver damage or inflammation can contribute to the carcinogenic process.\n\n6. **Elevated Serum HBV DNA Levels**: In patients with HBV, high levels of serum HBV DNA (â‰¥10,000 copies/mL) are strong predictors of HCC risk, independent of other factors such as hepatitis B e antigen (HBeAg) status.\n\nIn summary, the primary causes of HCC are chronic hepatitis B and C infections, cirrhosis, alcoholic liver disease, and non-alcoholic fatty liver disease, with various other factors contributing to the risk.
