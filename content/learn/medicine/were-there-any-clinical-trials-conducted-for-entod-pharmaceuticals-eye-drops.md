question: >-
    Were there any clinical trials conducted for ENTOD Pharmaceuticals' eye drops?
answer: >-
    Yes, clinical trials have been conducted for ENTOD Pharmaceuticals' eye drops. The company has received approval from the Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation (CDSCO) to carry out phase III clinical trials for their PresVu eye drops, which are designed to treat presbyopia, a common age-related vision impairment. These trials have demonstrated the efficacy of PresVu in enhancing close-up vision within minutes of application.\n\nAdditionally, ENTOD Pharmaceuticals has also received approval for phase III trials of their 0.05% Atropine eye drops, which are intended to treat myopia, particularly in children. The approval for these trials was based on successful controlled clinical trials involving 234 patients, which showed both efficacy and safety.\n\nOverall, ENTOD Pharmaceuticals has been actively involved in conducting clinical trials for their innovative eye drop formulations, contributing to advancements in ophthalmic therapeutics.
