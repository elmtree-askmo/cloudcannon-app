question: >-
    What structures are found within the inguinal canal?
answer: >-
    The inguinal canal is an important anatomical structure located in the lower anterior abdominal wall, and its contents differ between males and females. \n\nIn males, the primary structures found within the inguinal canal include:\n- **Spermatic cord**: This contains the ductus deferens, testicular artery, pampiniform plexus of veins, and various nerves.\n- **Ilioinguinal nerve**: This nerve arises from the T12 and L1 spinal nerves and provides sensory innervation to the skin of the groin and upper thigh.\n- **Genital branch of the genitofemoral nerve**: This nerve arises from the L2 spinal nerve and supplies the cremaster muscle and skin of the scrotum.\n\nIn females, the contents of the inguinal canal are less complex and primarily include:\n- **Round ligament of the uterus**: This structure helps to anchor the uterus in place.\n- **Ilioinguinal nerve**: Similar to males, it also traverses the female inguinal canal.\n- **Genital branch of the genitofemoral nerve**: This nerve is present in females as well.\n\nBoth males and females share the ilioinguinal nerve and the genital branch of the genitofemoral nerve, but the spermatic cord is exclusive to males, while the round ligament is exclusive to females. \n\nThe inguinal canal serves as a passageway for these structures, running parallel to the inguinal ligament and extending from the deep inguinal ring to the superficial inguinal ring. Understanding the anatomy and contents of the inguinal canal is crucial for diagnosing and managing conditions such as inguinal hernias.
