question: >-
    What were the initial symptoms presented by the patient?
answer: >-
    The initial symptoms presented by a patient with cancer can vary significantly depending on the type of cancer and its location. In many cases, patients may not experience any noticeable symptoms during the early stages of cancer. However, certain early signs can be indicative of specific types of cancer. \n\nFor example, early signs of colorectal cancer may include:\n- Changes in bowel habits, such as diarrhea, constipation, or stool narrowing lasting more than a few days.\n- A persistent feeling of needing to have a bowel movement without relief.\n- Bright red rectal bleeding or blood in the stool.\n\nIn the case of brain cancer, initial symptoms often depend on the tumor's location within the brain. Common early symptoms may include:\n- Headaches that feel different from previous headaches.\n- Seizures.\n- Changes in personality or behavior.\n- Difficulty thinking, speaking, or finding words.\n- Vision problems or memory loss.\n\nAdditionally, some patients may experience systemic symptoms such as severe fatigue, unexplained weight loss, or night sweats, which can be associated with various types of cancer.\n\nIt's important to note that these symptoms can mimic those of other medical conditions, making it crucial for individuals experiencing such symptoms to seek medical evaluation for accurate diagnosis and timely intervention.
