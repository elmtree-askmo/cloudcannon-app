question: >-
    How has "Night" been translated over the years, and into how many languages?
answer: >-
    Elie Wiesel's memoir "Night," which recounts his harrowing experiences during the Holocaust, has been translated into more than 30 languages since its initial publication. The book was originally published in Yiddish in 1956 under the title "La Nuit" and was later translated into French and then into English in 1960. The English translation was done by Stella Rodway and published by Hill & Wang. Over the years, "Night" has become a significant work in Holocaust literature and is widely read in schools across the United States and around the world. Its translations have allowed it to reach a diverse audience, ensuring that the lessons and memories of the Holocaust are preserved and shared globally.
