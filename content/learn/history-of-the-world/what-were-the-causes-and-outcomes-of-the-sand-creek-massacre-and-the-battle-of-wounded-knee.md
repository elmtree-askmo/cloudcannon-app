question: >-
    What were the causes and outcomes of the Sand Creek Massacre and the Battle of Wounded Knee?
answer: >-
    The Sand Creek Massacre and the Battle of Wounded Knee were two significant events in the history of Native American relations with the United States government, both marked by violence and tragic outcomes.\n\n### Sand Creek Massacre (1864)\n\n**Causes:**\n1. **Westward Expansion:** The U.S. government's push for westward expansion led to increased tensions between settlers and Native American tribes. The Cheyenne and Arapaho tribes were among those affected, as settlers encroached on their lands.\n2. **Broken Treaties:** Previous treaties that guaranteed land to Native Americans were often ignored or violated by the U.S. government, leading to distrust and conflict.\n3. **Military Presence:** The presence of U.S. troops in the region heightened tensions. The Colorado Territory's governor, John Evans, encouraged military action against Native Americans, viewing them as obstacles to settlement.\n\n**Outcomes:**\n1. **Mass Killing:** On November 29, 1864, Colorado militia attacked a peaceful encampment of Cheyenne and Arapaho at Sand Creek, resulting in the deaths of approximately 150-200 Native Americans, mostly women and children.\n2. **Public Outcry:** The massacre sparked outrage among some Americans, including figures like Helen Hunt Jackson, who wrote "A Century of Dishonor" to expose the injustices faced by Native Americans.\n3. **Increased Violence:** The massacre intensified hostilities between Native Americans and U.S. forces, leading to further conflicts in the region.\n\n### Battle of Wounded Knee (1890)\n\n**Causes:**\n1. **Cultural Suppression:** The U.S. government's efforts to suppress Native American cultures, including the Ghost Dance movement, which sought to restore Native American ways of life, created fear among authorities.\n2. **Economic Hardship:** Many Native Americans faced dire economic conditions due to loss of land and resources, leading to desperation and unrest.\n3. **Military Tensions:** The U.S. Army's presence and the fear of uprisings led to increased military readiness and tension in Native American communities.\n\n**Outcomes:**\n1. **Massacre:** On December 29, 1890, U.S. troops opened fire on a group of Lakota Sioux at Wounded Knee Creek, resulting in the deaths of around 250 Native Americans, including women and children.\n2. **End of Armed Resistance:** The massacre is often considered the end of the Indian Wars and marked a significant defeat for Native American resistance against U.S. expansion.\n3. **Legacy of Trauma:** The events at Wounded Knee left a lasting impact on Native American communities, contributing to a legacy of trauma and loss that continues to affect them today.\n\n### Conclusion\n\nBoth the Sand Creek Massacre and the Battle of Wounded Knee exemplify the violent conflicts that arose from U.S. expansionist policies and the systemic oppression of Native American peoples. These events not only resulted in significant loss of life but also highlighted the broader issues of cultural suppression, broken treaties, and the struggle for Native American rights that persist in various forms to this day.
