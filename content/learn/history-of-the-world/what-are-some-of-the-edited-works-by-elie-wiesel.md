    What are some of the edited works by Elie Wiesel?
answer: >-
    Elie Wiesel, a prominent Holocaust survivor and author, wrote extensively throughout his life, producing over fifty books that encompass memoirs, essays, and fiction. Some of his notable edited works include:\n\n1. **Against Silence: The Voice & Vision of Elie Wiesel** - A collection of shorter writings edited by Irving Abrahamson, published in three volumes in 1985.\n2. **Night/Dawn/Day** - This compilation includes Wiesel's first memoir, "Night," and his first two novels, "Dawn" and "Day," collectively known as The Night Trilogy, published in 1985.\n3. **Job ou Dieu dans la tempÃªte** - A dialogue and commentary with Josy Eisenberg, published in 1986.\n4. **A Song for Hope** - A cantata published in 1987.\n5. **The Nobel Speech** - Wiesel's acceptance speech for the Nobel Peace Prize, delivered in 1987.\n\nIn addition to these edited works, Wiesel's bibliography includes significant titles such as:\n\n- **Souls on Fire: Portraits and Legends of Hasidic Masters** (1972)\n- **Messengers of God: Biblical Portraits and Legends** (1976)\n- **One Generation After** (1970)\n- **And the Sea is Never Full: Memoirs Vol. II** (1999)\n\nWiesel's writings often reflect his experiences during the Holocaust and his commitment to human rights, making his works essential for understanding the moral and ethical implications of that period in history.
