question: >-
    How did the public react to the assassinations of George Moscone and Harvey Milk?
answer: >-
    The public reaction to the assassinations of George Moscone and Harvey Milk on November 27, 1978, was one of profound shock and grief. More than 25,000 people gathered in the park and streets around San Francisco's City Hall in a spontaneous demonstration to mourn the loss of these two prominent figures. The murders sent ripples through the LGBTQ+ community and the broader San Francisco populace, as both Moscone and Milk were highly respected leaders.\n\nIn the immediate aftermath, the city experienced a mix of peaceful candlelight vigils and violent protests, notably the White Night Riot, which erupted in response to the perceived leniency of Dan White's trial and verdict. White, who had assassinated both men, was charged with first-degree murder but ultimately convicted of voluntary manslaughter, leading to public outrage and unrest.\n\nThe assassinations not only highlighted the political tensions of the time but also galvanized the LGBTQ+ rights movement, with Milk becoming a martyr and symbol for the cause. His legacy continues to influence the fight for LGBTQ+ rights, and the events surrounding his death are still commemorated and discussed today. The public's reaction was a pivotal moment in San Francisco's history, marking a significant turning point in the socio-political landscape of the city and the nation.
