question: >-
    What challenges did Rizal face during the publication of Noli Me Tangere?
answer: >-
    JosÃ© Rizal faced several significant challenges during the publication of his novel "Noli Me Tangere." \n\n1. **Health and Financial Struggles**: Rizal experienced periods of sickness and poverty, which contributed to his despair over the publication process. At one point, he nearly destroyed the manuscript due to these overwhelming feelings.\n\n2. **Censorship and Controversy**: Upon its publication in 1887 in Berlin, "Noli Me Tangere" quickly became a sensation but also faced severe backlash from the Spanish clergy and colonial authorities in the Philippines. The book was banned shortly after its release, which posed a significant challenge for Rizal, as it limited its distribution and readership in his home country.\n\n3. **Political Repercussions**: The novel's critical portrayal of the Spanish colonial government and the Catholic Church led to personal risks for Rizal. After returning to the Philippines, he was forced to abandon his newly established clinic due to the controversy surrounding the book, which put him in a precarious position with the authorities.\n\n4. **Publication Logistics**: The process of getting the book printed was also fraught with difficulties. Rizal had to finance the printing himself, which cost him 300 pesos for 2,000 copies. This financial burden was significant, especially given his limited resources at the time.\n\n5. **Cultural and Social Resistance**: The themes of social injustice and colonial oppression that Rizal addressed in "Noli Me Tangere" were not only controversial but also met with resistance from those who benefited from the status quo. This societal pushback made it challenging for Rizal to promote his work and its messages effectively.\n\nOverall, these challenges highlight the complex interplay of personal, social, and political factors that Rizal navigated in his quest to publish "Noli Me Tangere," a work that would ultimately play a crucial role in the Philippine nationalist movement.
