question: >-
    Who were the key figures in the 1857 Revolt and what roles did they play?
answer: >-
    The Revolt of 1857, also known as the First War of Independence, saw the emergence of several key figures who played significant roles in the uprising against British rule. Here are some of the prominent leaders and their contributions:\n\n1. **Bahadur Shah II (Bahadur Shah Zafar)**: The last Mughal Emperor, he was a symbolic figurehead of the revolt. Although he had limited power, he was acknowledged as the sovereign leader by many rebels, and his presence in Delhi galvanized support for the uprising.\n\n2. **Begum Hazrat Mahal**: The Begum of Awadh (Oudh), she played a crucial role in leading the resistance against the British in Lucknow. She took charge of the administration and organized the defense of the city, becoming a prominent figure in the revolt.\n\n3. **Nana Sahib**: The adopted son of the deposed Maratha Peshwa Baji Rao II, Nana Sahib led the revolt in Kanpur. He was instrumental in rallying troops and civilians against the British, and he played a key role in the siege of Kanpur.\n\n4. **Rani Lakshmibai**: The queen of Jhansi, she became one of the most iconic figures of the revolt. Rani Lakshmibai led her troops valiantly against the British forces and became a symbol of resistance and bravery.\n\n5. **Tantia Tope**: A close associate of Nana Sahib, Tantia Tope was a military leader who played a significant role in various battles during the revolt. He was known for his guerrilla warfare tactics and was instrumental in the recapture of Kanpur.\n\n6. **General Bakht Khan**: He was a prominent leader in Delhi and took command of the rebel forces during the siege of the city. His military experience was vital in organizing the defense against British attacks.\n\n7. **Birjis Qadir**: The son of Nawab Wajid Ali Shah of Awadh, he was declared the ruler by the rebels in Lucknow and played a significant role in the administration and military efforts during the revolt.\n\n8. **Ahmadullah Shah**: A religious leader and a key figure in the revolt, he inspired many to join the fight against the British, particularly in the region of Awadh.\n\nThese leaders, among others, represented a diverse array of backgrounds and regions, uniting various segments of Indian society in a collective effort to resist British imperialism. Their contributions were pivotal in the initial successes of the revolt, although ultimately, the uprising was suppressed by the British forces.
