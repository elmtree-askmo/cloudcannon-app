    What methodologies are used in historical analysis to study Celtic societies?
answer: >-
    In historical analysis, particularly when studying Celtic societies, several methodologies are employed to gain a comprehensive understanding of their social systems, cultures, and historical contexts. Here are the primary methodologies used:\n\n1. **Historical Textual Studies**: This method involves the examination of written records, which can include texts in Celtic languages, Latin, and other languages that document the history and culture of Celtic-speaking peoples. These texts provide insights into societal structures, beliefs, and practices.\n\n2. **Archaeological Analysis**: Archaeology plays a crucial role in understanding Celtic societies by analyzing material culture, such as artifacts, settlement patterns, and burial sites. This method helps to reconstruct daily life, social organization, and economic practices of the Celts.\n\n3. **Regional Comparisons**: Researchers often use regional comparisons to evaluate differences and similarities among various Celtic settlements.
