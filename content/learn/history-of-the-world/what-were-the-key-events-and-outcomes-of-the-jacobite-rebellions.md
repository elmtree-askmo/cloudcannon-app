question: >-
    What were the key events and outcomes of the Jacobite Rebellions?
answer: >-
    The Jacobite Rebellions were a series of uprisings in Great Britain during the late 17th and 18th centuries, primarily aimed at restoring the Stuart monarchy to the throne. The key events and outcomes of these rebellions can be summarized as follows:\n\n### Key Events\n\n1. **The Glorious Revolution (1688)**: The rebellions were rooted in the political turmoil following the Glorious Revolution, which saw James II (James VII in Scotland) deposed in favor of William of Orange and Mary II. This event led to a significant division between supporters of the Stuart claim (Jacobites) and the new Protestant monarchy.\n\n2. **The Jacobite Rebellion of 1715**: \n   - **May 10, 1715**: The Jacobite army, led by the Earl of Mar, began its campaign by marching on Perth.\n   - **August 21, 1715**: The Jacobites faced a significant defeat at the Battle of Dunkeld, which weakened their forces and morale.\n   - **December 22, 1715**: The Old Pretender, James Francis Edward Stuart, landed in Scotland but was unable to secure a lasting victory.\n\n3. **The Jacobite Rebellion of 1745**:\n   - **July 23, 1745**: Prince Charles Edward Stuart, known as "Bonnie Prince Charlie," landed on the Isle of Eriskay, marking the start of the 'Forty-Five' Rebellion.\n   - The Jacobite forces achieved initial successes, including the capture of Edinburgh and a victory at the Battle of Prestonpans.\n   - **April 16, 1746**: The rebellion culminated in the Battle of Culloden, where the Jacobite forces were decisively defeated by the British government troops. This battle is noted as the last major conflict fought on British soil.\n\n### Outcomes\n\n- **Political Consequences**: The Jacobite Rebellions highlighted the deep-seated divisions in British society regarding monarchy and governance. The failure of the rebellions solidified the Hanoverian dynasty's hold on the British throne and led to increased repression of Jacobite sympathizers.\n\n- **Cultural Impact**: The aftermath of the rebellions saw a significant cultural shift in Scotland, with the British government implementing measures to suppress Highland culture, including the banning of tartans and the wearing of kilts.\n\n- **Legacy**: The Jacobite cause has since become a symbol of Scottish nationalism and resistance against English rule. The romanticized view of the Jacobite Rebellions has influenced literature, music, and Scottish identity.\n\nOverall, the Jacobite Rebellions were pivotal in shaping the political landscape of Britain and the cultural identity of Scotland, leaving a lasting legacy that continues to resonate today.
