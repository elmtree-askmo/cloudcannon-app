question: >-
    What is the historical context of India-Maldives relations and India's role in it?
answer: >-
    The historical context of India-Maldives relations is characterized by a deep-rooted cultural, economic, and strategic partnership that has evolved significantly since the Maldives gained independence from British rule in 1965. \n\n1. **Cultural and Historical Ties**: India and the Maldives share strong cultural links, including similarities in language, religion, and traditions. These connections date back centuries, fostering a sense of kinship between the two nations.\n\n2. **Diplomatic Relations**: India was one of the first countries to recognize the Maldives after its independence, establishing diplomatic relations shortly thereafter. The Indian mission in the Maldives was set up in 1972, and a resident High Commissioner was appointed in 1980, marking the formalization of diplomatic ties.\n\n3. **Economic Cooperation**: The economic relationship has been bolstered through various agreements, including a comprehensive trade agreement signed in 1981. India has provided significant economic aid to the Maldives, contributing to infrastructure development, healthcare, and education. Notably, India established the Indira Gandhi Memorial Hospital in MalÃ© and has increased scholarships for Maldivian students.\n\n4. **Strategic Importance**: The Maldives holds a strategic position in the Indian Ocean, acting as a gateway to the Arabian Sea. This geographical significance has made the Maldives a focal point in India's foreign policy, particularly in the context of countering China's growing influence in the region. The "Neighbourhood First" policy of India emphasizes the importance of maintaining strong ties with the Maldives.\n\n5. **Military Cooperation**: India has played a crucial role in the Maldives' security, particularly during the 1988 coup attempt when India intervened to restore order. This operation, known as Operation Cactus, solidified military cooperation between the two nations.\n\n6. **Recent Developments**: In recent years, the relationship has faced challenges, particularly with the rise of the "India Out" campaign in the Maldives, which has been fueled by domestic political changes. However, recent political shifts, including the election of President Mohammed Muizzu, have led to a reevaluation of Maldives' foreign policy, with a renewed focus on strengthening ties with India.\n\n7. **Current Dynamics**: Despite occasional strains, such as derogatory remarks from Maldivian officials and the impact of domestic politics on bilateral relations, India remains a key partner for the Maldives. The strategic importance of the Maldives to India ensures that fostering this relationship continues to be a priority for both nations.\n\nIn summary, the historical context of India-Maldives relations is marked by a blend of cultural affinity, economic cooperation, and strategic partnership, which has evolved through various political and diplomatic developments over the decades.
