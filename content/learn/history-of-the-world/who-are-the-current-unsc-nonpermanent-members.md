question: >-
    Who are the current UNSC non-permanent members?
answer: >-
    As of June 2023, the current non-permanent members of the United Nations Security Council (UNSC) are:\n\n1. Algeria\n2. Guyana\n3. Republic of Korea\n4. Sierra Leone\n5. Slovenia\n\nThese countries were elected by the United Nations General Assembly and will serve a two-year term starting in January 2024. Additionally, the other non-permanent members currently serving are Albania, Brazil, Gabon, Ghana, and the United Arab Emirates. \n\nThe UNSC consists of 15 members in total, with 5 permanent members (China, France, Russia, the United Kingdom, and the United States) and 10 non-permanent members, which are elected for two-year terms.
