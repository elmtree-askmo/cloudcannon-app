question: >-
    What was Deborah Champion's mission in espionage during the American Revolution?
answer: >-
    Deborah Champion played a significant role in espionage during the American Revolution by acting as a courier for the Continental Army. Her mission involved carrying important dispatches, which were crucial for the communication and coordination of military efforts. Champion's daring actions are often compared to those of Paul Revere, highlighting her bravery and commitment to the Patriot cause.\n\nAccording to historian Carol Berkin, Champion's contributions are part of a broader narrative of women's involvement in espionage during the war. Women like Champion often operated under the radar, taking on roles that were essential to the war effort while navigating the societal constraints of their time. Her express-riding missions not only showcased her courage but also underscored the vital contributions of women in the American Revolution, alongside other notable figures such as Sybil Ludington and Agent 355 from the Culper Spy Ring.\n\nIn summary, Deborah Champion's mission in espionage was to deliver critical messages for the Continental Army, making her a key figure in the network of female spies and couriers who supported the American Revolution.
