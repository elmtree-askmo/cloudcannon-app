question: >-
    What was Anna Smith Strong's role in the Culper Spy Ring?
answer: >-
    Anna Smith Strong played a significant role in the Culper Spy Ring during the American Revolution, which was a crucial intelligence network established to gather information for General George Washington. Based in Setauket, Long Island, New York, she was one of the few female members of this spy ring, which was organized by Major Benjamin Tallmadge in 1778.\n\nHer primary contribution involved relaying signals to a courier who was responsible for smuggling and military missions. Anna utilized a clever method of communication by using her laundry as a means to send coded messages. For instance, the number of items she hung out to dry would indicate specific messages to the courier, who would then transport the information to Washington. This innovative approach allowed her to operate under the British's radar, as they underestimated her capabilities as a spy.\n\nAnna's involvement in the Culper Spy Ring was particularly notable given the context of her personal life; her husband, Selah Strong, was a captain in the New York militia and was imprisoned by the British as a suspected spy. Despite the dangers, Anna remained committed to her role in the spy ring, contributing to the American effort against British forces.\n\nOverall, Anna Smith Strong's actions exemplified the vital contributions of women in espionage during the American Revolution, showcasing their courage and resourcefulness in supporting the Patriot cause.
