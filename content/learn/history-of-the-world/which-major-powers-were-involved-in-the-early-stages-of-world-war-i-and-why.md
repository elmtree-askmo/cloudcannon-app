question: >-
    Which major powers were involved in the early stages of World War I and why?
answer: >-
    In the early stages of World War I, the major powers involved were divided into two main factions: the Central Powers and the Allied Powers.\n\n1. **Central Powers**: This coalition primarily consisted of:\n   - **Germany**: A leading military power in Europe, Germany sought to expand its influence and territory.\n   - **Austria-Hungary**: Motivated by nationalism and the desire to suppress Serbian nationalism, Austria-Hungary declared war on Serbia following the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand.\n   - **Ottoman Empire**: Joined the Central Powers later in the conflict, seeking to regain lost territories and maintain its influence in the region.\n\n2. **Allied Powers**: This coalition included:\n   - **France**: Eager to counter German expansion and reclaim Alsace-Lorraine, which had been lost in the Franco-Prussian War.\n   - **Russia**: Supported Serbia due to Slavic ties and aimed to assert its influence in the Balkans.\n   - **United Kingdom**: Initially pursuing a policy of disengagement, Britain entered the war to support Belgium and counter German militarism.\n   - Other nations such as Belgium, Japan, and later the United States and Italy also joined the Allies.\n\nThe outbreak of the war was precipitated by a complex web of alliances, militarism, nationalism, and imperial ambitions. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand on June 28, 1914, acted as a catalyst, leading Austria-Hungary to issue an ultimatum to Serbia, which ultimately escalated into a full-scale war as alliances were activated. The intricate system of alliances, including the Triple Entente (France, Russia, and the UK) and the Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, and Italy), played a crucial role in the rapid escalation of the conflict. \n\nThus, the major powers involved were driven by a combination of national interests, historical grievances, and the desire for territorial expansion, which collectively contributed to the outbreak of World War I.
