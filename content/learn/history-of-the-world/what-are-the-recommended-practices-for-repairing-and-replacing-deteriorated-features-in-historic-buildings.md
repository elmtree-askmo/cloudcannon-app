question: >-
    What are the recommended practices for repairing and replacing deteriorated features in historic buildings?
answer: >-
    The recommended practices for repairing and replacing deteriorated features in historic buildings are guided by the Secretary of the Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation. These practices emphasize a careful and respectful approach to maintaining the historical integrity of the building while addressing necessary repairs. Here are the key recommendations:\n\n1. **Repair Historic Materials and Features**: The first step in addressing deteriorated features is to repair rather than replace. This involves using the least degree of intervention possible, such as patching, piecing-in, splicing, consolidating, or reinforcing the existing materials. This approach helps preserve the original character-defining elements of the building.\n\n2. **Replace Extensively Deteriorated Features**: If a feature is too deteriorated to repair, it may be appropriate to replace it. In such cases, the replacement should be based on documentary evidence and should replicate the form and detailing of the original feature. This ensures that the new feature is consistent with the historic character of the building.\n\n3. **Evaluate Physical Condition**: Before undertaking repairs or replacements, a thorough evaluation of the building's physical condition should be conducted. This assessment helps determine the extent of deterioration and informs the decision-making process regarding repairs or replacements.\n\n4. **Use Appropriate Materials**: When replacing features, it is crucial to use materials that are compatible with the historic building. This not only aids in maintaining the aesthetic integrity but also ensures the longevity of the repairs.\n\n5. **Follow Guidelines for Rehabilitation**: The Illustrated Guidelines for Rehabilitating Historic Buildings provide specific recommendations for various types of materials and features, including masonry, wood, and architectural metals. These guidelines help property owners and developers apply the Standards effectively during the rehabilitation process.\n\nBy adhering to these practices, the rehabilitation of historic buildings can be carried out in a manner that respects their historical and cultural significance while meeting contemporary needs.
