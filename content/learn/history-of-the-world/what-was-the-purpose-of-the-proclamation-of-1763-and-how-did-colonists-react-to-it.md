question: >-
    What was the purpose of the Proclamation of 1763, and how did colonists react to it?
answer: >-
    The Proclamation of 1763 was a significant policy issued by the British government following the conclusion of the French and Indian War (also known as the Seven Years' War). Its primary purpose was to stabilize relations with Indigenous nations by prohibiting colonial settlement west of the Appalachian Mountains. This boundary was intended to prevent further conflicts over land between colonists and Indigenous peoples, as the British sought to manage the aftermath of the war and the newly acquired territories.\n\nColonists reacted negatively to the Proclamation of 1763, particularly those who were land speculators and settlers eager to expand westward. Many viewed the proclamation as an infringement on their rights and ambitions, leading to widespread discontent. The restriction on westward expansion was seen as a barrier to economic opportunity and personal freedom, which contributed to growing tensions between the colonies and Britain. This discontent was a precursor to the larger colonial resistance that would eventually culminate in the American Revolutionary War.\n\nIn summary, the Proclamation of 1763 aimed to maintain peace with Indigenous nations and manage colonial expansion, but it instead fueled resentment among colonists, highlighting the growing divide between British authorities and American settlers.
