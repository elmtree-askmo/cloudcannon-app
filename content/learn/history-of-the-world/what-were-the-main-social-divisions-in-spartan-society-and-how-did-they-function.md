question: >-
    What were the main social divisions in Spartan society, and how did they function?
answer: >-
    In Spartan society, the main social divisions were primarily based on citizenship status, which played a crucial role in the functioning of their militaristic and communal lifestyle. The three primary classes were:\n\n1. **Spartiates (Homoioi)**: This group comprised the full citizens of Sparta, who were enfranchised male citizens. They underwent the rigorous state-sponsored education and training system known as the **Agoge**, which emphasized military discipline, physical fitness, and communal values. Spartiates had the right to participate in governance and held significant political power within the city-state. Their primary responsibility was to serve as soldiers, and they were expected to maintain a lifestyle that reflected Spartan ideals of austerity and bravery.\n\n2. **Perioikoi**: The perioikoi were free inhabitants of the surrounding regions who lived in areas controlled by Sparta but were not considered full citizens. They shared cultural similarities with the Spartiates but were excluded from political rights and governance. The perioikoi played an essential role in the economy, engaging in trade and crafts, and they were often called upon to serve in the military alongside the Spartiates during times of war.\n\n3. **Helots**: The helots were a subjugated class primarily composed of the Messenian population, who were enslaved by the Spartans following their conquest of Messenia. Helots worked the land and provided agricultural support for the Spartan economy, allowing the Spartiates to focus on military training and governance. They were bound to the land and had limited rights, often facing harsh treatment and oppression. The helots could occasionally revolt, leading to significant social tensions, as seen during the Messenian Wars.\n\nThis rigid social structure was essential for maintaining Sparta's military-oriented society. The Spartiates relied on the labor of the helots to sustain their way of life, while the perioikoi contributed to the economy without threatening the political dominance of the Spartiates. The interplay between these classes created a unique social dynamic that was integral to the functioning of Spartan society, emphasizing the importance of military readiness and communal responsibility.
