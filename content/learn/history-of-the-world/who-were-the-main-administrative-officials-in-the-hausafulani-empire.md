question: >-
    Who were the main administrative officials in the Hausa-Fulani Empire?
answer: >-
    The main administrative officials in the Hausa-Fulani Empire, also known as the Sokoto Caliphate, were part of a hierarchical structure that facilitated governance across the empire. The key officials included:\n\n1. **Caliph (Emir)**: The highest authority in the empire, responsible for both political and spiritual leadership. The Caliph was the ultimate decision-maker and had significant influence over the administration.\n\n2. **Waziri**: The senior official and administrative adviser to the Emir, often acting as the Prime Minister. The Waziri played a crucial role in the day-to-day administration of the empire.\n\n3. **Galadima**: This official was in charge of the capital and was responsible for overseeing the administration within the central region of the empire.\n\n4. **Madawaki**: The commander of the army, responsible for military affairs and the defense of the empire.\n\n5. **Hakimi**: Appointed by the Emir, the Hakimi was responsible for the administration of districts within the emirate. Each emirate was divided into districts for administrative convenience.\n\n6. **Village Heads**: These officials were appointed by the Hakimi to assist in local governance, including tax collection and maintaining law and order within villages.\n\n7. **Shura Council**: This advisory council comprised respected figures from different regions of the empire and was tasked with advising the Caliph on governance and the application of Islamic law.\n\nThe administrative structure of the Hausa-Fulani Empire was characterized by a clear division of labor and responsibilities, ensuring effective governance across its vast territories.
