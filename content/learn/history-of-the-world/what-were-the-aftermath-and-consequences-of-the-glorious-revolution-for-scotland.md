question: >-
    What were the aftermath and consequences of the Glorious Revolution for Scotland?
answer: >-
    The aftermath of the Glorious Revolution of 1688-89 had significant consequences for Scotland, both politically and religiously. Here are some key points regarding its impact:\n\n1. **Change of Monarchy**: The Glorious Revolution resulted in the deposition of James VII (James II of England) and the ascension of William III and Mary II as joint monarchs. This transition was confirmed by a Convention of Estates in Scotland in March 1689, which recognized William and Mary as the rightful rulers.\n\n2. **Religious Shifts**: The revolution marked a significant shift in religious power. The Protestant establishment gained dominance, leading to the marginalization of Catholics and those loyal to James VII. The revolution did not establish religious liberty as intended; instead, it transferred power from one denomination to another, solidifying the Presbyterian Church of Scotland's position while suppressing Catholicism.\n\n3. **Political Turmoil and Jacobite Rebellions**: The aftermath saw the rise of Jacobitism, a movement supporting the restoration of the Stuart monarchy. Many Scots who remained loyal to James VII became known as Jacobites, leading to a series of uprisings against the new regime. The most notable of these was the Jacobite rising of 1715 and the subsequent uprisings, which were ultimately unsuccessful.\n\n4. **Legislative Changes**: The Glorious Revolution led to significant legislative changes in Scotland. The Act of Settlement 1701, which followed the revolution, further entrenched Protestant succession to the throne and laid the groundwork for future political developments, including the eventual union with England in 1707.\n\n5. **Impact on Governance**: The revolution also had implications for governance in Scotland. It marked a shift towards a more parliamentary system, reducing the power of the monarchy and increasing the influence of Parliament. This change was part of a broader trend in Britain that continued to evolve in the following centuries.\n\n6. **Economic Consequences**: The political instability and conflicts that followed the Glorious Revolution, including the Jacobite uprisings, had economic repercussions. The turmoil affected trade and agriculture, contributing to economic distress in certain regions of Scotland.\n\nIn summary, the Glorious Revolution had profound and lasting effects on Scotland, reshaping its political landscape, altering religious dynamics, and setting the stage for future conflicts and governance structures.
