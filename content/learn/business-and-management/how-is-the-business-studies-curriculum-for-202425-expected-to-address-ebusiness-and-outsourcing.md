question: >-
    How is the business studies curriculum for 2024-25 expected to address e-business and outsourcing?
answer: >-
    The business studies curriculum for the academic year 2024-25, particularly for CBSE Class 11, is expected to address e-business and outsourcing through a structured approach that emphasizes emerging modes of business. The syllabus includes topics such as Business Process Outsourcing (BPO), which covers its concept, need, and scope. This indicates a focus on understanding how outsourcing functions within the broader context of business operations.\n\nAdditionally, the curriculum is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of various business concepts, including the transformative impact of e-business. By integrating these topics, the curriculum aims to equip students with the knowledge necessary to navigate the evolving business landscape, which increasingly relies on digital solutions and outsourcing strategies.\n\nStudents will likely explore the benefits of e-business, such as cost efficiency and enhanced operational capabilities, while also addressing security concerns associated with online transactions. This holistic approach will prepare students to critically analyze the differences between e-business and traditional business models, as well as the implications of outsourcing in contemporary business practices.\n\nOverall, the 2024-25 business studies curriculum is set to provide a robust framework for students to understand the significance of e-business and outsourcing in today's economy, fostering skills that are essential for future business leaders.
