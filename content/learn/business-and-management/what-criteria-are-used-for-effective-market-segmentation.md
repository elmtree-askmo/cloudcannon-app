question: >-
    What criteria are used for effective market segmentation?
answer: >-
    Effective market segmentation is crucial for businesses to tailor their marketing strategies to meet the specific needs of different consumer groups. The criteria for effective segmentation include:\n\n1. **Measurability**: This criterion refers to the ability to quantify the characteristics of the segment. It is essential that the size and purchasing power of the segment can be measured to ensure that it is viable for targeting.\n\n2. **Accessibility**: A segment must be reachable through marketing efforts. This means that the company should be able to effectively communicate with and deliver products or services to the segment.\n\n3. **Substantiality**: The segment should be large enough to be profitable. If a segment is too small, it may not justify the marketing costs associated with targeting it.\n\n4. **Differentiability**: The segment must be distinct from other segments. This means that the needs, preferences, or behaviors of the segment should be different enough to warrant a tailored marketing approach.\n\n5. **Actionability**: The company must be able to design effective marketing strategies to attract and serve the segment. This involves having the resources and capabilities to implement targeted marketing programs.\n\nBy applying these criteria, businesses can identify and focus on the most promising market segments, allowing for more efficient resource allocation and more effective marketing strategies.
