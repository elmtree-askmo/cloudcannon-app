question: >-
    What are the pricing plans for Airtable and how do they compare to competitors?
answer: >-
    Airtable offers a variety of pricing plans to cater to different user needs, which can be summarized as follows:\n\n1. **Free Plan**: This plan is available at no cost and supports up to five users. It provides basic features suitable for small teams or individual users.\n\n2. **Team Plan**: Formerly known as the Pro plan, this plan costs $24 per user per month. It includes advanced features that are beneficial for collaborative teams, such as increased record limits and additional automation capabilities.\n\n3. **Business Plan**: This plan is priced at $48 per user per month and is designed for larger teams that require more robust features, including enhanced security and advanced integrations.\n\n4. **Enterprise Scale Plan**: Pricing for this plan is custom and tailored for large organizations that need extensive features and support for scaling applications across their operations.\n\nIt's important to note that the Plus plan was deprecated in 2023, and existing users were transitioned to the Team plan.\n\n### Comparison with Competitors\n\nWhen comparing Airtable's pricing to competitors like **monday.com**, **ClickUp**, and **Notion**, Airtable's pricing structure is generally competitive. For instance:\n\n- **monday.com** offers plans starting from around $8 per user per month, but the features vary significantly based on the plan tier.\n- **ClickUp** provides a free tier and paid plans starting at $7 per user per month, which includes unlimited integrations and features that can be appealing for project management.\n- **Notion** has a free plan for personal use and charges $8 per user per month for team collaboration features.\n\nAirtable's unique value proposition lies in its flexibility and user-friendly interface, which allows users to create custom databases and project management tools tailored to their specific needs. This flexibility can justify its pricing, especially for teams that require a more database-oriented approach to project management.\n\nIn summary, while Airtable's pricing may be higher than some competitors, its features and capabilities can provide significant value for teams looking for a versatile tool that goes beyond traditional project management.
