question: >-
    What are the defining characteristics of a corporation?
answer: >-
    A corporation is a specific legal form of business organization that possesses several defining characteristics, which distinguish it from other forms of business entities such as sole proprietorships and partnerships. Here are the key characteristics of a corporation:\n\n1. **Separate Legal Entity**: A corporation is recognized as a separate legal entity from its owners, known as shareholders. This means that the corporation can own property, enter into contracts, and be sued in its own name.\n\n2. **Limited Liability**: One of the most significant advantages of a corporation is limited liability protection. Shareholders are typically not personally liable for the debts and obligations of the corporation. Their financial risk is limited to the amount they have invested in the corporation.\n\n3. **Perpetual Existence**: Corporations have a perpetual lifespan, meaning they continue to exist independently of changes in ownership or the status of shareholders. If a shareholder sells their shares or passes away, the corporation remains unaffected.\n\n4. **Double Taxation**: Corporations are subject to double taxation, where the corporation pays taxes on its profits, and shareholders also pay taxes on dividends received. However, certain types of corporations, such as S corporations, can avoid this double taxation by allowing profits and losses to pass through to shareholders' personal tax returns.\n\n5. **Professional Management**: Corporations often have a structured management system, with a board of directors overseeing the company's operations and making strategic decisions. This professional management can attract skilled individuals to run the business effectively.\n\n6. **Transferability of Ownership**: Ownership in a corporation is easily transferable through the sale of shares. This feature makes it easier for investors to enter or exit the business compared to other forms of business organization.\n\nThese characteristics make corporations a popular choice for entrepreneurs and investors seeking to limit personal liability, access capital, and ensure the continuity of their business operations.
