question: >-
    What are the trends in foreign acquisitions of U.S. companies?
answer: >-
    The trends in foreign acquisitions of U.S. companies have shown significant fluctuations in recent years, particularly in 2023. According to preliminary statistics from the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis, expenditures by foreign direct investors to acquire, establish, or expand U.S. businesses totaled $148.8 billion in 2023. This figure represents a decrease of $57.4 billion, or 28 percent, from the revised total of $206.2 billion in 2022. Furthermore, this amount is notably below the annual average of $265.6 billion recorded from 2014 to 2022.\n\nIn 2023, the breakdown of expenditures indicates that acquisitions accounted for a substantial portion, totaling $136.5 billion, while expenditures to establish new U.S. businesses were $7.4 billion, and those to expand existing foreign-owned businesses were $5.0 billion. This data suggests a strong focus on acquisitions over new business establishments or expansions.\n\nAdditionally, the 2023 M&A Trends Report highlights that dealmakers are adjusting their strategies in response to various pressures, including economic uncertainties, geopolitical tensions, climate change, and supply chain issues. There is a notable increase in interest among dealmakers for targets located in the Americas, with 78% of respondents expressing this interest, which is a significant rise compared to the previous year.\n\nOverall, while foreign acquisitions of U.S. companies remain a critical component of international investment, the trends indicate a cautious approach from foreign investors amid changing economic and geopolitical landscapes.
