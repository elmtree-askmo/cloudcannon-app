question: >-
    How should a notice of a meeting be issued according to the Companies Act 2013?
answer: >-
    According to the Companies Act 2013, the issuance of a notice for a meeting is governed primarily by Section 101. Here are the key requirements for issuing a notice of a meeting:\n\n1. **Notice Period**: A general meeting must be called by giving not less than 21 clear days' notice. This notice can be provided in writing or through electronic mode, as specified in the Act. For Section 8 companies, the notice period is reduced to 14 days.\n\n2. **Contents of the Notice**: The notice must include:\n   - The day, date, hour, and place of the meeting.\n   - A statement of the business to be transacted at the meeting.\n\n3. **Explanatory Statement**: If any business is to be transacted at the general meeting, an 'Explanatory Statement' must be annexed to the notice. This requirement is outlined in Section 102 of the Act. Failure to comply with this provision can result in penalties for the promoters, directors, managers, or other key managerial personnel who are in default.\n\n4. **Mode of Communication**: The notice can be sent through electronic mode, which is defined as any communication sent by the company through its authorized and secured computer program capable of producing confirmation and keeping a record of such communication.\n\n5. **Adjourned Meetings**: If a board meeting is adjourned due to a lack of quorum, the meeting will automatically stand adjourned to the same day and time in the following week, unless the company's articles provide otherwise.\n\n6. **Participation Options**: The notice should inform directors about the option to participate in the meeting through video conferencing or other audio-visual means.\n\nThese provisions ensure that all stakeholders are adequately informed about the meetings and the matters to be discussed, thereby promoting transparency and compliance within the company.
