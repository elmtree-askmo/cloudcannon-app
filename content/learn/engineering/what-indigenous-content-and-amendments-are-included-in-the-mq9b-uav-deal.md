question: >-
    What indigenous content and amendments are included in the MQ-9B UAV deal?
answer: >-
    The MQ-9B UAV deal between India and General Atomics includes several key aspects regarding indigenous content and amendments. Currently, the proposed indigenous content in the deal stands at approximately 8-9%. However, India is actively negotiating to increase this figure to around 15-20%. Discussions are ongoing, and General Atomics has shown a positive stance towards these negotiations, pending approval from the U.S. government.\n\nThe deal, valued at approximately $3.99 billion, involves the procurement of 31 MQ-9B High Altitude Long Endurance (HALE) Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs), which includes 15 Sea Guardians for the Indian Navy and 16 Sky Guardians. One significant amendment approved by the Defence Acquisition Council (DAC) pertains to the inclusion of higher indigenous content, which is part of India's broader strategy to enhance its domestic defense manufacturing capabilities.\n\nAdditionally, General Atomics is set to establish a Global Maintenance, Repair, and Overhaul (MRO) facility in India, further contributing to the local defense ecosystem. The DAC's approval of these amendments reflects India's commitment to developing its military-industrial complex and achieving greater self-reliance in defense production, in line with the Atmanirbhar Bharat initiative.\n\nIn summary, the MQ-9B UAV deal is not only a significant procurement effort but also a strategic move towards increasing indigenous manufacturing and capabilities within India's defense sector.
