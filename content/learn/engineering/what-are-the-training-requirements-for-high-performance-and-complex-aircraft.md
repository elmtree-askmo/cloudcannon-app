question: >-
    What are the training requirements for high performance and complex aircraft?
answer: >-
    The training requirements for operating high-performance and complex aircraft are outlined in the Federal Aviation Regulations (FAR), specifically under 14 CFR 61.31. Hereâ€™s a detailed overview of the requirements:\n\n### High-Performance Aircraft\nA high-performance airplane is defined as one that has an engine with more than 200 horsepower. According to FAR 61.31(f), no person may act as pilot in command (PIC) of a high-performance airplane unless they have:\n\n1. **Ground and Flight Training**: The pilot must receive and log ground and flight training from an authorized instructor in a high-performance airplane or in a full flight simulator that is representative of a high-performance airplane.\n2. **Proficiency Determination**: The instructor must determine that the pilot is proficient in the operation and systems of the high-performance airplane.\n\n### Complex Aircraft\nA complex airplane is defined under FAR 61.31(e) as an airplane that has:\n- Retractable landing gear\n- Flaps\n- A controllable pitch propeller\n\nTo operate a complex aircraft, a pilot must receive a one-time endorsement from an authorized instructor, confirming that they have received the necessary training to operate such aircraft.\n\n### Training Steps\n1. **Ground Training**: This includes learning about the systems of the aircraft and the operational aspects of higher power aircraft.\n2. **Flight Training**: The pilot must log flight training in a complex or high-performance aircraft to gain practical experience.\n\n### Additional Requirements for Commercial Pilots\nFor commercial pilot applicants seeking a single-engine airplane rating, FAR 61.129 requires them to log at least 10 hours of training in a complex airplane, turbine-powered airplane, or a technically advanced airplane (TAA). \n\n### Summary\nIn summary, pilots must undergo specific ground and flight training to operate high-performance and complex aircraft, and they must receive endorsements from authorized instructors to validate their training and proficiency. This ensures that pilots are adequately prepared to handle the complexities and performance characteristics of these aircraft safely.
