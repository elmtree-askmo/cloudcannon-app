question: >-
    What recent developments have occurred in radar technology in India?
answer: >-
    Recent developments in radar technology in India have been significant, particularly in the context of enhancing national security and defense capabilities. Here are some key advancements:\n\n1. **Indigenous GaN Technology**: The Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) has successfully developed Gallium Nitride (GaN)-based Monolithic Microwave Integrated Circuits (MMICs) at its Gallium Arsenide Enabling Technology Centre (GAETEC) in Hyderabad. This innovation is expected to play a crucial role in the development of next-generation radars and electronic warfare systems, providing India with a technological edge on the battlefield.\n\n2. **3D Central Acquisition Radar**: DRDO's 3D Central Acquisition Radar has been designed for extended-range search and track capabilities, exceeding 2000 kilometers. This radar is capable of detecting low Radar Cross Section (RCS) aircraft, making it a vital asset for India's defense infrastructure.\n\n3. **AESA Radar Upgrade**: The upgrade of the Su-30MKI fighter jets under Project Virupaksha involves the installation of an Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA) radar. This system replaces mechanically steered antennas with an electronically steered array, enhancing the aircraft's agility, versatility, and ability to track multiple targets simultaneously.\n\n4. **New Acquisitions**: The Indian Air Force is set to acquire new high-power radars and close-in weapon systems from Larsen and Toubro, with deals worth approximately Rs 13,000 crore approved by the Cabinet Committee on Security. These acquisitions aim to bolster surveillance capabilities along the borders with China and Pakistan.\n\n5. **Focus on Dual-Use Technologies**: Many radar applications developed in India are of dual-use nature, meaning they can serve both civilian and military purposes. This reflects a broader trend of integrating Information and Communication Technology (ICT) applications into radar systems.\n\n6. **Strategic Developments**: The advancements in radar technology are part of a larger strategy to close the technological gap with neighboring countries, particularly China, which has advantages in numbers and stealth technology.\n\n7. **Industry Involvement**: Companies like HFCL Limited are also focusing on radar technologies to meet the critical Information, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) needs of the Indian defense sector, indicating a growing involvement of the private sector in defense technology.\n\nThese developments highlight India's commitment to enhancing its radar capabilities and achieving greater self-reliance in defense technology, with over 90% of materials sourced domestically.
