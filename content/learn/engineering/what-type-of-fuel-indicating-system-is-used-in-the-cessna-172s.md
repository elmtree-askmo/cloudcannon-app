question: >-
    What type of fuel indicating system is used in the Cessna 172S?
answer: >-
    The Cessna 172S utilizes a fuel indicating system that provides pilots with accurate information about the fuel levels in the aircraft's tanks. Each of the two wing-mounted fuel tanks has a capacity of 28 gallons, with 26.5 gallons being usable. The fuel indicating system typically includes a fuel quantity gauge located on the instrument panel, which displays the amount of fuel in each tank.\n\nThe system is designed to ensure that pilots can monitor fuel levels effectively, which is crucial for flight planning and safety. The fuel gauges are calibrated to reflect the actual fuel levels, allowing for precise management of fuel consumption during flight. Additionally, the aircraft is certified to use 100LL (low lead) aviation gasoline, which is indicated by the blue color of the fuel.\n\nOverall, the fuel indicating system in the Cessna 172S is an essential component that aids in maintaining safe operational practices by providing real-time data on fuel availability.
