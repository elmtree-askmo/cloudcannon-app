question: >-
    What were the achievements of the Indian economy in the industrial sector between 1950-1990?
answer: >-
    Between 1950 and 1990, the Indian economy experienced significant achievements in the industrial sector, which played a crucial role in the country's overall economic development. Here are some key accomplishments:\n\n1. **Increase in Industrial Contribution to GDP**: The proportion of GDP contributed by the industrial sector rose from 11.8% in 1950-51 to 24.6% in 1990-91. This increase indicates a substantial growth in industrial activities and reflects the sector's growing importance in the economy.\n\n2. **Annual Growth Rate**: The industrial sector achieved an impressive annual growth rate of approximately 6% during this period. This consistent growth contributed to the overall economic advancement of the country.\n\n3. **Five-Year Plans**: The implementation of the Five-Year Plans was instrumental in promoting industrial development. These plans focused on rapid industrialization, with a particular emphasis on creating large state-owned enterprises (SOEs) in key industries such as iron, steel, and heavy machinery. The plans aimed to enhance the production capacity and diversify the industrial base.\n\n4. **Development of Basic and Capital Goods Industries**: The period saw the establishment and growth of basic and capital goods industries, which are essential for the development of a self-reliant economy. Industries producing essential goods laid the foundation for further industrialization and economic stability.\n\n5. **Shift from Agriculture to Industry**: While agriculture remained a significant part of the economy, the industrial sector's growth helped reduce the dependency on agriculture. This shift was crucial for economic diversification and modernization.\n\n6. **Employment Generation**: The industrial sector provided stable employment opportunities, contributing to the reduction of over-dependency on agriculture and promoting urbanization.\n\n7. **Policy Framework**: The 'Industrial Policy Resolution' of 1948 and subsequent policies reflected a commitment to industrialization, encouraging both public and private sector participation in economic development.\n\nOverall, the achievements of the Indian industrial sector between 1950 and 1990 laid the groundwork for future economic reforms and growth, setting the stage for India's transition towards a more liberalized economy in the 1990s.
