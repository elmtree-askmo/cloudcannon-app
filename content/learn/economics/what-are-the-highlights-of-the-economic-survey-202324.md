question: >-
    What are the highlights of the Economic Survey 2023-24?
answer: >-
    The Economic Survey 2023-24, tabled in Parliament by Union Minister for Finance and Corporate Affairs, Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman, presents a comprehensive analysis of India's economic performance and projections for the upcoming fiscal year. Here are the key highlights:\n\n1. **GDP Growth Projection**: The Economic Survey conservatively projects a real GDP growth of 6.5% to 7% for the financial year ending March 2025. This is a decrease from the estimated growth rate of 8.2% for the previous financial year.\n\n2. **Inflation Forecast**: The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) anticipates inflation to be around 4.5% in FY25. The Survey warns that any escalation in geopolitical conflicts could lead to supply disruptions, higher commodity prices, and renewed inflationary pressures.\n\n3. **Current Account Deficit (CAD)**: India's CAD is expected to improve, projected at 0.7% of GDP in FY24, down from 2.0% in FY23, indicating a more stable external position.\n\n4. **Economic Resilience**: The Survey highlights that the Indian economy has shown resilience, growing over 7% for the third consecutive year, supported by stable consumption and improving investment demand.\n\n5. **Policy Focus Areas**: Key areas of policy focus in the short to medium term include job and skill creation, maximizing the potential of the agriculture sector, and addressing challenges faced by Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises (MSMEs).\n\n6. **Sectoral Insights**: The Survey provides detailed analysis across various sectors, emphasizing the need for structural reforms to sustain growth and enhance productivity.\n\n7. **Format and Presentation**: The highlights are presented through charts, infographics, and tables, minimizing text to keep the document concise, typically around 30-35 pages.\n\nOverall, the Economic Survey 2023-24 serves as a critical tool for understanding the current economic landscape and guiding future policy decisions in India.
